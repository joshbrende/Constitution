[STEP] What "predictive" means in municipal context

## Predictive Modeling Fundamentals

**Predictive** means using **past** data and **assumptions** to **estimate** or **forecast** **future** values (demand, revenue, population, infrastructure need). It supports **IDP**, **MTREF**, **budget**, and **SDBIP** by making **planning** and **investment** **evidence-based** and **forward-looking**. **SALGA Outcome 2** (Customised Innovative Solutions—predictive capabilities) and **Outcome 5** (better planning) both benefit.

### Predictive vs other types of analysis

| Type | What it does | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|
| **Descriptive** | What **happened** (past). | "Revenue was R Xm last year; faults were Y." |
| **Diagnostic** | **Why** it happened. | "Revenue down because collection fell and tariffs delayed." |
| **Predictive** | What **may** happen (future). | "If growth 1.5% and collection 75%, revenue in 3 years ~ R Zm." |
| **Prescriptive** | What we **should do**. | "To meet demand, add Z connections and upgrade pump by 2027." |

**Predictive** does **not** guarantee the future—it **reduces uncertainty** by making **assumptions** explicit and **sensitivity** visible.

[STEP] Types of forecasts and models

## Types of Forecasts and Models

| Approach | What it uses | When it fits |
|----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Trend / time series** | Historical **values over time**; project forward (growth rate, moving average, seasonal). | When **driver** is hard to measure or as **sanity check**. |
| **Driver-based** | **Underlying drivers** (population, connections, tariff, consumption, collection). | When you have or can **estimate** drivers; **IDP** and **budget** often use this. |
| **Scenario** | **Alternatives** (base, upside, downside) by **changing** key assumptions. | When **uncertainty** is high; **council** and **Treasury** often want scenarios. |
| **Expert / judgment** | **Expert** or **stakeholder** input when **data** is scarce. | Rural, new areas, or **one-off** (e.g. new tariff structure). |

**Combination:** Many municipal forecasts **combine** (e.g. **driver-based** for revenue, **scenario** for upside/downside). **AI** can help **suggest** **driver** structures and **scenario** assumptions—you **build** the model in **your** Excel or system.

[STEP] Limitations and assumptions

## Limitations and Assumptions

- **Assumptions drive results.** If **population growth** or **collection rate** is wrong, the **forecast** is wrong. **State** assumptions **clearly**; do **sensitivity** (change one, see impact).
- **Data quality.** **Garbage in, garbage out.** Use **best available** data; note **gaps** and **sources**.
- **Black swans.** **Predictive** models often **miss** rare, large events (disaster, pandemic, sudden policy change). **Scenarios** and **risk** (Module 8) help you **prepare** for **alternatives**.
- **Human oversight.** **AI** can suggest **structures** and **assumptions**; **you** and **Planning** / **CFO** **validate** and **approve** before **council** or **Treasury**.
