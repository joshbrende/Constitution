## AI Application: Population Growth Prediction

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **aggregated**, **generic** or **public**-type figures (e.g. "population 250 000," "growth 1.5%"). **Never** put **person-specific** or **confidential** data into public AI. AI **cannot** run **live** on your **data** or **replace** **Stats SA**. It can: (1) **suggest** a **structure** for a **population-driven** **demand** model (population → households → connections → consumption); (2) **propose** **sources** (Stats SA, community survey, provincial) and **sanity-check** **growth** **ranges** for South African municipalities; (3) **draft** a short **assumption** or **narrative** from **aggregated** numbers you provide. You **build** the **model** in **your** Excel; **validate** with **Planning**.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest a population-to-demand structure

### Task A: Suggest a Population-to-Demand Structure

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want a **population-to-demand** **structure** for **planning**: how to go from **population** (or **households**) to **demand** for **water**, **sanitation**, and **waste**. We will NOT put our data into AI—we only want the **concept**. For each service, give: (1) the **chain** (e.g. Population → Households → Water connections → Consumption); (2) **typical** **ratios** or **ranges** for SA (e.g. persons per household, kl per connection per year) with a **source** or "typical"; (3) one **assumption** we must **state** and **sensitivity**-test. Use a table. We will build the model in Excel with our own numbers and sources.

2. **Use the output:** **Check** **ratios** against **your** **data** (billing, **community** **survey**). Use the **structure** in your **Demand** worksheet (Practical). **AI** suggests; **you** **own** the **model** and **inputs**.

[STEP] Task B — Propose population growth sources and sanity-check

### Task B: Propose Population Growth Sources and Sanity-Check

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We need **population** **projections** for **IDP** and **demand** **forecasting**. List: (1) **5–6 sources** we can use (e.g. Stats SA, Census, community survey, provincial, our own); (2) **typical** **annual** **growth** **ranges** for South African municipalities (rural, secondary city, metro) in broad terms (e.g. 0.5–1%, 1–2%); (3) **3–4** **sanity-check** **questions** we should ask (e.g. "Is our growth above regional?", "Does household size match Stats SA?"). We will use our own data—we are NOT putting it into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Validate** with **Planning** and **Stats** or **research** if you have. Use **sources** and **ranges** to **justify** **assumptions** in your **Demand** and **Scenario** sheets. **AI** suggests; **you** **choose** **sources** and **values**.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a population assumption paragraph

### Task C: Draft a Population Assumption Paragraph

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** numbers only. Example:

   > Our population assumption for the IDP: base 250 000; growth 1.5% p.a. from Stats SA mid-year and provincial trend. Household size 3.5. We sensitivity-tested 1% and 2%: demand and revenue vary by about ±X%.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **one-paragraph** **population** **assumption** for our **IDP** or **MTREF** **document**. Include: source, growth, sensitivity. Formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **Planning**. Use in **IDP** or **budget** **narrative**. **AI** drafts; **you** **own** the **content**.
