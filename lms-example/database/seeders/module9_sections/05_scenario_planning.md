[STEP] What is scenario planning?

## Scenario Planning

**Scenario planning** explores **different** **future** outcomes by **varying** **key** **assumptions** (e.g. population growth, tariff, grant, bulk price, climate). It helps: (1) **council** and **Treasury** see **alternatives**; (2) **stress-test** the **budget** and **IDP**; (3) **prepare** for **upside** and **downside**. **SALGA Outcome 2** (predictive capabilities) and **Outcome 5** (better planning) support **scenario**-based **decision-making**.

### Typical scenarios

| Scenario | Typical assumptions | Use |
|----------|---------------------|-----|
| **Base** | **Most likely**: moderate growth, current policy, mid-range tariff and grant. | **Primary** plan; **MTREF** and **IDP** **base case**. |
| **Upside** | **Better**: higher growth, higher collection, grant increase, cost savings. | **Stretch** targets; **optional** projects if **revenue** allows. |
| **Downside** | **Worse**: lower growth, lower collection, grant cut, bulk or cost increase. | **Contingency**; what to **cut** or **delay**; **reserves**. |

You can **add** **sector** scenarios (e.g. "water bulk +15%") or **combined** (e.g. "downside + drought"). **AI** can help **suggest** **assumption** tables and **narrative** for each scenario—you **plug in** **your** numbers and **validate** with **Planning** and **CFO**.

[STEP] How to use scenarios in IDP and MTREF

## How to Use Scenarios in IDP and MTREF

- **IDP:** **Long-term** (5–10 years). **Scenarios** show **range** of **demand**, **revenue**, and **investment** under **different** growth and **policy** paths. **Council** can **adopt** a **base** and **note** **upside**/ **downside** for **flexibility**.
- **MTREF / budget:** **3-year** (or 1-year) **revenue** and **expenditure**. **Treasury** and **CoGTA** often ask for **sensitivity** or **scenario** (e.g. "If equitable share is X% lower, what gives?"). Your **scenario** template (Practical) can **feed** that.
- **Reporting:** In **Section 72** or **in-year** reports, if **reality** moves toward **downside**, **revise** **estimates** and **alert** **council**. **Scenarios** **prepare** you for that **conversation**.

[STEP] Sensitivity — one variable at a time

## Sensitivity: One Variable at a Time

- **Sensitivity:** Change **one** assumption (e.g. collection rate −5%) and see **impact** on **revenue** or **surplus**. Shows **which** assumptions **matter most**.
- **Table:** Rows = assumption (e.g. collection 70%, 75%, 80%); column = **revenue** or **surplus**. **AI** can help **draft** the **structure** and **example** **narrative**—you **populate** with **your** numbers.
