## Practical: Scenario Planning Template

**Tools:** **Google Sheets** or **Microsoft Excel**.

This template supports **scenario** planning (Module 9 theory) for **IDP**, **MTREF**, or **council** **stress-testing**. It uses **key** **assumptions** from your **Demand** or **budget** model and **varies** them for **base**, **upside**, and **downside**. **AI** can help **suggest** **assumption** **ranges** and **narrative**—you **populate** with **your** numbers; **validate** with **Planning** and **CFO**.

### Step 1: Create the workbook and sheets

1. Open **Google Sheets** or **Excel** (you can add to your **Demand** workbook or create new).
2. Create sheets: **Scenario assumptions**, **Scenario results** (or **Revenue / surplus**), and **Cover** (or reuse).
3. **Cover:** Title "Scenario Planning — [Municipality]", "For: [IDP 2025–30 / MTREF 2025–28]", "Data as at [date]", "Owner: [Planning / CFO]".

### Step 2: Scenario assumptions sheet — structure

In the **Scenario assumptions** sheet:

- **Row 1:** Headers: `Assumption` | `Base` | `Upside` | `Downside` | `Impact (short)`.
- **Rows 2–10:** One row per **key** **assumption**, e.g.:
  - *Population growth (% p.a.)*
  - *Collection rate (%)*
  - *Tariff increase (%)*
  - *Equitable share (real growth %)*
  - *Bulk electricity price (% increase)*
  - *Other* (add 2–4 that **materially** affect **revenue** or **surplus**)

- **Base:** Most likely. **Upside:** Better (e.g. higher growth, higher collection). **Downside:** Worse (e.g. lower growth, grant cut). **Impact (short):** One line (e.g. "Revenue ±R Xm").

### Step 3: Scenario results sheet — structure

In the **Scenario results** sheet:

- **Rows 1–2:** **Scenario** (Base, Upside, Downside) across columns; or **one** **table** with **rows** = **metric**, **columns** = **Scenario**.
- **Rows 3–12:** **Metrics** that **change** with **scenarios**, e.g.:
  - *Total revenue (Y1, Y2, Y3)*
  - *Total expenditure (Y1, Y2, Y3)*
  - *Surplus / deficit (Y1, Y2, Y3)*
  - *Cumulative surplus (or key capex headroom)*

- **Formulas:** **Link** to **Scenario assumptions** (e.g. if **Base** column uses **Base** assumptions, **Upside** uses **Upside**). Or **run** **three** **versions** of your **Demand** or **budget** **model** with **different** **inputs** and **paste** **results** here.

### Step 4: One-page scenario summary (narrative)

- **Bottom** of **Scenario results** or **new** **Summary** sheet: **3 short paragraphs** (or **bullets**)—one per **scenario**—with: (1) **key** **assumptions**; (2) **revenue** and **surplus** **outcome**; (3) **one** **implication** (e.g. "Downside: defer project X; use reserves for Y"). **AI** can help **draft** this from **aggregated** numbers you provide—you **validate** and **approve**.

### Step 5: Sensitivity — one variable (optional)

- **Extra** sheet or **table:** **Rows** = **one** assumption (e.g. collection rate 70%, 72%, 75%, 78%); **Column** = **revenue** or **surplus** in **Y3**. Shows **how** **sensitive** the result is to **that** assumption. **AI** can suggest **structure**—you **populate**.

### Use this

- **In your municipality:** Use **your** **Demand** and **budget** **model**. **Planning** and **CFO** **approve** **assumptions** and **ranges**. Use in **IDP** **submission**, **MTREF** **narrative**, or **council** **workshop**. **AI** can **draft** **narrative** from **aggregated** **results**—**never** **confidential** **Treasury** or **council** **detail** in public AI.
