[STEP] What we mean by resource allocation optimization

## Resource Allocation Optimization

**Resource allocation** is how you **assign** **limited** **budget**, **staff**, and **assets** across **competing** needs (water, roads, waste, new connections, maintenance, etc.). **Optimization** means **choosing** in a **structured** way so you **maximise** outcomes (e.g. service delivery, IDP objectives) **within** constraints (budget, capacity, time). **SALGA Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return) and **Outcome 2** (predictive capabilities) support **evidence-based** allocation.

### Demand vs capacity

| Concept | What it means | Use in planning |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Demand** | **Future** need (connections, volume, traffic, tonnage) from your **demand** forecast. | **How much** we need to **provide** or **invest**. |
| **Capacity** | **Current** and **planned** ability (treatment, network, fleet, staff). | **What we have**; **gap** = demand − capacity. |
| **Gap** | **Shortfall** when demand &gt; capacity. | **Prioritised** for **capex**, **O&M**, or **partnerships**. |

When **resources** are **limited**, you **prioritise**: by **IDP** priority, **risk**, **equity** (e.g. underserviced areas), **revenue** or **cost recovery**, or **regulatory** (e.g. water quality). **AI** can help **suggest** **prioritisation** **criteria** and **weights**—you **decide** and **council** **approves**.

[STEP] Prioritisation frameworks

## Prioritisation Frameworks

- **IDP and spatial:** Allocate to **IDP** priorities and **spatial** plans (e.g. catalytic projects, densification, rural). **Demand** and **backlog** by **ward** or **area** help.
- **Cost–benefit or cost–effectiveness:** **Benefit** (e.g. households served, jobs) vs **cost** (rand, time). **Rank** projects; fund **top** until **budget** is used.
- **Risk-based:** **High-risk** (e.g. treatment at capacity, safety) get **priority**.
- **Equity:** **Underserviced** or **indigent** areas get a **share** of **allocation**. **SALGA** and **national** policy often stress **equity**.
- **Multi-criteria:** **Score** each **option** on **several** criteria (IDP fit, cost, risk, equity); **weight** and **sum**. **AI** can help **design** the **score** structure—you **populate** and **approve**.

[STEP] Link to budget and MTREF

## Link to Budget and MTREF

- **MTREF** and **budget** **allocate** **revenue** and **grant** to **votes**, **programmes**, and **projects**. Your **demand** forecast and **prioritisation** **justify** **allocation** to **council** and **Treasury**.
- **Trade-offs:** More **water** capex may mean **less** **roads**. **Scenarios** (next section) help **council** see **alternatives**. **AI** can help **draft** a **one-page** **allocation** or **trade-off** **brief** from **aggregated** numbers you provide—you **validate** and **approve**.
