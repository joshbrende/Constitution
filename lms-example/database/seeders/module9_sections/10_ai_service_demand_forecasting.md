## AI Application: Service Demand Forecasting

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **aggregated** figures (e.g. "50 000 connections," "growth 2%"). **Never** put **confidential** or **person-specific** data into public AI. AI can: (1) **suggest** **driver** **structures** and **formulas** for **water**, **electricity**, **waste**, **roads** demand; (2) **propose** **typical** **consumption** or **usage** **ranges** for SA municipalities (with "typical" or "check your data" caveats); (3) **draft** a short **demand** **summary** or **assumption** table from **aggregated** numbers you provide. You **build** the **forecast** in **your** worksheet; **validate** with **Planning** and **technical** directors.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest driver structures for water, electricity, waste

### Task A: Suggest Driver Structures for Water, Electricity, Waste

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want **demand** **forecast** **structures** for: (1) **Water** (connections, consumption, revenue); (2) **Electricity** (connections, sales, revenue); (3) **Waste** (households, tonnage, revenue). For each, give: the **driver** **chain** (e.g. Households → Connections → Consumption × Tariff); **one** **formula** in words; and **one** **key** **assumption** to **sensitivity**-test. Use a table. We will build in Excel with our own data—we are NOT putting it into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Adapt** to your **billing** and **technical** **structure**. Use it in your **Demand** worksheet. **AI** suggests **logic**; **you** **implement** and **own** the **model**.

[STEP] Task B — Propose typical consumption ranges (sanity-check)

### Task B: Propose Typical Consumption Ranges (Sanity-Check)

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > For South African municipalities, what are **typical** **ranges** (broad) for: (1) **Water** consumption in kl per connection per year (residential, and a note on commercial); (2) **Electricity** in kWh per connection per year (residential); (3) **Waste** in kg per household per month or tonnage per capita per year? We need **sanity-checks** for our demand model—we will use our own billing data. Give ranges and one **caveat** each (e.g. "Varies by region, meter type").

2. **Use the output:** **Compare** your **billing** or **technical** **data** to these **ranges**. If **outside** **range**, **investigate** (data quality, **non-revenue** water, **unbilled**). **AI** gives **context**; **you** **validate** with **your** **data**.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a demand forecast summary

### Task C: Draft a Demand Forecast Summary

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** numbers only. Example:

   > Our 5-year demand: Water connections 55 000 to 60 500; consumption +2% p.a. Electricity connections 48 000 to 52 000; sales +2.5%. Waste: tonnage +1.5% p.a. Main drivers: population 1.5%, new connections from IDP. Key risk: bulk price above 5% p.a.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **one-paragraph** **demand** **forecast** **summary** for our **IDP** or **council**. Include: services, growth, drivers, one risk. Formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **Planning**. Use in **IDP** or **MTREF** **narrative**. **AI** drafts; **you** **own** the **content**.
