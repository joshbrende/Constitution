## AI Application: Climate Change Impact Modeling

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **generic**, **structural** descriptions. **Never** put **confidential** or **sensitive** **municipal** **data** into public AI. AI can: (1) **suggest** a **framework** of **climate** **impact** **areas** for municipalities (water, infrastructure, energy, health, spatial); (2) **propose** **what** to **monitor** and **what** **scenarios** to **consider** (e.g. **drier**, **wetter**, **hotter**, **extreme** **events**); (3) **draft** a short **climate** **assumption** or **"what we are doing"** paragraph from **aggregated** points you provide. You **customise** and **validate** with **Planning** and **technical**; **align** with **national** and **provincial** **climate** **strategies** where applicable.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest a climate impact framework for municipalities

### Task A: Suggest a Climate Impact Framework for Municipalities

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want a **climate** **change** **impact** **framework** for **planning**: what **impact** **areas** to **consider** and what to **monitor** or **model**. We will NOT put our data into AI. Suggest: (1) **5–7** **impact** **areas** (e.g. water availability, flooding, drought, heat, infrastructure, energy demand, health, agriculture if relevant); (2) for each: **one** **example** **impact** (e.g. "Drought: lower dam levels, supply risk"); (3) **one** **type** of **data** or **indicator** we could **monitor** (e.g. "Dam levels; demand vs supply"). Use a table. We will customise with our Planning and technical teams.

2. **Use the output:** **Discuss** with **Planning**, **water**, **roads**, and **environment**. Use it to **prioritise** **impact** **areas** for your **IDP** or **sector** **plans**. **AI** suggests **structure**; **you** **own** the **priorities** and **actions**.

[STEP] Task B — Propose climate scenarios for planning

### Task B: Propose Climate Scenarios for Planning

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > For South African municipal **planning**, what **climate** **scenarios** should we **consider** (e.g. **drier**, **wetter**, **hotter**, **more** **extreme** **events**)? For each: (1) **name**; (2) **one** **sentence** on what it means for **water**, **infrastructure**, or **services**; (3) **one** **suggested** **planning** **response** (e.g. "Drier: demand management; alternative sources; storage"). Use a table. We will align with national/provincial climate strategies and our own context.

2. **Use the output:** **Align** with **national** (e.g. **National** **Climate** **Change** **Adaptation** **Strategy**) and **provincial** **guidance**. Use it in **IDP** **scenario** or **resilience** **planning**. **AI** suggests; **you** **adapt** and **approve**.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a climate assumption or "what we are doing" paragraph

### Task C: Draft a Climate Assumption or "What We Are Doing" Paragraph

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** points only. Example:

   > For our IDP we considered: drought risk (we have demand management and backup boreholes); flooding in low-lying areas (we track and have drainage plans); bulk water and electricity price risk. We will do a fuller climate risk assessment in 2025. We align with provincial climate strategy.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **one-paragraph** **climate** **assumption** or **"what we are doing on climate"** for our **IDP** or **council** **report**. Formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **Planning** and **technical**. Use in **IDP** or **annual** **report**. **AI** drafts; **you** **own** the **content** and **commitments**.

[STEP] Consolidation — when to use which AI task

## When to Use Which AI Task

| Module 9 area | AI task | What you get | What you must do |
|---------------|---------|--------------|------------------|
| **Population** | Structure; sources; assumption paragraph | Design and narrative | Build model in Excel; validate with Planning; use aggregated only. |
| **Service demand** | Driver structures; consumption ranges; summary | Logic and sanity-checks | Implement in Demand worksheet; validate with Planning/technical. |
| **Infrastructure / budget** | Asset life; lifecycle structure; budget assumption table; summary | Structures and narrative | Implement with your asset register and budget; CFO and technical approve. |
| **Climate** | Impact framework; scenarios; "what we are doing" paragraph | Framework and narrative | Customise with Planning/technical; align with national/provincial. |

**Remember:** AI **supports** **structure**, **assumptions**, and **narrative**—it does **not** replace **Stats SA**, **your** **billing** **data**, or **council** **decisions**. Use **aggregated** data only; **validate** and **approve** with **Planning** and **CFO** before use in **IDP**, **MTREF**, or **council**.
