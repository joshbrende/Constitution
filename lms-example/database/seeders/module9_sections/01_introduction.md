# Module 9: Predictive Analytics for Municipal Planning

Time Slot: 09:00 - 10:30 (Day 3)

## Purpose of This Session

This session applies **predictive** and **forecasting** thinking to **municipal planning**: demand for services, resource allocation, scenario planning, and long-term trends. **SALGA Outcome 2** (Customised Innovative Solutions—predictive capabilities) and **Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return—better planning) both benefit from **evidence-based** and **forward-looking** planning. You will learn **theory** on predictive modeling basics, demand forecasting, resource allocation, scenario planning, and long-term trends; **practical** tasks to build a **demand forecast** worksheet and a **scenario planning** template; and **AI application** tasks for **population** and **service demand** forecasting, **infrastructure lifecycle** and **budget** forecasting, and **climate change impact** concepts—using **aggregated, non-confidential** data only.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** predictive modeling fundamentals: what "predictive" means, types of forecasts, and limitations.
2. **Describe** demand forecasting: drivers (population, connections, consumption, growth); how it supports **IDP**, **budget**, and **SDBIP**.
3. **Outline** resource allocation optimization: linking **demand** and **capacity** to **budget** and **plans**; prioritisation when resources are limited.
4. **Explain** scenario planning: base, upside, downside; how to use scenarios in **IDP**, **MTREF**, and **council** decisions.
5. **Describe** long-term trend analysis: demographic, economic, and service trends; how they inform **planning** and **investment**.
6. **Build** a demand forecast worksheet and a scenario planning template in Excel or Google Sheets.
7. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to support **population** and **service demand** forecast **structures**, **infrastructure lifecycle** and **budget** forecast **concepts**, and **climate impact** **frameworks**—with **aggregated** data only; **validate** with **planning** and **CFO**.

## How to Use This Module

1. **Connect to Modules 3, 6, and 8.** Your **IDP**, **SDBIP**, **Logic Model** (Module 3), **financial** and **revenue** forecasting (Module 6), and **risk** (Module 8) all link to **planning** and **scenarios**. Use the same principles: **assumptions** stated, **sources** clear, **owner** for each forecast or scenario.
2. **Work through each step.** Theory sets the concepts; **Practical** and **AI Application** give you structures you can use in your municipality.
3. **Use aggregated data only.** In **AI Application** tasks, use **aggregated** figures (e.g. "population growth 1.5%," "water connections 50k"). **Never** put **confidential** or **person-specific** data into public AI. **AI** suggests **structures**, **drivers**, and **narrative**; you **build** the forecast in **your** Excel or planning system and **own** the assumptions.
4. **Validate with planning and CFO.** **IDP** coordinators, **Planning**, and **CFO** should **validate** forecast **assumptions** and **scenario** inputs before use in **council** or **Treasury**.
