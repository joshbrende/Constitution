## AI Application: Infrastructure Lifecycle and Budget Forecasting

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **aggregated**, **generic** information (e.g. "pump life 20 years," "R Xm replacement"). **Never** put **confidential** **asset** **or** **budget** **detail** into public AI. AI can: (1) **suggest** **typical** **asset** **life** **ranges** (pipes, pumps, substations, roads) for **lifecycle** **planning**; (2) **propose** a **structure** for **replacement** and **refurbishment** **forecasting** (age, condition, cost); (3) **draft** a **budget** **forecast** **assumption** table or **narrative** from **aggregated** figures you provide. You **build** the **model** in **your** system; **validate** with **technical** and **CFO**.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest typical asset life and lifecycle structure

### Task A: Suggest Typical Asset Life and Lifecycle Structure

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want to **forecast** **infrastructure** **replacement** and **refurbishment** for **lifecycle** **planning**. We will NOT put our asset data into AI. Suggest: (1) **typical** **useful** **life** (years) for: water pipes (distribution), pumps, reservoirs; sewer; electricity distribution; roads (surface); (2) a **simple** **structure** for **forecasting** (e.g. "For each asset type: list age; if age &gt; 80% of life, schedule replacement or refurb in next 5–10 years; estimate cost"). Use a table for (1); short bullets for (2). We will implement with our asset register and costs.

2. **Use the output:** **Check** against your **asset** **policy** and **technical** **standards**. Use the **structure** to **design** a **lifecycle** or **replacement** **forecast** in **Excel** or **asset** **system**. **AI** suggests; **you** **own** the **model** and **data**.

[STEP] Task B — Propose a budget forecast assumption table

### Task B: Propose a Budget Forecast Assumption Table

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We need a **budget** **forecast** **assumption** table for our **MTREF** or **IDP**: what **key** **assumptions** to **state** for **revenue** and **expenditure** over 3–5 years. Include: (1) **revenue** (e.g. tariff increase %, collection rate, equitable share growth, other grants); (2) **expenditure** (e.g. employee cost %, bulk electricity %, bulk water %, other). For each, give: assumption, typical **range** or "municipality-specific," and one **sensitivity** (e.g. "If bulk +10%, expenditure +R Xm"). Use a table. We will plug in our own numbers.

2. **Use the output:** **Align** with your **budget** and **Treasury** **format**. Use it in your **Scenario** or **MTREF** **narrative**. **AI** suggests **structure**; **you** **populate** and **CFO** **approves**.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a budget forecast summary

### Task C: Draft a Budget Forecast Summary

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** numbers only. Example:

   > Our MTREF forecast: Revenue growth 6% p.a. (tariff 6%, collection 75%, equitable share 2% real). Expenditure 5.5% (employee 5.5%, bulk 7%). Surplus R 5m, R 4m, R 3m over 3 years. Key risks: bulk above 7%; collection below 75%. We have a downside scenario.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **one-paragraph** **budget** **forecast** **summary** for the **Mayoral Committee** or **council**. Include: revenue, expenditure, surplus, one risk. Formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **CFO**. Use in **MTREF** or **Section 72** **narrative**. **AI** drafts; **you** **own** the **content**.
