[STEP] Why demand forecasting matters

## Demand Forecasting

**Demand forecasting** estimates **future** need for **services** (water, sanitation, electricity, roads, waste) and **revenue** (connections, consumption, tariffs). It supports: (1) **IDP** and **SDBIP** (how many connections, what capacity); (2) **budget** and **MTREF** (revenue, capex); (3) **infrastructure** planning (when to upgrade, where to expand). **SALGA Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return—quality services) and **Outcome 2** (predictive capabilities) both benefit.

### Key drivers by service

| Service | Typical drivers | What you forecast |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Water** | Population; households; connections; consumption per connection; **non-revenue** water. | Connections; **volume**; **revenue**; **bulk** need. |
| **Sanitation** | Same as water (households, connections); **sludge** if applicable. | Connections; **volume**; **revenue**; **treatment** capacity. |
| **Electricity** | Connections; **consumption** (residential, commercial, industrial); **load** growth. | Connections; **sales**; **revenue**; **bulk** or **peak** need. |
| **Roads** | **Traffic** (vehicles, growth); **condition**; **network** length. | **Traffic**; **maintenance** and **upgrade** need; **budget**. |
| **Waste** | Households; **tonnage** per capita; **recycling**; **illegal** dumping. | **Tonnage**; **revenue**; **landfill** life; **collection** routes. |

[STEP] Population as a master driver

## Population as a Master Driver

- **Population** often drives **households**, **connections**, and **consumption**. **Sources:** **Stats SA** (Census, mid-year estimates, **community survey**); **provincial** or **municipal** projections; **ward** or **spatial** breakdowns if available.
- **Growth rate:** Historical or **assumed** (e.g. 1.5% per year). **Sensitivity:** Try 1%, 1.5%, 2% and see impact on **demand** and **revenue**.
- **AI** can help **suggest** **population**-to-**demand** **ratios** or **formulas** from **generic** municipal examples—you **plug in** **your** numbers and **sources**.

[STEP] Link to IDP, budget, and SDBIP

## Link to IDP, Budget, and SDBIP

- **IDP:** **Long-term** (5–10 years) **demand** and **investment**; **spatial** priorities. Your **demand** forecast feeds **IDP** **objectives** (e.g. "X new water connections by 2030").
- **Budget / MTREF:** **Revenue** (tariff × volume × collection); **capex** (new connections, upgrades). **Demand** drives **volume** and **capacity** need.
- **SDBIP:** **Annual** **outputs** (e.g. "Y new connections this year") should align with **multi-year** **demand** and **IDP**. Your **demand** worksheet (Practical) can **feed** **SDBIP** **targets**.
