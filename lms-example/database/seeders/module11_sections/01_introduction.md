# Module 11: AI Implementation Roadmap

Time Slot: 13:30 - 15:00 (Day 3)

## Purpose of This Session

This session ties together the **AI applications** from **Modules 2–10** into a **practical** **roadmap** for **implementing** AI in your municipality. **SALGA Outcome 1** (Agile Force—implementation capability) and **Outcome 2** (Customised Innovative Solutions—practical roadmap) require a **clear path** to AI adoption with **realistic** expectations and **sustainable** implementation. You will learn **theory** on **change management** for AI adoption, **phased** implementation, **resource** requirements (budget, skills, technology), **vendor** selection and **partnerships**, and **success** metrics and **evaluation**; **practical** tasks to build an **implementation** **roadmap** template and an **AI** **initiative** **prioritisation** matrix; and **AI application** tasks for **maturity** **assessment**, **planning** **templates**, **ROI** **calculator** concepts, **risk** **assessment** for AI implementation, and **success** **factor** analysis.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** change management for AI adoption: **resistance**, **champions**, **training**, and **communication**.
2. **Describe** a phased implementation approach: **quick wins** vs **foundational** vs **advanced**; **sequencing** and **dependencies**.
3. **Outline** resource requirements: **budget** (licences, **vendor**, **internal**); **skills** (build, **buy**, **partner**); **technology** (data, **systems**, **security**).
4. **Explain** vendor selection and partnerships: **criteria**, **evaluation**, **contracts**, and **governance**.
5. **Describe** success metrics and evaluation: **what** to **measure**, **when**, and **how** to **learn** and **adapt**.
6. **Build** an implementation roadmap template and an AI initiative prioritisation matrix in Excel or Google Sheets.
7. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to support **maturity** **assessment** **questions**, **planning** **templates**, **ROI** **and** **risk** **structures**, and **success** **factor** **checklists**—with **aggregated** or **generic** inputs only; **validate** with **MM**, **CFO**, or **IT** **lead**.

## How to Use This Module

1. **Connect to Modules 2–10.** The **AI** **use** **cases** (drafting, **analysis**, **dashboards**, **financial**, **service**, **compliance**, **planning**, **engagement**) from earlier modules become **candidates** for your **roadmap** and **prioritisation**. Use **Module 12** (Action Planning) to **finalise** **individual** or **team** **plans**.
2. **Work through each step.** Theory sets the concepts; **Practical** and **AI Application** give you **templates** and **structures** you can **customise** for your municipality.
3. **Use generic or aggregated inputs for AI.** In **AI** tasks, use **generic** **descriptions** (e.g. "metro," "150 staff," "no AI yet") or **aggregated** **stats**. **Never** put **confidential** **budget**, **contract**, or **person-specific** data into public AI. **Validate** **outputs** with **MM**, **CFO**, or **IT**.
4. **Be realistic.** AI adoption is **incremental**. **Start** with **low-cost**, **high-trust** use cases (e.g. **prompts** for **drafting**, **checklists**); **add** **integrated** systems and **vendor** solutions when **readiness** and **budget** allow.
