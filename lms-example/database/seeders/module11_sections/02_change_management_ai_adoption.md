[STEP] Why change management matters for AI

## Change Management for AI Adoption

**AI adoption** is **organisational** **change**: new **tools**, **workflows**, **skills**, and sometimes **fear** of **replacement** or **loss** of **control**. **Change management** helps you: (1) **anticipate** **resistance** and **address** it; (2) **build** **champions** and **early** **adopters**; (3) **train** and **support** staff; (4) **communicate** **why**, **what**, and **how** in a way that **builds** **trust**. **SALGA Outcome 1** (Agile Force—implementation capability) depends on **people** **adopting** and **using** AI—not just **technology** **being** **installed**.

### Common sources of resistance

| Source | What it looks like | Possible response |
|--------|--------------------|-------------------|
| **Fear of replacement** | "AI will take our jobs." | **Reframe**: AI **augments**; **human** **oversight** and **judgment** remain. **Examples** of **higher-value** work (e.g. **analysis**, **strategy**) when **routine** tasks are **assisted**. |
| **Lack of trust** | "Can we trust AI output?" | **Pilot** with **validated** use cases; **always** **human** **sign-off**; **transparency** on **limits** and **errors**. **Ethics** and **POPI** **training** (Module 2). |
| **Skill anxiety** | "I'm not technical." | **Emphasise** **prompt-based** and **spreadsheet** use (Modules 2–10); **no** **coding** **required** for **many** **tasks**. **Peer** **support** and **champions**. |
| **Workload** | "Another thing to learn on top of everything." | **Start** with **tasks** that **reduce** **load** (e.g. **drafting**, **checklists**); **show** **time** **saved**. **Phased** **rollout** so **not** **everyone** **at** **once**. |
| **Silo / turf** | "That's IT's job" or "We've always done it this way." | **Cross-functional** **ownership** (e.g. **CFO** for **financial** AI, **Customer Care** for **chatbot**); **leadership** **champions** from **each** **directorate**. |

[STEP] Champions, training, and communication

## Champions, Training, and Communication

- **Champions:** **Identify** **early** **adopters** in **each** **directorate** or **key** **role** (e.g. **PMU**, **Revenue**, **Customer Care**). **Give** them **time** to **experiment**, **share** **wins**, and **support** **colleagues**. **Champions** **model** **use** and **answer** "how do I…?" **questions**.
- **Training:** **Structured** **training** (this **course**) plus **on-the-job** **practice**. **Refreshers** and **advanced** **sessions** (e.g. **prompt** **library**, **new** **use** **cases**). **Include** **ethics**, **POPI**, and **"when** **not** **to** **use** **AI"** (confidential data, **final** **decisions** without **human** **review**).
- **Communication:** **Consistent** **messages** from **MM** and **directors**: **why** AI (e.g. "Better **service**, **efficiency**, **SALGA** **alignment**"); **what** we are **doing** (e.g. "Starting with **drafting** and **checklists**"); **how** staff can **get** **help**. **Celebrate** **quick** **wins**; **address** **concerns** **openly**. **AI** can help **draft** **communication** **plans** or **FAQs**—you **customise** and **approve**.
