## AI Application: Chatbots for Citizen Queries

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** **Chatbots** that **interact** with **citizens** **directly** (on your **website** or **app**) must be **designed**, **hosted**, and **governed** by **your** **municipality** or **vendor**—**not** by **public** **ChatGPT** or **Claude**. **POPI** and **municipal** **policy** require **control** over **data**, **accuracy**, and **escalation**. In this **session**, we use **AI** to: (1) **suggest** **FAQ** **topics** and **answer** **structures** for a **municipal** **chatbot**; (2) **design** a **conversation** **flow** (welcome, **menu**, **escalate** to **human**); (3) **draft** **sample** **Q&A** **pairs** you can **load** into **your** **chatbot** **platform**. **You** **implement** in **your** **system**; **never** **send** **live** **citizen** **chats** to **public** **AI**.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest FAQ topics and answer structures

### Task A: Suggest FAQ Topics and Answer Structures

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want to design a **chatbot** for **citizen** **queries** on our **website**. We will NOT put any citizen data into AI. Suggest: (1) **10–12** **FAQ** **topics** that citizens often ask (e.g. how to report a fault, how to pay a bill, who to contact for water, IDP submission deadlines, council meeting times); (2) for **3** of those, give a **short** **answer** **structure** (2–4 sentences) we could use as a **draft**—we will **customise** and **approve**. Use a table for (1); short paragraphs for (2).

3. **Use the output:** **Review** with **Customer Care** and **Communications**. **Add** **municipality-specific** **contact** **details** and **links**. **Load** into **your** **chatbot** **or** **help** **database**. **AI** suggests; **you** **own** the **content** and **accuracy**.

[STEP] Task B — Propose a simple conversation flow

### Task B: Propose a Simple Conversation Flow

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We are designing a **municipal** **chatbot** for **citizen** **queries**. Suggest a **simple** **conversation** **flow** as a **numbered** **list**: (1) **Welcome** message (one sentence); (2) **Menu** of **3–5** **options** (e.g. Report fault, Pay bill, Contact, Council info, Other); (3) **What** happens when user picks each (e.g. "Report fault" → brief **how** + **link** to **form** or **number**); (4) **When** to **escalate** to **human** (e.g. "If user says X or asks for person, offer **call** **centre** **number** or **request** **call-back**"); (5) **Goodbye**. We will implement in our own platform—we are NOT putting citizen chats into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Adapt** to your **channels** (web, **app**, **WhatsApp** if **used**). **Ensure** **escalation** **path** is **clear** and **monitored**. **AI** suggests **flow**; **you** **implement** and **test** with **real** **users**.

[STEP] Task C — Draft 3 sample Q&A pairs

### Task C: Draft 3 Sample Q&A Pairs

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > For a South African municipal **chatbot**, write **3** **sample** **Q&A** **pairs** for: (1) "How do I report a water leak?"; (2) "When is the next council meeting?"; (3) "How do I submit input to the IDP?" For each: **Question** (as citizen might ask); **Answer** (2–4 sentences: what to do, where to go, contact or link if applicable). Use **placeholder** like [Link to fault form] or [Contact number] where we will add our own. We will **customise** and **load** into our chatbot—we are NOT using this for live citizens in AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Replace** **placeholders** with **your** **links**, **numbers**, and **deadlines**. **Validate** with **Customer Care**. **AI** drafts; **you** **own** the **final** **content**.
