## AI Application: Predictive Citizen Satisfaction

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **aggregated**, **non-confidential** data (e.g. "satisfaction 68%," "complaints 320," "resolution 78%"). **Never** put **identifiable** **citizen** or **respondent** data into **public** **AI**. **Predictive** **satisfaction** **models** (forecasting **future** satisfaction from **leading** indicators) are typically run **in-house** or by a **vendor** on **your** **data**. Here we use **AI** to: (1) **suggest** **leading** **indicators** that might **predict** satisfaction (e.g. **resolution** **rate**, **backlog**, **response** **time**); (2) **propose** a **simple** **conceptual** **model** (which **variables** to **track**); (3) **draft** a **one-paragraph** **satisfaction** **outlook** or **"what to watch"** from **aggregated** **stats** you provide. **You** **build** any **predictive** **model** in **your** **environment**; **validate** with **Customer Care** and **Planning**.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest leading indicators for satisfaction

### Task A: Suggest Leading Indicators for Satisfaction

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want to **predict** or **anticipate** **citizen** **satisfaction** (from **surveys**) using **leading** **indicators** we can **track** **before** the **survey** (e.g. **complaint** **resolution** **rate**, **fault** **backlog**, **response** **time**, **themes** in **feedback**). We will NOT put our data into AI. Suggest: (1) **6–8** **leading** **indicators**; (2) for each, **why** it might **predict** satisfaction (e.g. "If resolution falls, satisfaction likely to fall"); (3) **how** we could **get** the **data** (which **system** or **report**). Use a table. We will **track** these and **build** our **own** **analysis** or **model**.

2. **Use the output:** **Discuss** with **Customer Care** and **Planning**. **Add** to your **Engagement** or **Citizen** **Feedback** **tracker** (Modules 7, 10). **AI** suggests **concepts**; **you** **own** the **data** and **model**.

[STEP] Task B — Propose a simple predictive satisfaction model (concept)

### Task B: Propose a Simple Predictive Satisfaction Model (Concept)

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We want a **simple** **conceptual** **model** for **predictive** **citizen** **satisfaction**: which **variables** to **track** over **time** so we can **anticipate** if **satisfaction** (from **surveys**) might **rise** or **fall** next quarter. We will NOT put our data into AI. Suggest: (1) **3–4** **input** **variables** (e.g. **complaint** **volume**, **resolution** **within** **SLA** %, **negative** **sentiment** % from **feedback**, **fault** **backlog**); (2) **one** **sentence** on **how** they might **relate** to **satisfaction** (e.g. "If resolution and sentiment improve, satisfaction likely to improve"); (3) **one** **suggested** **output** (e.g. "Satisfaction outlook: Up / Stable / Down" or "Risk of drop: Low / Medium / High"). We will implement with our own data.

2. **Use the output:** **Use** as **design** for a **simple** **dashboard** or **report** (e.g. "Leading indicators this month: resolution 78%, sentiment 35% negative → Outlook: **Watch**"). **Refine** with **data** over **time**. **AI** suggests **logic**; **you** **own** the **implementation**.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a satisfaction outlook from aggregated stats

### Task C: Draft a Satisfaction Outlook from Aggregated Stats

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** **numbers** only. Example:

   > Last quarter satisfaction 68%. This quarter so far: complaint resolution 78% (target 80%); negative sentiment from feedback 38% (up from 35%); fault backlog 31+ days up by 10. We have not run a survey yet this quarter.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **one-paragraph** **satisfaction** **outlook** or **"what to watch"** for the Municipal Manager. Say: what the **leading** **indicators** suggest; **one** **risk**; **one** **recommended** **action**. Formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **Customer Care**. Use in **management** or **performance** **reports**. **AI** drafts from **aggregates**; **you** **own** the **data** and **message**.

## When to Use Which AI Task

| Module 10 area | AI task | What you get | What you must do |
|----------------|---------|--------------|------------------|
| **Chatbots** | FAQ topics; conversation flow; sample Q&A | Design and content drafts | Implement in **your** platform; **never** live citizen chats in public AI. **POPI**. |
| **Sentiment (social, feedback)** | Categorise/themes; taxonomy; summary paragraph | Themes, taxonomy, narrative | **Anonymise** **strictly** before public AI. **Aggregated** only in reports. |
| **Complaint (categorisation, routing, NLP)** | Routing rules; keywords; NLP structure for submissions | Rules and structures | Implement in **your** system; **anonymise** for any **NLP** in public AI. |
| **Predictive satisfaction** | Leading indicators; model concept; outlook paragraph | Concepts and narrative | Build **model** in **your** environment; use **aggregated** data only in public AI. |

**Remember:** **POPI** and **anonymisation** are **non-negotiable** for **citizen** and **social** **media** **data**. **AI** **supports** **design**, **rules**, and **narrative**—it does **not** replace **your** **complaint** **system**, **chatbot** **platform**, or **human** **judgment**. **Validate** with **Customer Care**, **Communications**, and **Legal**.
