## AI Application: Sentiment Analysis of Social Media and Feedback

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Critical — POPI:** Use **only** **anonymised** **text**. **Remove** **names**, **handles**, **IDs**, **contact** **details**, and any **content** that could **identify** a **person** **before** pasting into ChatGPT or Claude. **Aggregated** **results** (themes, **sentiment** %, **counts**) are **safe** for **reports**. **Never** put **identifiable** **citizen** or **social** **media** **user** **data** into **public** **AI**. This builds on **Module 7** (sentiment and themes); here we **include** **social** **media** and **engagement** **feedback** **sources**.

[STEP] Task A — Categorise and sentiment from anonymised social excerpts

### Task A: Categorise and Sentiment from Anonymised Social Excerpts

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. **Prepare:** Take 5–10 **short** **excerpts** from **social** **media** or **feedback** (e.g. **posts**, **comments** about your **municipality** or **services**). **Anonymise**: remove **handles**, **names**, **profile** **info**. Keep only the **gist** (e.g. "No water for 3 days in our area"; "Thanks for fixing the pothole quickly").
3. Paste the **anonymised** **excerpts** and send:

   > Below are **anonymised** **excerpts** from **social** **media** or **feedback** about municipal services. For each: (1) **Category** (Water, Roads, Billing, Electricity, Waste, Other); (2) **Theme** (1–3 words, e.g. "No water", "Quick fix", "Billing error"); (3) **Sentiment** (Positive, Neutral, Negative). Use a table. Do not add or infer **personal** **data**.

4. **Use the output:** **Validate** **categories** and **themes**. **Aggregate** (e.g. "8 negative, 2 positive; top themes: water, roads"). **Report** in your **Engagement** **tracker** or **dashboard**. **Never** **paste** **identifiable** **content** into **public** **AI**.

[STEP] Task B — Suggest a social listening taxonomy

### Task B: Suggest a Social Listening Taxonomy

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We monitor **social** **media** and **feedback** about our South African municipality. We want a **taxonomy** (standard **categories** and **themes**) to **tag** **posts** and **comments** **consistently**. We will NOT put actual posts into AI. Give: (1) **6–8** **categories** (e.g. Water, Sanitation, Roads, Electricity, Waste, Billing, General service, Other); (2) for each **category**, **3–4** **theme** **labels** (e.g. for Water: "No water", "Leak", "Quality", "Pressure"). Use a table. We will use this to **train** staff or **configure** our **own** **tool**—we tag **anonymised** or **aggregated** **only**.

2. **Use the output:** **Adapt** with **Communications** and **Customer Care**. Use as **pick** **list** when **manually** or **AI-assisted** **tagging** **anonymised** **content** in **your** **systems**. **Consistency** helps **trends** and **reporting**.

[STEP] Task C — One-paragraph sentiment summary from aggregated stats

### Task C: One-Paragraph Sentiment Summary from Aggregated Stats

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** **numbers** only. Example:

   > This quarter: 150 **social** **media** **mentions** (anonymised analysis). Sentiment: 40% negative, 35% neutral, 25% positive. Top themes: water (45), roads (30), billing (25). Complaints from **other** **channels**: 320; resolution 78% within SLA. Satisfaction survey: 68% positive.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the aggregated stats above, write a **one-paragraph** **sentiment** and **feedback** **summary** for the **Municipal** **Manager** and **Communications**. Include: **social** **sentiment**, **top** **themes**, **complaint** **resolution**, **satisfaction**. Formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **Communications** or **Customer Care**. Use in **engagement** or **performance** **reports**. **AI** drafts from **aggregates**; **you** **own** the **data** and **message**.
