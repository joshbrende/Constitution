# Module 10: Citizen Engagement and Feedback Systems

Time Slot: 11:00 - 12:30 (Day 3)

## Purpose of This Session

This session deepens **citizen engagement** and **feedback** beyond the **satisfaction** and **complaints** monitoring in Module 7: **digital** platforms, **feedback** collection and analysis, **complaint** management systems, **public participation** in **IDP** processes, and **transparency** and **accountability**. **SALGA Outcome 4** (Effective Networks—citizen engagement) and **Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return—active citizenry) both depend on **meaningful** engagement and **responsive** systems. You will learn **theory** on digital engagement, feedback, complaints, IDP participation, and transparency; **practical** tasks to build an **engagement and feedback** tracker and an **IDP participation and transparency** checklist; and **AI application** tasks for **chatbots**, **sentiment** analysis of **social media** and **feedback**, **automated** **complaint** **categorisation** and **routing**, **NLP** of **public** submissions, and **predictive** **citizen** **satisfaction**—with **POPI** and **anonymisation** throughout.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** digital citizen engagement platforms: types, benefits, and how they support **feedback** and **participation**.
2. **Describe** feedback collection and analysis: channels, **themes**, and **link** to **performance** reporting (building on Module 7).
3. **Outline** complaint management systems: logging, **routing**, **resolution**, **SLA**, and **escalation**.
4. **Explain** public participation in **IDP** processes: **Municipal Systems Act**, **ward** committees, **consultation**, and **how** to **record** and **use** **inputs**.
5. **Describe** transparency and accountability: **public** **information**, **reporting**, and **responsiveness** to citizens.
6. **Build** an engagement and feedback tracker and an IDP participation and transparency checklist in Excel or Google Sheets.
7. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to support **chatbot** **design** concepts, **sentiment** and **theme** analysis of **anonymised** **social** **media** and **feedback**, **complaint** **categorisation** and **routing** **rules**, **NLP** **structures** for **public** **submissions**, and **predictive** **satisfaction** **concepts**—with **POPI**-compliant use of data (**anonymise** before public AI; **never** identifiable data).

## How to Use This Module

1. **Connect to Module 7.** Module 7 covered **citizen satisfaction**, **complaints**, **themes**, and **sentiment** (anonymised). This module **extends** to **digital** platforms, **IDP** participation, **transparency**, **chatbots**, **NLP**, and **predictive** satisfaction. Use your **Citizen Feedback** dashboard (Module 7) as a **foundation**.
2. **Work through each step.** Theory sets the concepts; **Practical** and **AI Application** give you outputs you can use in your municipality.
3. **POPI and anonymisation.** For **AI** tasks: **anonymise** all **citizen** text (no names, IDs, contact details). **Social** **media** and **public** **submissions** must be **stripped** of **identifiers** before use in ChatGPT, Claude, or other public AI. **Aggregated** summaries (themes, %) are safe. **Never** put **identifiable** data into public AI.
4. **Validate with Customer Care and Communications.** **Customer Care**, **Communications**, **IDP** coordination, and **Legal** should **validate** engagement **design** and **AI**-assisted **outputs** before use in **council** or **public** **reports**.
