[STEP] What are digital citizen engagement platforms?

## Digital Citizen Engagement Platforms

**Digital citizen engagement platforms** are **online** or **mobile** tools that enable **two-way** interaction between the **municipality** and **citizens**: information **push** (notices, reports, alerts) and **pull** (queries, feedback, complaints, **participation** in **consultations**). They support **SALGA Outcome 4** (Effective Networks—citizen engagement) and **Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return—active citizenry). **Digital** does **not** replace **face-to-face** (ward committees, imbizos, door-to-door)—it **complements** and can **reach** more people and **capture** **data** for **analysis** and **AI**.

### Types of platforms (examples)

| Type | What it does | Use |
|------|--------------|-----|
| **Municipal website** | **Information**, **forms**, **downloads**, **links** to **services**. | **Transparency**; **first** **port** of call. |
| **Mobile app** | **Fault** and **complaint** **reporting**; **billing**; **alerts**; **surveys**. | **Convenience**; **real-time** **feedback**. |
| **SMS / USSD** | **Short** **messages**; **surveys**; **reminders**; **status** **updates**. | **Reach** in **low**-data areas. |
| **Social media** | **Facebook**, **Twitter/X**, **WhatsApp** (if used for **broadcast** or **group**). | **Awareness**; **unsolicited** **feedback**; **crisis** **comms**. |
| **Online consultation / survey** | **IDP**, **budget**, **by-law** **input**; **satisfaction** **surveys**. | **Structured** **participation**; **themes** for **analysis**. |
| **Chatbot** | **Automated** **answers** to **frequently** **asked** **questions**; **routing** to **human** when needed. | **24/7** **availability**; **reduce** **call** **volume**; **AI**-enabled. |

[STEP] Benefits and link to feedback

## Benefits and Link to Feedback

- **Reach:** More **channels** = more **citizens** can **engage** (including **youth**, **busy** **residents**, **those** **outside** **ward** **meetings**).
- **Data:** **Digital** **feedback**, **complaints**, and **submissions** are **structured** or **semi-structured**—easier to **categorise**, **analyse**, and **feed** **AI** for **themes** and **sentiment** (after **anonymisation**).
- **Responsiveness:** **Faster** **acknowledgment** and **tracking** (e.g. **reference** **number**; **SLA** **visible**). **Transparency** when **metrics** (e.g. **resolution** **rate**) are **published**.
- **Cost and equity:** **Digital** can **reduce** **cost** per **interaction**; **equity** requires **access** (data, devices, literacy)—so **blend** with **non-digital** (SMS, **walk-in**, **ward** **committees**).

[STEP] Choosing and governing platforms

## Choosing and Governing Platforms

- **Fit for purpose:** Align with **objectives** (information, **feedback**, **complaints**, **IDP** **participation**). **Start** with **what** you have (website, **call** **centre**); **add** (e.g. **chatbot**, **app**) when **capacity** and **need** justify.
- **Ownership:** **Communications**, **Customer Care**, or **IT** often **own**; **IDP** and **Performance** **use** **data** for **planning** and **reporting**. **Governance** (who **approves** **content**, **who** **analyses** **feedback**) should be **clear**.
- **POPI and terms:** **Privacy** **notice**; **consent** where **required**; **secure** **storage** and **access**. **Terms** of **use** for **apps** and **portals**. **AI** (e.g. **chatbot**, **sentiment** on **feedback**) must **comply** with **POPI** and **municipal** **policy**—**anonymise** before **public** **AI**; **in-house** **AI** must also **protect** **personal** **data**.
