[STEP] Why MFMA compliance matters

## MFMA Compliance Monitoring

The **Municipal Finance Management Act (MFMA)** sets the framework for municipal **budget**, **revenue**, **expenditure**, **reporting**, and **governance**. Non-compliance leads to **audit findings**, **disclaimers** or **qualifications**, **grant** conditions, and **reputational** and **legal** risk. **SALGA Outcome 3** (Capable and Reputable Local Government) and **CoGTA**/ **National Treasury** expect systematic **MFMA compliance monitoring**.

### Key MFMA obligations (high level)

| Area | What to monitor | Typical deadline / cycle |
|------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| **Budget** | Adopted budget tabled and approved; adjustments; virements. | Per MFMA and municipal calendar. |
| **Section 71** | Monthly budget and financial reports to council and Treasury. | Monthly (often by 10th of following month). |
| **Section 72** | In-year reports; revised revenue and expenditure estimates. | Mid-year and as required. |
| **Annual report** | Annual financial statements; performance; AG process. | Per MFMA (e.g. by 31 Jan following FY). |
| **Supply chain** | SCM policy; procurement thresholds; bid processes; contract management. | Ongoing; audits check. |
| **Asset and liability** | Asset register; provisions; debt. | Ongoing; in Section 71 and AFS. |

[STEP] Section 71 and 72 — what to track

## Section 71 and 72: What to Track

- **Section 71 (monthly):** Treasury and council require **monthly** submissions on **revenue and expenditure** vs budget, **cash flow**, and other aggregates. Your **budget variance** work (Module 6) feeds this. **Tracking:** (1) **Due date** (e.g. 10th); (2) **Submitted** (yes/no, date); (3) **Quality** (queries from Treasury? corrections?); (4) **Owner** (usually CFO).
- **Section 72 (in-year):** **Mid-year** and other **in-year** reports; **revised** revenue and expenditure estimates if material changes. **Tracking:** Due dates; submitted; key variances explained; council approval.

**Compliance dashboard / tracker:** One row per **submission** (e.g. "Section 71 — January"). Columns: Due date | Submitted (Y/N, date) | Status (Green/Amber/Red) | Owner | Note. **AI** can help **design** the checklist or **draft** a "compliance status" narrative from **aggregated** status you provide—you **never** put **confidential** Treasury or AG correspondence into public AI.

[STEP] Treasury regulations and municipal-specific rules

## Treasury Regulations and Municipal-Specific Rules

- **Treasury regulations** and **municipal circulars** add **detail** (formats, additional returns, conditions). Your **CFO** or **Treasury** liaison has the **authoritative** list. Your **compliance tracker** should align with that list.
- **Municipal-specific:** By-laws, **delegations**, **petty cash**, **banking**, **investments**—each has MFMA or treasury-linked rules. **Internal Audit** and **Risk** often maintain or feed the **compliance calendar**.
