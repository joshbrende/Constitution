[STEP] What we mean by risk in municipalities

## Risk Identification and Assessment

**Risk** is the **uncertainty** that **events** or **conditions** could affect the achievement of **objectives** (IDP, SDBIP, service delivery, compliance, financial sustainability). **Risk management** involves: **identify** → **assess** (likelihood, impact) → **treat** (mitigate, accept, transfer, avoid) → **monitor** and **review**. **SALGA Outcome 1** (Agile Force) and **Outcome 3** (Capable Local Government) both benefit from systematic **risk** identification and **early** action.

### Risk categories (examples)

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| **Financial** | Revenue shortfall; debt; fraud; budget overrun; grant conditions. |
| **Operational** | Service failure; infrastructure; skills; systems. |
| **Compliance** | MFMA; labour; POPI; audit findings. |
| **Strategic** | IDP delivery; political; reputational. |
| **Governance** | Council; MPAC; delegations; ethics. |
| **External** | Economic; climate; bulk supply; intergovernmental. |

[STEP] Likelihood and impact — simple assessment

## Likelihood and Impact: Simple Assessment

- **Likelihood:** How **likely** is the risk to occur? (e.g. 1–5: Rare, Unlikely, Possible, Likely, Almost certain.) Or **%** if you prefer.
- **Impact:** If it occurs, how **severe**? (e.g. 1–5: Negligible, Minor, Moderate, Major, Severe.) Consider: **financial** (rand, grant), **service**, **reputation**, **legal**, **safety**.
- **Risk score / level:** Often **Likelihood × Impact** or a **matrix**. Used to **prioritise**: **High** and **Critical** get **treatment** and **monitoring** first; **Low** may be **accepted** and reviewed periodically.

[STEP] Risk register and treatment

## Risk Register and Treatment

- **Risk register:** For each risk: **ID** | **Description** | **Category** | **Likelihood** | **Impact** | **Score** | **Owner** | **Treatment** (what we do) | **Status** | **Next review**.
- **Treatment:** **Mitigate** (reduce likelihood or impact); **Accept** (document and monitor); **Transfer** (insurance, contract); **Avoid** (stop the activity). **Action plans** with **due dates** for high risks.
- **Review:** **Quarterly** or when **context** changes (new audit, disaster, restructure). **Early warning** (next section) uses **leading indicators** to **trigger** review or escalation **before** a risk materialises.
