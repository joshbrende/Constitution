# Module 8: Compliance and Risk Monitoring

Time Slot: 15:15 - 16:45 (Day 2)

## Purpose of This Session

This session applies **dashboard**, **KPI**, and **data** skills from Modules 3–5 (and patterns from **financial** and **service delivery** monitoring in Modules 6–7) to **compliance** and **risk**: MFMA, legislation, risk identification, early warning, and audit preparation. **SALGA Outcome 3** (Capable and Reputable Local Government) requires legal compliance, by-law enforcement, and systems thinking; **Outcome 1** (Agile Force) includes risk management. You will learn **theory** on MFMA and legislative compliance, risk and early warning, and audit readiness; **practical** tasks to build a compliance checklist/tracker and a risk register with early-warning triggers; and **AI application** tasks for **automated compliance checking**, **risk scoring**, **early-warning rules**, **audit trail** concepts, and **predictive risk**—with **aggregated, non-confidential** data only.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** MFMA compliance monitoring: Section 71/72, treasury regulations, key submissions, and how to track them.
2. **Describe** legislative compliance tracking: MFMA, Municipal Systems Act, Labour Relations Act, POPI, and other laws; how to maintain a compliance calendar.
3. **Outline** risk identification and assessment: categories, likelihood and impact, risk registers, and link to audit and oversight.
4. **Explain** early warning systems: leading indicators, triggers, escalation, and when to alert MM and council.
5. **Describe** audit preparation: what auditors look for, documentation, and how compliance and risk monitoring supports clean audits.
6. **Build** a compliance checklist and tracker and a risk register with early-warning triggers in Excel or Google Sheets.
7. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to support **compliance checklists**, **risk-scoring** concepts, **early-warning rules**, **audit-trail** structures, and **predictive risk** ideas—with **aggregated** data only; **never** confidential audit or person-specific data in public AI.

## How to Use This Module

1. **Connect to Modules 3–7.** Your **SDBIP**, **data–KPI mapping**, **dashboards**, and **financial** and **service delivery** trackers all feed into **compliance** (e.g. Section 71) and **risk** (e.g. revenue, service, governance). Use the same principles: purpose, audience, traffic lights, "Data as at," owner.
2. **Work through each step.** Theory sets the concepts; **Practical** and **AI Application** give you outputs you can use in your municipality.
3. **Protect confidentiality.** In **AI Application** tasks, use only **aggregated, non-confidential** information. **Never** put **confidential** audit documents, **person-specific** data, or **internal** legal advice into ChatGPT, Claude, or other public AI. **AI** suggests **structures**, **checklists**, and **rules**; you **implement** with **your** data in **your** environment.
4. **Validate with owners.** **CFO**, **Risk**, **Internal Audit**, and **Legal** should **validate** and **approve** compliance and risk outputs before use in council or external reporting.
