## AI Application: Audit Trail Analysis and Predictive Risk

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Critical:** Use only **generic**, **structural** descriptions. **Never** put **confidential** **audit** working papers, **AG** drafts, **internal** audit reports, or **person-specific** data into public AI. AI can: (1) **suggest** an **audit-trail** or **evidence-index** **structure** (what to document, where to store) for **key** processes; (2) **propose** **predictive risk** **ideas** (which **leading** indicators might **predict** an audit finding or compliance failure)—as **concepts** you can implement with **your** data in **your** environment; (3) **draft** a **generic** audit **preparation** checklist from **public** AG and treasury guidance. You **customise** and **validate** with **Internal Audit** and **CFO**.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest an audit-trail / evidence-index structure

### Task A: Suggest an Audit-Trail / Evidence-Index Structure

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want an **audit-trail** or **evidence-index** structure for **key** processes that auditors (AG, internal) typically check: (1) **Budget** (adoption, adjustments, virements); (2) **Section 71 / 72** (submissions, council approval); (3) **Revenue and expenditure** (reconciliations, approvals); (4) **Procurement** (award, contract, payment). For each process, suggest: (1) what **evidence** to keep (e.g. council resolution, signed return, reconciliation); (2) **who** approves; (3) **where** to store (e.g. folder, system). Use a table. We will implement in our own systems—we are NOT putting any confidential audit or internal data into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Validate** with **Internal Audit** and **CFO**. Use it to **design** or **improve** your **documentation** and **filing**. **AI** suggests **structure**; **you** own the content and storage.

[STEP] Task B — Propose predictive risk concepts (leading indicators for audit/compliance)

### Task B: Propose Predictive Risk Concepts (Leading Indicators for Audit/Compliance)

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We want **predictive risk** ideas: which **leading indicators** might **predict** a higher chance of an **audit finding** or **compliance** failure in a South African municipality? We will NOT put our data into AI—we only want **concepts**. Suggest 5–7 **leading indicators** (e.g. "Section 71 submitted late more than twice in a year"; "No risk register review in 6 months"; "High staff turnover in Finance") and for each: (1) the indicator; (2) why it might predict risk; (3) one **suggested** action. Use a table. We will track these in our own systems and decide thresholds.

2. **Use the output:** **Discuss** with **Risk** and **Internal Audit**. Use it to **add** **triggers** or **early-warning** rules to your **risk** and **compliance** trackers. **AI** suggests **concepts**; **you** implement with **your** data.

[STEP] Task C — Generate a generic audit preparation checklist

### Task C: Generate a Generic Audit Preparation Checklist

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We will have an **Auditor-General** and **internal** audit. We need a **generic** **audit preparation checklist**: what to have **ready** before the audit (documents, reconciliations, access, key contacts). Base this on **public** AG and Treasury guidance for municipalities. Give 12–15 items in a simple checklist: # | Item | Who typically prepares | When (e.g. 4 weeks before). We will **customise** with our Internal Audit and CFO—we are NOT putting any confidential or internal audit data into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Customise** with your **audit** liaison and **CFO**. Use it in your **audit readiness** process. **AI** gives a **generic** list; **you** and **Internal Audit** **own** the final checklist.
