## Module 8: Key Takeaways

1. **MFMA compliance monitoring:** **Section 71** (monthly), **Section 72** (in-year), **annual report**, **treasury** returns, and **SCM** are core. A **compliance tracker** with **due dates**, **submitted (Y/N)**, **owner**, and **status** supports on-time submission and **audit readiness**. **AI** can help **design** checklist structures and **"when to flag"** rules—**never** put **confidential** Treasury or AG documents into public AI.

2. **Legislative compliance tracking:** Beyond MFMA: **Systems Act**, **Structures Act**, **LRA**, **POPI**, **OHS**, and **sector** laws. A **compliance calendar** with **obligations**, **due dates**, **owners**, and **evidence** links supports **audit** and **oversight**. **Early warning** can **alert** when due dates approach and submissions are not yet done.

3. **Risk identification and assessment:** **Categories** (financial, operational, compliance, strategic, governance, external); **likelihood** and **impact**; **risk score** (e.g. L×I) and **bands** (Low, Medium, High, Critical). **Risk register** with **owner**, **treatment**, and **review** dates. **AI** can suggest **matrix**, **bands**, and **prioritisation** rules—you **implement** and **validate** with **Risk** and **audit committee**.

4. **Early warning systems:** **Leading** indicators and **triggers** (e.g. "Section 71 not submitted 2 days before due"; "Collection rate &lt;72%") to **alert** before a **risk** or **compliance** failure. **Escalation** (owner → CFO → MM → council) must be **documented**. **AI** can suggest **trigger** and **escalation** rules—you **implement** in **your** trackers with **your** thresholds.

5. **Audit preparation:** **Ongoing** compliance and risk monitoring = **audit readiness**. **Documentation** and **evidence** (who, what, when, where) for **budget**, **Section 71/72**, **revenue/expenditure**, **procurement**. **Audit-trail** or **evidence-index** structure; **generic** audit **prep** checklist. **AI** can suggest **structures** and **checklists** from **public** guidance—**never** **confidential** audit or AG data in public AI. **Internal Audit** and **CFO** **validate**.

6. **Practical skills:** You can **build** a **compliance checklist and tracker** (MFMA/Treasury and Legislative sheets; due dates, status, owner) and a **risk register** with **early-warning** triggers. Both support **dashboards** for the MM, **audit committee**, and **council**.

7. **AI Application—data and ethics:** Use only **aggregated**, **generic**, **non-confidential** data. **Never** **confidential** audit, **Treasury**, **legal**, or **person-specific** data in public AI. **Validate** and **approve** all AI output with **CFO**, **Risk**, or **Internal Audit**. **AI supports**; it does **not** replace **Internal Audit**, **Risk**, or **legal** advice. **Ethics** in compliance and governance (accountability, transparency, human oversight) and in AI use are explicit in this module (SAQA-aligned for governance-related learning).
