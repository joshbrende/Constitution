## AI Application: Automated Compliance Checking

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **generic**, **non-confidential** information. **Never** put **confidential** Treasury returns, **AG** working papers, **legal** advice, or **person-specific** data into public AI. AI **cannot** access your **live** systems or **submit** on your behalf. It can: (1) **suggest** a **compliance checklist** structure from **public** MFMA and treasury guidance; (2) **generate** **"when to flag"** rules for due dates and submissions; (3) **draft** a short **compliance status** narrative from **aggregated** status you provide (e.g. "10 of 12 on time; 2 late."). You **implement** in **your** tracker; you **validate** with **CFO** and **Risk**.

[STEP] Task A — Generate an MFMA compliance checklist

### Task A: Generate an MFMA Compliance Checklist

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We need a **compliance checklist** for **MFMA** and **National Treasury** submissions. Base this on **public** MFMA and Treasury requirements (Section 71, Section 72, annual report, and common circulars). For each obligation: (1) name; (2) source (e.g. MFMA Section 71); (3) typical frequency (monthly, quarterly, annual); (4) one-line description of what is required; (5) who typically owns it (role). Use a table. We will **customise** with our CFO and Treasury liaison—we are NOT putting any confidential or internal data into AI.

3. **Use the output:** **Compare** with your **CFO** and **Treasury** liaison's **list**. **Add** or **remove** rows to match **your** obligations. Use it as the **basis** for your **Compliance** tracker (Practical). **AI** suggests; **you** own the final checklist.

[STEP] Task B — Suggest "when to flag" rules for compliance

### Task B: Suggest "When to Flag" Rules for Compliance

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We track MFMA Section 71 (monthly, due e.g. 10th), Section 72 (mid-year, annual), and annual report. We want **early-warning rules**: when to **flag** or **alert** so we avoid late submission. Suggest 5–7 rules in the form: "IF [condition] THEN [action]". E.g. "IF Section 71 due in 5 days and not yet submitted THEN alert CFO and MM." Consider: due date approach; not submitted; and who to escalate to. Use a table. We will implement in our tracker with our own due dates and roles.

2. **Use the output:** **Adapt** to your **due dates**, **roles**, and **escalation** policy. Use it in your **Compliance** and **Early warning** sheets. **AI** suggests; **you** implement.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a compliance status paragraph from aggregated status

### Task C: Draft a Compliance Status Paragraph from Aggregated Status

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** information only. Example:

   > Our compliance status this quarter: MFMA Section 71 — 3 of 3 submitted on time. Section 72 mid-year — submitted 2 weeks before deadline. Legislative: IDP review on track; one LRA return late by 5 days, now done. No overdue items as of today.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **one-paragraph compliance status** for the Municipal Manager and Audit Committee. Include: (1) MFMA; (2) other legislative; (3) any late or at-risk items and what was done; (4) one sentence on what to watch next. Use formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **CFO** or **Risk**. Use in **governance** or **audit committee** papers. **Never** include **confidential** detail.
