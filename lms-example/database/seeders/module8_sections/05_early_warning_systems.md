[STEP] What is an early warning system?

## Early Warning Systems

An **early warning system** uses **leading indicators** and **triggers** to **signal** that a **risk** or **performance problem** may be emerging, so you can **act before** it becomes critical. It supports **SALGA Outcome 1** (Agile Force—risk management) and **Outcome 3** (Capable Local Government). Modules 5 (anomaly and alerts), 6 (financial), and 7 (service delivery) already introduced **alert** and **"when to flag"** ideas—here we apply them to **governance**, **compliance**, and **risk**.

### Leading vs lagging indicators

| Type | Meaning | Examples |
|------|---------|----------|
| **Lagging** | Tell you **after** the fact (e.g. audit outcome, qualified report). | AG opinion; Section 71 late; fraud discovered. |
| **Leading** | **Predict** or **warn** before the bad outcome. | Submission **not yet** sent as due date nears; **trend** in variances; **rise** in complaints or fault backlogs. |

**Early warning** focuses on **leading** indicators and **rules** (e.g. "If Section 71 not submitted 2 days before due, alert CFO and MM").

[STEP] Triggers and escalation

## Triggers and Escalation

- **Trigger:** A **condition** that, when **true**, generates an **alert**. Examples: (1) **Compliance:** Due date in X days and submission not done. (2) **Financial:** Collection rate &lt; Y%; or 90+ debt &gt; Z%. (3) **Service:** Backlog 31+ &gt; threshold. (4) **Risk:** A high-risk item has no **treatment** update in 90 days.
- **Escalation:** Who is **notified** and **when**. E.g. **Owner** first; if not resolved in 24h, **CFO** or **MM**; if **critical**, **MM** and **Chair of Council** or **Mayor**. **Document** the escalation path; **AI** can help **suggest** trigger rules and escalation levels from **generic** descriptions—you **implement** with **your** thresholds and **your** structure.

[STEP] Governance and council-facing early warning

## Governance and Council-Facing Early Warning

- **Governance risks:** Late **Section 71**; **audit** deadline at risk; **high-risk** item with **no** owner; **MPAC** resolution **not** implemented. **Dashboard** or **tracker** with **traffic lights** and **"What needs attention"** is a form of **early warning** for the MM and council.
- **Frequency:** **Weekly** or **monthly** for **compliance** and **risk**; **real-time** or **daily** only where you have **systems** to support it. **Start simple:** a **manual** or **Excel** check against **due dates** and **thresholds**; **automate** later where value is high.
