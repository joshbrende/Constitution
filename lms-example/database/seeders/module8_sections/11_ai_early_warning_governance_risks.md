## AI Application: Early Warning for Governance Risks

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **generic** descriptions of **governance** and **compliance** **situations**—**never** **confidential** audit, **legal**, or **person-specific** data. AI can: (1) **suggest** **leading indicators** and **triggers** for **governance** and **compliance** risks (e.g. Section 71, audit, MPAC); (2) **propose** **escalation** levels (owner → CFO → MM → council); (3) **draft** a short **"early-warning design"** or **trigger list** you can **customise**. You **implement** in **your** trackers and **validate** with **Risk** and **MM office**.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest leading indicators and triggers for governance

### Task A: Suggest Leading Indicators and Triggers for Governance

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want **early-warning triggers** for **governance** and **compliance**. We track: MFMA Section 71 (monthly), Section 72, annual report, audit deadlines, and high risks from our risk register. Suggest 8–10 **triggers** in the form: "IF [leading indicator or condition] THEN [alert/action]". For each, add: (1) the trigger; (2) who should be alerted first; (3) one sentence on why it is a leading (not lagging) indicator. Use a table. We will implement with our own dates and roles—we are NOT putting confidential data into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Adapt** to your **due dates**, **roles**, and **systems**. Use it in your **Early warning** sheet (Practical). **AI** suggests; **you** own the final triggers.

[STEP] Task B — Propose escalation levels

### Task B: Propose Escalation Levels

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We have early-warning triggers for compliance (Section 71, Section 72, audit) and risk (e.g. revenue, service backlogs). We want **escalation levels**: Level 1 = owner; Level 2 = CFO or Director; Level 3 = MM; Level 4 = MM and Mayor/Chair. Suggest: for each of 4 **example** trigger types (submission due soon; submission overdue; high risk with no treatment update; audit deadline at risk), which **level** to start at and when to **escalate** to the next (e.g. "If not resolved in 24 hours"). Use a table. We will customise for our municipality.

2. **Use the output:** **Validate** with **MM** office and **Risk**. Use it to **document** your **escalation** policy. **AI** suggests; **you** adopt.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a one-paragraph "early-warning design" summary

### Task C: Draft a One-Paragraph "Early-Warning Design" Summary

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** design only. Example:

   > We have 8 early-warning triggers: 4 for compliance (Section 71, 72, annual report, one other), 4 for risk (revenue, service backlog, audit, one governance). Escalation: owner first; then CFO at 24h; MM if 48h. We check triggers weekly. We will add 2 more when we have data.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **one-paragraph summary** of our early-warning system for the Audit Committee. Describe: what we monitor, how we escalate, and how often we check. Formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **Risk** or **MM**. Use in **audit committee** or **governance** papers.
