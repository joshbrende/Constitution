## AI Application: Risk Scoring

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **generic** risk **categories** and **scoring logic**—**never** your **actual** risk descriptions if they are **confidential** or **sensitive**. AI can: (1) **suggest** a **risk matrix** (likelihood × impact) and **band** definitions (Low, Medium, High, Critical); (2) **propose** **factors** or **weights** for a **composite** risk score if you want one; (3) **draft** **prioritisation** rules (which risks to treat first, review most often). You **implement** in **your** risk register; **validate** with **Risk** and **Internal Audit**.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest a risk matrix and bands

### Task A: Suggest a Risk Matrix and Bands

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We need a **risk matrix** for our risk register: **Likelihood** 1–5 (1=Rare, 5=Almost certain) and **Impact** 1–5 (1=Negligible, 5=Severe). We will use **Likelihood × Impact** as the score. Suggest: (1) **Score bands** (e.g. 1–4 Low, 5–9 Medium, 10–15 High, 16–25 Critical) with a one-line action for each (e.g. "Critical: treat immediately; report to council"); (2) a small **matrix table** (5×5) showing the score in each cell. We will use this in our risk register—we are NOT putting our actual risks into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Align** with your **Risk** or **audit committee** **policy**. Use it in your **Risk register** (Practical). **AI** suggests; **you** adopt and **sign off**.

[STEP] Task B — Propose risk prioritisation and review rules

### Task B: Propose Risk Prioritisation and Review Rules

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We have a municipal risk register with **Likelihood** and **Impact** and a **score** (L×I). We want **prioritisation rules**: (1) which **score band** (e.g. High, Critical) gets **treatment** and **council** or **audit committee** reporting; (2) how **often** to **review** each band (e.g. Critical: monthly; High: quarterly; Medium: bi-annually; Low: annually); (3) **one** rule for when to **escalate** (e.g. "If risk score increases by 2 or more, re-assess and escalate to MM"). Use a table for (1) and (2); one short paragraph for (3). We will implement in our process.

2. **Use the output:** **Validate** with **Risk** and **audit committee**. Use it to **set** **Next review** and **escalation** in your **Risk register**. **AI** suggests; **you** own the policy.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a risk summary from aggregated stats

### Task C: Draft a Risk Summary from Aggregated Stats

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** numbers only. Example:

   > Our risk register: 15 risks. By band: Critical 2, High 4, Medium 6, Low 3. Both Critical have treatment and owners. One High risk (revenue) has a trigger: collection &lt;72%. All High and Critical were reviewed this quarter.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **one-paragraph risk summary** for the Audit Committee. Include: (1) overall picture (number, bands); (2) that Critical/High have owners and treatment; (3) one sentence on what to watch. Formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with **Risk** or **MM**. Use in **audit committee** or **council** papers. **Never** put **confidential** risk detail in public AI.
