## Module 2: Key Takeaways

1. **AI** includes Machine Learning, NLP, and—for daily drafting and analysis—**generative AI** (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude). Generative AI is especially useful for drafting, summarising, structuring text, and supporting checklists.

2. **AI complements** traditional dashboards and reports: use both. AI assists; you validate and approve.

3. **Hands-on use**: You can already use generative AI for briefs, summaries, extracting commitments and KPIs from plans, analysing non-confidential feedback, and generating or tailoring compliance checklists.

4. **Ethics and POPI:** Do not put personal or confidential data into public AI tools. Use anonymised, public, or synthetic material. Prefer approved internal tools when handling sensitive data. **Ethics** in AI use (fairness, transparency, human-in-the-loop) and **POPI** are explicit in this module and reinforced in all later AI sections (SAQA-aligned).

5. **Build a prompt library** and refine it for your context. You will apply and extend these skills in Modules 3–12.
