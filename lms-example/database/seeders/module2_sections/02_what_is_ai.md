## What is AI? Branches Relevant to the Public Sector

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is a broad field. For performance management and municipal work, three branches matter most:

| Branch | What it does (in plain language) | Municipal use you will see in this course |
|--------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Machine Learning (ML)** | Systems that learn patterns from data and make predictions or classifications without being explicitly programmed for every case. | Revenue and debt forecasting; fraud-risk scoring; demand forecasting for services. |
| **Natural Language Processing (NLP)** | AI that works with human language: reading, summarizing, translating, extracting information, and generating text. | Summarising reports; extracting KPIs from IDPs; categorising complaints; drafting and editing. |
| **Computer Vision** | AI that interprets images and video. | (Less central in this course) Asset inspection, meter reading, document scanning. |

**Generative AI** is a part of NLP. It uses **large language models (LLMs)**—such as those behind ChatGPT, Claude, and similar tools—to *generate* new text (and sometimes images) from a **prompt** (your instruction or question). You give it text in; you get text out. It can draft, summarise, analyse, compare, and brainstorm. For municipal officials with heavy reading and writing loads, generative AI is the most immediately useful: it can accelerate drafting, checking alignment, and turning documents into structured outputs (lists, tables, briefs).
