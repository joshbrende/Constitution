## Data Privacy and the POPI Act

The **Protection of Personal Information Act (POPI)** governs how personal data is collected, used, and stored. When using **public generative AI tools** (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude) that send data to external servers:

### Do **not** put into public AI tools

- **Personal information** of staff, councillors, or residents (names, ID numbers, contact details, medical or financial details, etc.).
- **Confidential** council, legal, or procurement documents.
- **Confidential** internal memos, disciplinary matters, or sensitive HR content.
- Any data that your municipality classifies as restricted or confidential.

### You **may** use (with care)

- **Anonymised or aggregated** figures and descriptions (e.g. "Our collection rate is 72%" or "We have 50 km of gravel roads").
- **Public** documents: published MFMA circulars, SALGA material, legislation, or your own *non-confidential* excerpts.
- **Hypothetical or generic** scenarios (e.g. "Draft a brief for a municipality facing revenue pressure" without naming people or the municipality).
- **Your own wording** that does not disclose personal or confidential information.

### Practical rules

- If in doubt, **do not paste** the content. Use summaries, anonymised examples, or generic versions.
- Prefer **internal or approved** AI tools (if your municipality has them) for any work involving more sensitive data.
- In the hands-on tasks in this module, we use only **non-confidential, synthetic, or public** material.
