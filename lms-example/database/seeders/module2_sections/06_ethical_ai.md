## Ethical AI and the Public Sector

When using AI in the public sector, several principles apply:

- **Fairness and non-discrimination**: AI should not reinforce bias against groups. Be aware that models can reflect biases in training data; use and review outputs with this in mind.
- **Transparency and accountability**: You—and your municipality—remain accountable for decisions. AI is a tool to support judgment, not to replace it. Document when and how you use AI in preparing reports or decisions where appropriate.
- **Human in the loop**: Always validate AI output before it becomes an official document, a KPI, or a basis for action. Do not automate final decisions on hiring, sanctions, or service denial without human review.
- **Purpose and proportionality**: Use AI where it adds value and is proportionate to the task. Align with your municipality’s policies and, where relevant, SALGA and national guidelines.

These principles will be reinforced in the hands-on tasks: you will use AI to *draft*, *suggest*, and *structure*—then you will *check*, *edit*, and *approve*.
