## AI Application 1: Generative AI for Drafting and Summarising

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai) (free tiers are sufficient).

### Task A: Summarise a technical or policy paragraph

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Paste this sample (or a short, **non-confidential** excerpt from an MFMA circular, SALGA communiqué, or similar):

   > Municipalities must submit the Annual Report to the council by 31 January. The report must include the annual performance report, the annual financial statements, and the report of the auditor-general. The municipal manager is responsible for ensuring that the report is prepared in accordance with the Local Government: Municipal Systems Act and the MFMA.

3. Send this prompt:

   > Summarise the paragraph above in 4 short bullet points: (1) deadline, (2) main components of the report, (3) who is responsible, (4) the main legislation. Use plain language.

4. **Use the output:** Compare the bullets to the source. Did the AI miss anything or add anything? In practice, you would correct and then use this as a quick reference or briefing note for your team.

### Task B: Draft a one-pager for a council or committee submission

1. In the **same or a new chat**, send this prompt (edit the bracketed parts for your context):

   > I am a [e.g. Director: Finance / Performance Management Officer] in a South African municipality. I need a one-page briefing for the [Finance Committee / Council] on why we should invest in improving our revenue collection systems, with a focus on reducing the debt collection period. Include: (1) the problem in 2–3 sentences (our collection period, impact on cash flow), (2) what we propose (broad strokes: data, technology, and process improvements), (3) what we need from the committee (e.g. approval to procure, political support for enforcement). Use formal but clear language. Do not include specific figures—use placeholders like [X days] and [Y%].

2. **Use the output:** Treat this as a **first draft**. You would insert your real numbers, check alignment with your IDP and SDBIP, and get sign-off before submission. This illustrates how generative AI can speed up drafting while you keep control of accuracy and approvals.
