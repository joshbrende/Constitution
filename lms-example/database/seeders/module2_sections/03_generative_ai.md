## Generative AI: What It Is and Why It Matters for You

**Generative AI** refers to AI that *creates* new content—in our case, mainly text—based on patterns learned from vast amounts of written material. When you use **ChatGPT**, **Claude**, **Microsoft Copilot**, or similar tools, you are using generative AI.

### How it works in practice

- You write a **prompt**: an instruction or question (e.g. *"Summarise this paragraph in three bullet points"* or *"Draft a one-page brief for a council submission on revenue collection challenges"*).
- The tool returns generated text. You can refine with follow-up prompts (e.g. *"Make it more formal"* or *"Add a section on POPI considerations"*).
- You remain responsible for **accuracy, compliance, and approval**. The AI assists; it does not replace your judgment or your role as author.

### Why it matters for municipal performance management

- **Drafting**: Council submissions, committee reports, internal briefs, and emails can be drafted and edited faster, leaving more time for verification and strategic thinking.
- **Summarising**: Long MFMA circulars, SALGA communiqués, or internal reports can be summarised into action points and key takeaways.
- **Structuring**: Unstructured text (e.g. from IDP paragraphs, minutes, or feedback forms) can be turned into lists, tables, or KPIs for validation and reporting.
- **Checklists and compliance support**: You can ask the AI to generate or tailor checklists (e.g. MFMA submission checklist, governance checklist) or to extract actions and deadlines from documents.
- **Analysis of non-confidential feedback**: Themes and sentiment in citizen feedback, surveys, or open comments can be explored to support service delivery reporting.
