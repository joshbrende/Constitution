## AI vs Traditional Analytics

| | **Traditional (rules-based) analytics** | **AI-driven approaches** |
|---|----------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| **How it works** | Predefined rules, formulas, and reports (e.g. Excel, BI dashboards, fixed KPIs). | Learns from data or language; can generalise to new cases and generate new text. |
| **Typical use** | Monthly reports, budget variance, agreed KPI dashboards, compliance checklists you already have. | Predicting outcomes (e.g. non-payment, demand); extracting structure from unstructured text; drafting and summarising; discovering themes in feedback. |
| **When to use** | When the structure and rules are clear and stable. | When you have lots of text to process, need first drafts, or want to explore patterns and possibilities. |

In practice, you will **combine** both: dashboards and reports for your core numbers; generative AI for drafting, summarising, and analysing text; and predictive AI (in later modules) for forecasting and risk.
