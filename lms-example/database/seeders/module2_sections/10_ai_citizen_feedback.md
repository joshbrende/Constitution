## AI Application 3: Citizen Feedback and Sentiment

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

### Why this matters for performance management

Citizen feedback—from complaints, surveys, or public participation—is a key input for **service delivery** and **SALGA Outcome 5 (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return)**. Manually reading and coding hundreds of comments is time-consuming. AI can help **categorise themes** and **assess sentiment** (positive, neutral, negative) to support reporting and prioritisation. You still need to interpret and validate; AI gives a first pass.

### Task: Categorise and assess sentiment in sample feedback

1. In a **new chat**, paste this sample (synthetic—no real citizens; in practice, use only **non-confidential, anonymised** excerpts or aggregated summaries):

   > The water supply in our area is often interrupted, sometimes for two days. When we call the municipal hotline, we are put on hold for a long time. The roads in the township are in bad condition and the streetlights do not work. On the positive side, the waste collection has improved in the last few months and the staff are friendly when we go to the office.

2. Send this prompt:

   > For the citizen feedback above: (a) list the main themes or topics (e.g. water, roads, complaints process, waste, customer service); (b) for each theme, say if the sentiment is positive, negative, or neutral, and give a one-line summary of what the citizen is saying. Use a simple table: Theme | Sentiment | Summary.

3. **Use the output:** In a real case, you would run this kind of analysis on batches of **anonymised** feedback. The table helps you report to council or in your SDBIP: "Top themes in Q2 feedback were water, roads, and complaints handling; sentiment was mostly negative on water and roads, positive on waste and front-office service." This supports evidence-based performance reporting and prioritisation.
