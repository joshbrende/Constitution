## KPI Selection and Weighting

### Which KPIs for Which Dashboard?

Use your **SDBIP**, **Logic Model**, and **data–KPI mapping** (Modules 3 and 4) as the source. Do not invent new KPIs for the dashboard; use the ones you already report on, and add only where you have **data** and **ownership**.

| Dashboard type | Typical KPIs (examples) |
|----------------|-------------------------|
| **Executive / SDBIP overview** | Collection rate; top 5–6 SDBIP KPIs across directorates; maybe 1–2 risk or compliance flags. |
| **Revenue** | Collection rate; debtors ageing; revenue vs budget; new connections or billing. |
| **Service delivery** | Connections, faults, resolution time, citizen satisfaction; backlogs. |
| **Governance / compliance** | MFMA submissions on time; audit findings; council resolutions implemented; performance agreements signed. |
| **Programme or project** | Outputs and outcomes from the Logic Model for that programme. |

[STEP] Leading vs lagging — balance

## Leading vs Lagging: Balance Both

From Module 3: **leading** indicators help you **steer** during the year; **lagging** indicators support **year-end accountability**.

| | **Leading** | **Lagging** |
|---|-------------|-------------|
| **Examples** | % meters read on time; % performance agreements signed; backlog of faults; % of SDBIP data updated. | Collection rate; audit outcome; citizen satisfaction; connections delivered. |
| **Use on dashboard** | Early warning: “If this drops, we may miss the lagging target.” | Accountability: “This is what we report to council and CoGTA.” |

**Balance:** Do not show only lagging (you see problems too late). Do not show only leading (council and oversight need the headline results). A typical executive dashboard might have **2–3 leading** and **4–5 lagging** KPIs.

[STEP] Weighting and priority

## Weighting and Priority

- **Weighting** (e.g. for a scorecard or index): If you combine several KPIs into one “score,” define the weights explicitly (e.g. collection 40%, audit 30%, service delivery 30%). Document and get sign-off; do not hide the formula.
- **Priority on the screen:** The **order and size** of KPIs on the dashboard signal priority. Put the most important—for that audience and purpose—at the top or centre. Align with what the MM, council, or SALGA care about most.
- **Traffic lights** (next section) are a simple way to show status without implying a numeric weight. They support “what needs attention” at a glance.
