## Dashboard Design Principles

### 1. Purpose and Audience First

Every dashboard should have a **clear purpose** and **defined audience**:

| Purpose | Example | Audience |
|---------|---------|----------|
| **Executive oversight** | High-level view of SDBIP, revenue, and key risks. | MM, mayoral committee, council. |
| **Directorate or programme management** | KPIs for one area (e.g. water, revenue, HR). | Director, Section 56/57 managers. |
| **Operational** | Faults, tickets, daily collection, backlogs. | Supervisors, technical or revenue teams. |
| **Audit and compliance** | MFMA submissions, audit actions, compliance status. | CFO, audit, MM office. |

**Design for one primary audience per dashboard.** If you need both an executive and an operational view, use **two** dashboards or a clear hierarchy: summary on top, drill-down below.

[STEP] Link to the performance framework

### 2. Link to the Performance Framework

Dashboards should reflect your **Logic Model**, **SDBIP**, and **golden thread** (Module 3):

- **Output and outcome indicators** from your Logic Model and SDBIP belong on the dashboard. Avoid ad hoc metrics that are not in your plan.
- **Golden thread:** Where possible, show the link from KPI → SDBIP objective → IDP priority (e.g. in a subtitle or a separate one-pager). That supports audits and SALGA reporting.
- **SALGA outcomes:** Tag or group KPIs by the SALGA outcome they support (Outcome 2: innovation; 3: capable government; 5: service delivery; etc.). It makes alignment visible.

[STEP] Less is more — hierarchy

### 3. Less Is More: Hierarchy and Focus

| Principle | What it means |
|-----------|---------------|
| **One screen, one story** | A dashboard should answer **one main question** (e.g. “Are we on track for collection?” or “What is the status of service delivery KPIs?”). |
| **5–9 KPIs per view** | Beyond that, attention spreads. Use **tabs**, **pages**, or **drill-downs** for more. |
| **Hierarchy** | Most important KPI(s) at the **top** or **centre**, larger or with traffic lights. Supporting detail below or to the side. |
| **No decoration** | Avoid 3D charts, unnecessary icons, or colours that do not carry meaning. Every element should earn its place. |

[STEP] Consistency and maintainability

### 4. Consistency and Maintainability

- **Same KPI, same definition** across dashboards. Use the same formula and data source as in your SDBIP and data–KPI mapping (Module 4).
- **Naming:** Use the same labels as in your official plans (e.g. “Collection rate” as in Treasury or your policy).
- **Owner:** One person or team owns the dashboard: who updates it, who is allowed to change the layout, and who answers questions. Without an owner, dashboards go stale.
