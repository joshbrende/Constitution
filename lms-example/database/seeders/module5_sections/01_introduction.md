# Module 5: AI-Powered KPI Dashboards

Time Slot: 09:00 - 10:30

## Purpose of This Session

In Modules 1–4 you built the **strategic context** (SALGA 2026), **AI fundamentals**, the **performance framework** (Logic Models, indicators, golden thread), and the **data foundation** (inventory, quality, KPI–data mapping). This module brings that together into **dashboards**: visual, at-a-glance views of your KPIs that support proactive management, council briefings, and oversight. You will learn **theory** on real-time vs batch reporting, dashboard design, KPI selection, visual analytics, and mobile use; **practical** tasks to build a one-page KPI dashboard in Excel or Sheets and to write a dashboard specification; and **AI application** tasks to generate dashboard designs, anomaly and alert rules, and natural-language insights from KPI data.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** when to use real-time vs batch reporting and the trade-offs for municipalities.
2. **Apply** dashboard design principles: purpose, audience, hierarchy, and alignment with your SDBIP and Logic Model.
3. **Select and weight** KPIs for a dashboard—balancing leading and lagging, and linking to the golden thread.
4. **Use** visual analytics best practices: chart types, traffic lights, colour, and avoiding clutter.
5. **Consider** mobile and responsive design so dashboards work for MM, directors, and councillors on the move.
6. **Build** a one-page KPI dashboard for one area (e.g. revenue, service delivery, governance) in Excel or Google Sheets.
7. **Write** a dashboard specification (audience, KPIs, data source, refresh, owner) so IT or a BI tool can implement it.
8. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to design dashboards, suggest anomaly and alert rules, and generate short narrative insights from KPI numbers.

## How to Use This Module

1. **Connect to Modules 3 and 4.** Your **Logic Model** and **KPI list** (Module 3) and your **data source–KPI mapping** (Module 4) define what can go on a dashboard. Use them to pick KPIs and to confirm data is available.
2. **Work through each step.** Use **Next** and **Previous** to move. Theory sets the concepts; **Practical** and **AI Application** give you outputs you can use immediately.
3. **Start simple.** A one-page Excel or Sheets dashboard with 4–6 KPIs and traffic lights is a strong start. You can expand to BI tools (Power BI, Tableau, etc.) later.
4. **Validate AI output.** AI can suggest layouts, chart types, and alert rules; you and your PMU or data owner validate and adapt.
