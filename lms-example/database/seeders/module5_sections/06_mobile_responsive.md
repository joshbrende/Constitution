[STEP] Why mobile matters for municipal dashboards

## Mobile and Responsive Dashboards

### Why Mobile Matters

MM, directors, and councillors often check performance **on a phone or tablet**—in meetings, on site, or travelling. A dashboard that only works on a large desktop is less useful. **Mobile and responsive** design means the layout adapts to smaller screens so key numbers and status are readable and actionable.

| Need | How mobile helps |
|------|------------------|
| **Quick check** | “Is collection on track?” or “Any reds this week?” without opening a laptop. |
| **Council or committee** | View the one-page summary on a tablet during a meeting. |
| **Accountability** | Leadership can monitor KPIs without depending on a printed pack. |

[STEP] Constraints and simple adaptations

## Constraints and Simple Adaptations

| Constraint | Adaptation |
|------------|------------|
| **Small screen** | Fewer KPIs per screen; **one main number or status** per row. Scroll for more. |
| **Touch** | Larger tap targets; avoid tiny buttons or links. |
| **Portrait vs landscape** | Design for **portrait** (phone) first: stacking blocks vertically works. |
| **Heavy charts** | Complex charts (e.g. multi-line, detailed maps) are hard on mobile. Prefer **summary numbers and traffic lights** on the mobile view; offer “View full dashboard on desktop” for detail. |
| **Export or PDF** | Ensure the dashboard or its export (PDF, image) is readable when printed or sent to a phone. |

[STEP] What you can do in Excel or Sheets

## What You Can Do in Excel or Google Sheets

- **Freeze the top row** and left column so headers stay visible when scrolling.
- **Larger font** for the main KPI values (e.g. 18–24 pt) so they are readable on a phone when the sheet is opened or exported to PDF.
- **One dashboard “tab” or sheet for “Mobile summary”** with 4–6 key numbers and traffic lights only; hide detail in other tabs.
- **PDF export:** Many BI tools and Excel/Sheets can export to PDF. Test that the one-page summary fits and is legible on a phone.

You do not need a full responsive web app to start. A **simple, one-page sheet** with big numbers and traffic lights, exported to PDF, already works on mobile for “at a glance” use.
