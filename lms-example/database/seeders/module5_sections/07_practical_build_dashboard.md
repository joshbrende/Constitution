## Practical: Build a One-Page KPI Dashboard

**Tools:** **Google Sheets** or **Excel**.

### Step 1: Choose one area and 4–6 KPIs

Pick **one** area: e.g. **Revenue**, **Service delivery (water or roads)**, **Governance**, or **One programme** from your Logic Model. From your SDBIP or data–KPI mapping (Modules 3 and 4), choose **4–6 KPIs** you can populate (even with illustrative data for now).

### Step 2: Set up the layout

- **Row 1:** Title, e.g. “Revenue KPI Dashboard — [Municipality]” and **“Data as at [date]”** and **“Updated: monthly.”**
- **Rows 2–3:** One block per KPI. For each: **KPI name** | **Actual** | **Target** | **Status (traffic light)** | **Short note (optional).**

### Step 3: Add traffic lights

Use **conditional formatting** (Excel or Sheets):

- **Green:** Actual ≥ target (or within agreed range).
- **Amber:** Actual between 90% and 99% of target, or “at risk” by your rule.
- **Red:** Actual &lt; 90% of target, or “off track.”

If you do not have a target, use **vs prior period** (e.g. improved / flat / worsened) or **vs benchmark** if you have one. **Document the rule** in a note or a second sheet.

### Step 4: Add one simple chart

Choose **one** chart that fits the theory:

- **Trend:** e.g. collection rate or revenue by month (line chart).
- **Compare:** e.g. actual vs target (bar or bullet).
- **Part of whole:** e.g. revenue by source (bar or stacked bar).

Keep it **simple**: one chart, clear title, labelled axes.

### Step 5: One-sentence insight

At the **bottom** of the page, add one sentence: *“What needs attention this month?”* e.g. “Collection is below target; debtors 90+ days have increased.” This practises the **NLG** idea (AI Application section): turning numbers into a short narrative.
