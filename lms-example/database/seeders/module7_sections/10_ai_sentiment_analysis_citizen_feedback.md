## AI Application: Sentiment Analysis of Citizen Feedback

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Critical — POPI:** Use only **anonymised** feedback. **Remove** all names, ID numbers, contact details, addresses, and any text that could identify a person **before** pasting into ChatGPT or Claude. Use **aggregated** results (counts, themes, %) in reports and dashboards. **Never** put **identifiable** complainant or survey respondent data into public AI.

AI can: (1) **categorise** or **assign themes** to **anonymised** feedback excerpts; (2) **assess sentiment** (positive, neutral, negative) for **anonymised** text; (3) **summarise** recurring **themes** from a **batch** of **anonymised** comments. You **validate** and **approve** all output. This supports your **Citizen Feedback** dashboard and **SDBIP** reporting on satisfaction and complaints.

[STEP] Task A — Categorise and assign themes to anonymised excerpts

### Task A: Categorise and Assign Themes to Anonymised Excerpts

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. **Prepare:** Take 5–10 **short** complaint or feedback **excerpts** from your records. **Anonymise** them: delete names, IDs, phone numbers, exact addresses. Keep only the **gist** (e.g. "Water was off for three days"; "Road has potholes and no one comes"; "Staff were rude when I phoned"). If needed, **generalise** location (e.g. "Ward 3" is OK if it does not identify a person; avoid "house at 123 X Street").
3. Paste the **anonymised** excerpts and send:

   > Below are anonymised municipal service feedback excerpts. For each, give: (1) **Category** (Water, Sanitation, Electricity, Roads, Waste, Billing, Other); (2) **Theme** (1–3 words, e.g. "No water", "Slow response", "Attitude"); (3) **Sentiment** (Positive, Neutral, Negative). Use a table. Do not add or infer personal data.

4. **Use the output:** **Validate** the categories and themes. Use the **themes** to update the "Top themes" in your **Citizen Feedback** dashboard. **Repeat** with new **batches** of **anonymised** feedback periodically. **Never** paste **identifiable** data.

[STEP] Task B — Suggest a taxonomy of themes for your municipality

### Task B: Suggest a Taxonomy of Themes for Your Municipality

1. In a **new chat**, send (edit for your services):

   > We are a South African municipality. We log complaints and feedback for Water, Sanitation, Electricity, Roads, Waste, and Billing. We want a **taxonomy of themes** (a standard list of 3–5 themes per service) so we can consistently categorise feedback. For each service, give: (1) the service; (2) 3–5 theme labels (short, 1–3 words) that cover most complaints we might get; (3) a one-line definition of each. Use a table. We will use this to tag feedback in our system—we will NOT put the actual feedback into AI, only use this as a reference.

2. **Use the output:** **Adapt** with your **customer care** or **performance** team. Use it as the **dropdown** or **pick list** when logging complaints, or when you **manually** or **AI-assisted** assign themes to **anonymised** batches. **Consistency** helps **reporting** and **trends**.

[STEP] Task C — Summarise sentiment and themes from aggregated stats

### Task C: Summarise Sentiment and Themes from Aggregated Stats

1. In a **new chat**, provide only **aggregated** numbers. Example:

   > This quarter we had 200 complaints. By category: Water 80, Roads 50, Billing 40, Other 30. Our manual theme sample (anonymised) showed top themes: "No water" 35%, "Slow response" 25%, "Potholes" 20%, "Billing dispute" 15%, "Attitude" 5%. Satisfaction survey: 65% positive, 20% neutral, 15% negative. Water satisfaction down from last quarter.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the aggregated stats above, write a **one-paragraph summary** for the Municipal Manager and Council on citizen feedback this quarter. Include: (1) main categories and themes; (2) satisfaction; (3) one recommended priority. Use formal language. Do not add or infer. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with your **customer care** or **performance** lead. Use it in **SDBIP** or **annual report** narrative. **AI** drafts from **aggregates**; you **own** the data and the message.
