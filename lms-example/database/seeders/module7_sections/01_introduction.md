# Module 7: Service Delivery Monitoring

Time Slot: 13:30 - 15:00 (Day 2)

## Purpose of This Session

This session applies the **dashboard design**, **KPI**, and **data** skills from Modules 3–5 (and the **financial** monitoring patterns from Module 6) to **service delivery**: water, sanitation, electricity, roads, waste, and other municipal services. **SALGA Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return) and **Outcome 6** (Developmental Growth—quality services) require accessible, affordable, and quality basic services. You will learn **theory** on service delivery standards, infrastructure monitoring, citizen satisfaction, analytics, and benchmarking; **practical** tasks to build a faults-and-resolution tracker and a citizen-feedback dashboard; and **AI application** tasks for infrastructure and predictive maintenance, **sentiment analysis** of citizen feedback (using **anonymised** data only), **service quality scoring**, and **service delivery optimization**.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** service delivery standards: accessibility, affordability, and quality—and how they link to SALGA outcomes, IDP, and SDBIP.
2. **Describe** infrastructure monitoring: asset registers, condition, faults, and maintenance cycles; link to **predictive maintenance** concepts.
3. **Outline** citizen satisfaction measurement: surveys, complaints, and feedback; how to use them in performance reporting.
4. **Apply** service delivery analytics: KPIs, trends, and data sources for water, sanitation, roads, electricity, and waste.
5. **Explain** performance benchmarking: internal and external; how to compare and learn from others.
6. **Build** a faults-and-resolution tracker and a citizen-feedback/satisfaction dashboard in Excel or Google Sheets.
7. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to support **infrastructure and predictive maintenance** rules, **sentiment and theme** analysis of **anonymised** feedback, **service quality scoring** concepts, and **optimization** ideas—with **POPI**-compliant use of data (anonymised only in public AI).

[STEP] How to use this module

## How to Use This Module

1. **Connect to Modules 3, 4, 5, and 6.** Your **Logic Model** and **SDBIP** (Module 3), **data–KPI mapping** (Module 4), **dashboard design** (Module 5), and **financial** patterns (Module 6) apply here: same principles for purpose, audience, traffic lights, and "Data as at."
2. **Work through each step.** Use **Next** and **Previous** to move. Theory sets the concepts; **Practical** and **AI Application** give you outputs you can use in your municipality.
3. **Protect personal data (POPI).** In **AI Application** tasks that use **citizen feedback**, use only **anonymised** text (no names, IDs, or contact details). **Never** put identifiable complainant or respondent data into ChatGPT, Claude, or other public AI. **Aggregated** summaries (e.g. "45% positive, 30% neutral, 25% negative") are safe; raw, identifiable feedback is not.
4. **Validate AI output.** AI can suggest rules, themes, and structures; you and your **Service Delivery** or **Customer Care** lead must validate and adapt for your context.
