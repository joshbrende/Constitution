## Module 7: Key Takeaways

1. **Service delivery standards:** Three dimensions—**accessibility** (can people access the service?), **affordability** (is it affordable?), **quality** (reliable, acceptable?). They link to **IDP**, **SDBIP**, and **SALGA Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return) and **Outcome 6** (Developmental Growth—quality services). Your KPIs should reflect these.

2. **Infrastructure monitoring:** **Asset registers**, **condition**, **faults**, and **maintenance** (reactive, planned, **predictive**) are the building blocks. Key fault KPIs: **volume**, **resolution time**, **backlog** and **ageing** (0–7, 8–30, 31+ days), **repeat faults**. **Predictive maintenance** uses data to schedule repair or inspection **before** failure—start with **rules** (e.g. fault count, age) before advanced analytics. **AI** can suggest **prioritisation** and **"when to inspect"** logic from **aggregated** data; you **implement** in **your** systems.

3. **Citizen satisfaction measurement:** **Surveys**, **complaints**, **compliments**, and **ward/IDP** input. Indicators: **overall satisfaction**, **by service**, **by area**; **complaints volume, type, resolution**; **themes**. **POPI:** Use only **anonymised** feedback and **aggregated** results in **reports** and **public AI**—**never** identifiable complainant or respondent data.

4. **Service delivery analytics:** **KPIs by service** (water, sanitation, electricity, roads, waste): outputs (connections, faults, resolution) and outcomes (access, quality, satisfaction). **Trends** and **root-cause** questions (volume, capacity, asset, process, external) when KPIs are off track. **Data sources** and **refresh** must align with your **data–KPI mapping** (Module 4).

5. **Performance benchmarking:** **Internal** (over time), **target** (SDBIP), **peer** (similar municipalities), **best in class**. Use **like-for-like** definitions. **Benchmarks** can appear in dashboards and "How we compare" narrative in reports. **AI** can **draft** that narrative from **aggregated** figures—you **validate**.

6. **Practical skills:** You can **build a faults and resolution tracker** (summary by service: reported, resolved, % in target, backlog ageing) and a **citizen feedback and satisfaction dashboard** (complaints by category, resolution, themes; satisfaction scores if you have surveys). Both use **aggregated** data; **POPI**-compliant; link to **SDBIP** and your **service delivery** dashboard (Module 5).

7. **AI Application—data and ethics:** For **infrastructure/faults**: use **aggregated** data only (counts, %, ageing by service or area—avoid identifying persons). For **citizen feedback**: **anonymise** strictly—no names, IDs, or contact details in public AI; **themes** and **sentiment** from **anonymised** text or **aggregated** stats only. **Validate** and **approve** all AI output with the **service delivery**, **customer care**, or **performance** owner. **AI supports**; it does **not** replace your fault systems, surveys, or human oversight.
