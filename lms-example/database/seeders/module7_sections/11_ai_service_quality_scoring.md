## AI Application: Service Quality Scoring

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **aggregated** data (e.g. "resolution rate 78%," "satisfaction 3.2/5," "complaints 95"). **Never** put **identifiable** citizen or complainant data into public AI. AI can: (1) **suggest** a **structure** for a **service quality score** (which KPIs to combine and how); (2) **propose** **traffic-light** or **band** rules (Green/Amber/Red) for a KPI or composite score; (3) **draft** a one-paragraph **quality summary** from **aggregated** KPI numbers you provide. You **implement** the score in **your** dashboards and **validate** all rules with the **service delivery** or **performance** owner.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest a composite service quality score structure

### Task A: Suggest a Composite Service Quality Score Structure

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send (edit for your services):

   > We are a South African municipality. We want a **composite service quality score** (e.g. 0–100) for our main services (Water, Sanitation, Electricity, Roads, Waste) to use in a dashboard and SDBIP. We have: resolution rate (%), resolution within target (%), backlog (open and 31+), citizen satisfaction (1–5 or %), and complaints per 1000 connections (or similar). We will NOT put our actual data into AI—we only want the **concept**. Suggest: (1) which 4–6 **input KPIs** to use; (2) how to **weight** them (e.g. resolution 30%, satisfaction 40%); (3) how to **convert** each to a 0–100 scale (e.g. 90% resolution = 90); (4) a formula in words for the **composite**. We will build this in Excel or our BI tool with our own numbers.

2. **Use the output:** **Discuss** with your **performance** and **technical** teams. **Adjust** weights and KPIs to match your **SDBIP** and **data availability**. **Build** the score in **your** tracker or dashboard. **AI** helps you **design**; you **own** the formula and the data.

[STEP] Task B — Propose traffic-light rules for service KPIs

### Task B: Propose Traffic-Light Rules for Service KPIs

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We need **traffic-light rules** (Green / Amber / Red) for these service delivery KPIs: (1) **Resolution within target** (% of faults resolved in e.g. 48h); (2) **Backlog 31+ days** (number or % of open faults older than 31 days); (3) **Citizen satisfaction** (1–5 scale or %). For each KPI, suggest: Green threshold, Amber band, Red threshold, and a one-line reason. Use a table. We will use this in our dashboard—we will plug in our own targets and adjust. We are NOT putting actual data into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Align** with your **SDBIP** targets and **policy**. Use it in your **Faults** and **Citizen Feedback** dashboards (Practical sections). **AI** suggests; you **adopt** and **sign off**.

[STEP] Task C — Draft a one-paragraph quality summary from aggregated KPIs

### Task C: Draft a One-Paragraph Quality Summary from Aggregated KPIs

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** numbers only. Example:

   > Our service quality this month: Water resolution within 48h 78% (target 90%); Water backlog 31+ is 10. Roads resolution 88% (target 85%); Roads 31+ is 2. Satisfaction: Water 3.2/5, Roads 3.6/5, Overall 3.5/5. Complaints: Water 95, Roads 60.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the numbers above, write a **one-paragraph service quality summary** for the Mayoral Committee. Say what is on track, what needs attention, and one priority action. Use formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with the **service delivery** or **MM** office. Use in **reports** or **council** briefings. **AI** drafts; you **own** the data and the final text.
