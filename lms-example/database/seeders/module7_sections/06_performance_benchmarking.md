[STEP] What is performance benchmarking?

## Performance Benchmarking

**Benchmarking** is the process of **comparing** your performance to a **reference**: your own **past** (internal over time), **targets** (budget, SDBIP), or **other municipalities** (external). It helps you: (1) see if you are **improving**; (2) identify **gaps** and **best practice**; (3) **justify** resource or policy changes to council; (4) support **SALGA** and **CoGTA** reporting. **SALGA Outcome 6** (Developmental Growth—quality services) implies learning and **competitiveness**; benchmarking supports that.

### Types of benchmarking

| Type | What you compare | Use |
|------|------------------|-----|
| **Internal over time** | This year vs last; this quarter vs same quarter last year. | Trend; are we improving? |
| **Target** | Actual vs SDBIP target; vs IDP milestone. | Accountability; traffic lights. |
| **Peer / external** | Your municipality vs similar (size, type: metro, district, local) or vs provincial/national averages. | Best practice; where we stand. |
| **Best in class** | Your KPI vs a top performer (often from published reports, SALGA, CoGTA, or sector bodies). | Aspirational; what is possible. |

[STEP] What to benchmark and where to get data

## What to Benchmark and Where to Get Data

- **Service KPIs:** Resolution time, access %, satisfaction, faults per connection, etc. **Sources:** Your data; **CoGTA** or **provincial** reports; **SALGA** or **sector** benchmarks (e.g. water, electricity); **Stats SA**; **Municipal Benchmarking Initiative** or similar if available.
- **Financial (from Module 6):** Collection rate, cost per unit, etc. **Treasury**, **AG**, and **SALGA** sometimes publish comparative data.
- **Cautions:** Ensure **like for like** (same definition, same period). **Context** matters: rural vs urban, bulk vs distribution, so explain differences when you present.

[STEP] Using benchmarks in dashboards and reports

## Using Benchmarks in Dashboards and Reports

- **Dashboard:** Add a **benchmark** column or a **comparator** line on a chart (e.g. "Provincial average" or "Target"). Traffic lights can use **benchmark** as well as **target** (e.g. Green if we are in top quartile).
- **Reports:** One-paragraph "How we compare" for key KPIs in the **annual report** or **SDBIP** review. **AI** can help **draft** that paragraph from **aggregated** figures you provide—you **validate** and **approve**.
