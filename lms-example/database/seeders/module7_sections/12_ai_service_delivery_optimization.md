## AI Application: Service Delivery Optimization

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **aggregated, non-confidential** data. **Never** put **identifiable** citizen or location data that could identify a person into public AI. AI can: (1) **suggest** **prioritisation** criteria when resources are limited (which areas or services to focus on first); (2) **propose** **improvement** ideas or **root-cause** angles from **aggregated** problem descriptions; (3) **draft** a short **optimization** or **action** brief from **summary** KPIs you provide. You **validate** and **implement**; AI **supports** thinking and structure.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest prioritisation criteria when resources are limited

### Task A: Suggest Prioritisation Criteria When Resources Are Limited

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send (edit as needed):

   > We are a South African municipality. We have limited crews and budget for faults and maintenance. We need **prioritisation criteria** to decide: which **service** (water, sanitation, electricity, roads) to focus on first, and which **areas or factors** (e.g. ageing of fault, number of people affected, health/safety, SDBIP target at risk). We will NOT put any location or person data into AI. Give 6–8 criteria in a table: Criterion | What to consider | Example (hypothetical) | Suggested weight (1–5) or comment. We will use this to design our own prioritisation matrix with our own data.

2. **Use the output:** **Adapt** with your **technical** and **planning** teams. Use it to **design** your **scheduling** or **dispatch** rules, or to **brief** council on how you prioritise. **AI** suggests; you **own** the final criteria and weights.

[STEP] Task B — Propose root-cause and improvement angles from aggregated problems

### Task B: Propose Root-Cause and Improvement Angles from Aggregated Problems

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** problem descriptions. Example:

   > Our municipality has: Water resolution 78% vs 90% target; 10 faults 31+ days; complaints up; satisfaction for water down. Roads and electricity on target. We have ageing infrastructure and sometimes parts delays.

2. Then send:

   > Given the aggregated situation above, suggest: (1) **3–4 possible root causes** (e.g. capacity, asset condition, parts, process) with a one-line each; (2) **3–4 improvement ideas** we could consider (short-term and medium-term); (3) **one** suggested **first step** for the next 4 weeks. Use bullets. We will validate with our technical team—we are NOT putting detailed or identifiable data into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Discuss** with **technical**, **planning**, and **performance**. Use it to **populate** an **improvement** or **action** plan. **AI** suggests; you **decide** what to implement.

[STEP] Task C — Draft an optimization or action brief from summary KPIs

### Task C: Draft an Optimization or Action Brief from Summary KPIs

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** numbers only. Example:

   > Service delivery this quarter: Water off track (resolution, backlog, satisfaction). Roads and electricity on track. We have decided to: (1) add one extra water crew for Ward 3; (2) prioritise 31+ faults; (3) run a satisfaction survey in 6 months. Budget for (1) is approved.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the information above, write a **half-page action brief** for the Municipal Manager. Include: (1) current status (2–3 sentences); (2) what we will do (the three actions); (3) how we will measure success (1–2 sentences); (4) one risk or dependency. Use formal language. Do not add. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** and **approve** with the **MM** or **technical** lead. Use it for **internal** or **council** briefing. **AI** drafts; you **own** the content and the commitments.

[STEP] Consolidation — when to use which AI task

## When to Use Which AI Task

| Module 7 area | AI task | What you get | What you must do |
|---------------|---------|--------------|------------------|
| **Infrastructure / faults** | Prioritisation rules; "when to inspect" logic; "what to watch" narrative | Structures, drafts | Implement in your systems; use aggregated data only. |
| **Citizen feedback** | Theme categorisation; taxonomy; sentiment summary | Themes, summaries | **Anonymise** before any text; use only aggregates in AI. **POPI**. |
| **Service quality** | Composite score structure; traffic-light rules; quality summary | Design and narrative | Build in Excel/dashboard; validate with owner. |
| **Optimization** | Prioritisation criteria; root-cause and improvement ideas; action brief | Ideas and drafts | Validate with technical/planning; approve before use. |

**Remember:** AI **supports**; it does **not** replace your **fault systems**, **surveys**, or **human judgment**. Always **validate** and **approve** before use in reports, council, or SDBIP. For **citizen feedback**, **anonymise** strictly—**never** put identifiable data into public AI.
