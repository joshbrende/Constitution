[STEP] Why citizen satisfaction matters

## Citizen Satisfaction Measurement

**Citizen satisfaction** reflects whether residents and businesses perceive services as **accessible**, **affordable**, and **quality**. It is an **outcome** indicator: it tells you whether your **outputs** (connections, faults resolved, etc.) translate into **perceived** improvement. **SALGA Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return) and **Outcome 6** (Developmental Growth) both benefit from evidence on satisfaction. Satisfaction data supports: (1) **IDP** and **SDBIP** reporting; (2) **council** and **oversight** briefings; (3) **service improvement** priorities.

### Sources of data

| Source | What you get | Strengths | Limitations |
|--------|--------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Surveys** | Structured questions; satisfaction score (e.g. 1–5 or %); sometimes themes. | Comparable over time; can be representative. | Cost; timing; possible bias if sample is skewed. |
| **Complaints** | Logged grievances (water, roads, billing, etc.). | Real-time; points to specific problems. | Not representative; only unhappy users may complain. |
| **Compliments / thanks** | Positive feedback. | Balance; good for morale. | Often fewer than complaints. |
| **Social media / unsolicited** | Posts, comments (if you monitor). | Early signal; public. | Not representative; needs careful, ethical handling; POPI. |
| **Ward committee / IDP inputs** | Qualitative input from meetings. | Grounded in local experience. | Not always structured or comparable. |

[STEP] What to measure and how to report

## What to Measure and How to Report

| Indicator | Definition | Use in SDBIP / dashboard |
|----------|------------|-------------------------|
| **Overall satisfaction** | % satisfied or average score (e.g. 1–5) for a service or overall. | KPI; traffic light vs target. |
| **Satisfaction by service** | Water, electricity, roads, waste, etc. | Identify which services need focus. |
| **Satisfaction by ward or area** | Geographic breakdown. | Equity; where to prioritise. |
| **Complaints: volume, type, resolution** | Number; categories (e.g. no water, leak, billing); % resolved in X days. | Operational; link to faults and backlogs. |
| **Themes from feedback** | Recurring topics (e.g. "interruptions," "response time," "attitude"). | **AI** can help **categorise** and **summarise** from **anonymised** text. |

**Reporting:** Use **aggregated** results (%, scores, counts by category) in dashboards and reports. **Never** put **identifiable** complainant or respondent data in public reports or **public AI**—**POPI** requires care.

[STEP] POPI and feedback — anonymisation

## POPI and Feedback: Anonymisation

- **Surveys:** Design so responses can be **analysed** without identifying individuals (aggregate by area, not by name). If you store raw responses, control access and retention per **POPI**.
- **Complaints:** For **internal** use you may need IDs and contact details to resolve. For **analysis**, **reporting**, or **AI**: use **anonymised** extracts (no names, IDs, addresses, or contact details). **Themes** and **counts by category** are safe.
- **AI (ChatGPT, Claude):** Use only **anonymised** feedback text or **aggregated** summaries. **Do not** paste complaints that contain names, ID numbers, or contact details into public AI tools.
