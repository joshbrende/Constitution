[STEP] What we mean by service delivery standards

## Service Delivery Standards

**Service delivery standards** define what "good" looks like for municipal services: water, sanitation, electricity, roads, waste, and other basic services. They support **SALGA Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return—quality basic services) and **Outcome 6** (Developmental Growth—quality services for competitiveness). Standards are usually expressed in your **IDP**, **SDBIP**, **Service Delivery and Budget Implementation Plan**, and sector policies (e.g. water services, waste).

### Three dimensions

| Dimension | What it means | Examples of indicators |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------------|
| **Accessibility** | Can residents and businesses **access** the service? | % households with access to piped water; km of roads in good condition; % with refuse collection. |
| **Affordability** | Is the service **affordable** (tariffs, indirect costs)? | Cost as % of household income; indigent support; payment arrangements. |
| **Quality** | Is the service **reliable** and **acceptable**? | Interruptions per month; water quality compliance; fault resolution time; citizen satisfaction. |

[STEP] Link to IDP, SDBIP, and SALGA

## Link to IDP, SDBIP, and SALGA

- **IDP:** Long-term service delivery objectives (e.g. "Universal access to water by 2030"; "Reduce water losses by X%"). Your **Logic Model** (Module 3) links activities, outputs, and outcomes to these.
- **SDBIP:** Annual **output** and **outcome** KPIs (e.g. "Faults resolved within 48 hours"; "New water connections"; "Citizen satisfaction score"). These are what you **monitor** in dashboards and trackers.
- **SALGA Outcome 5:** Appropriate financial planning; equitable share; revenue collection; **quality basic services**; spatial justice. **Outcome 6:** Conducive business climate; **quality services**; Building Inclusive Green Municipalities (BIGM). Your service delivery KPIs should show progress toward these.

**Data sources:** Billing and technical systems (connections, consumption, faults); asset registers; complaints and feedback; surveys. Your **data–KPI mapping** (Module 4) should show which system feeds which KPI.

[STEP] Setting and reviewing standards

## Setting and Reviewing Standards

- **Policy and norms:** National and provincial norms (e.g. water quality, minimum service levels) set a floor. Your municipality can set **higher** or **more specific** standards (e.g. "Faults in CBD resolved within 24 hours").
- **Stakeholder input:** Ward committees, IDP consultations, and citizen feedback help define what "acceptable" means. **Benchmarking** (later in this module) helps you compare with similar municipalities.
- **Review cycle:** Standards should be reviewed when you **revise the IDP** or **SDBIP**. **AI** can help **structure** standards into indicator tables or **draft** briefs for council—you **validate** and **approve**.
