# Module 4: Data Foundation for AI

Time Slot: 15:15 - 16:45

## Purpose of This Session

In Modules 1–3 you learned the **strategic context** (SALGA 2026), **AI fundamentals** (including ethics and POPI), and the **performance management framework** (Theory of Change, Logic Models, indicators, golden thread). Those frameworks define *what* to measure. This module answers: **Is your data ready to measure it?** AI and dashboards can only be as good as the data they use. Poor-quality, siloed, or missing data leads to wrong decisions, failed AI models, and audit risk. This session gives you **theory** on data quality, governance, collection, integration, and “big data” in a municipal setting; **practical** tasks to build a data inventory and map your KPIs to data sources; and **AI application** tasks to assess quality, catalog data, and generate cleaning and validation rules.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** what data quality and data governance mean and why they matter for AI and performance management.
2. **Describe** common data collection methods and systems in municipalities (billing, assets, complaints, IDP/SDBIP, HR, projects).
3. **Identify** typical data integration challenges: silos, formats, legacy systems, ownership, and POPI.
4. **Interpret** “big data” in a municipal context—volume, variety, velocity—and what “AI-ready” means in practice.
5. **Apply** Data-as-a-Service and data-readiness concepts to prioritise which data to improve first.
6. **Build** a data inventory and a data quality checklist for one area of your municipality.
7. **Map** your KPIs (from Module 3) to the data sources that feed them and identify gaps.
8. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to assess data quality, generate data catalogs and discovery prompts, and create data-cleaning and validation rules.

[STEP] How to use this module

## How to Use This Module

1. **Connect to Module 3.** Your Logic Models and KPIs define *what* to measure. Here we focus on *where the data comes from* and *whether it is good enough*. Have your KPI list or linkage table to hand.
2. **Work through each step.** Use **Next** and **Previous** to move. Theory sets the concepts; **Practical** and **AI Application** give you tools you can use immediately.
3. **Be honest about gaps.** Many municipalities have siloed, paper-based, or inconsistent data. The inventory and KPI–data mapping will surface gaps—that is the first step to fixing them.
4. **Validate AI output.** AI can suggest quality rules, catalog structures, and validation checks; you and your IT or data owner must validate and apply them.

**Next:** Module 4: Data Quality and Governance — dimensions of quality, governance roles, and why they matter for AI.
