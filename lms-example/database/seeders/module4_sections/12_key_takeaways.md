## Module 4: Key Takeaways

1. **Data quality** means data is fit for purpose: accurate, complete, timely, consistent, valid, and unique. Poor-quality data leads to wrong KPIs, misleading AI, and audit risk. Prioritise by impact on your SDBIP, audits, and high-value decisions.

2. **Data governance** assigns **owners** and **stewards** to datasets. IT stores and secures; the **business owns** meaning and quality. POPI applies when data contains personal information—govern how it is collected, used, and shared, including for AI.

3. **Data collection** in municipalities comes from billing, assets, complaints, IDP/SDBIP, HR, projects, and council. Mix of **manual** (paper, Excel) and **automated** (systems, meters, sensors). Automated, structured data is easier for AI and dashboards; improve collection where it matters most.

4. **Data integration** is hard: **silos**, **inconsistent formats**, **legacy systems**, **ownership** disputes, and **POPI**. Document what exists, agree on standards and owners, and prioritise integration for the data that feeds your priority KPIs.

5. **“Big data”** in a municipal context is about **volume, variety, velocity**—but often the bottleneck is **quality** and **integration**, not raw size. Many use cases can start with Excel or simple DBs if data is clean and well defined.

6. **Data readiness for AI** means: structured data with clear definitions for analytics; documents in consistent form for document AI; historical data with complete key fields for predictive models; anonymised or aggregated text for sentiment if using public AI. **Prioritise** by KPI impact and feasibility.

7. **Practical skills:** You can build a **data inventory** (what exists, where, who owns it, quality) and a **data source–KPI mapping** (which data feeds which KPI; Green/Amber/Red gaps). These support governance, audit readiness, and AI readiness.

8. **AI Application:** You can use **ChatGPT** or **Claude** to: assess data quality from a description; generate quality rules for a KPI; create data catalog and data dictionary templates; discover what data you need for a use case; generate cleaning steps and validation rules for Excel/Sheets; and design a simple data quality scorecard. **Do not put personal data** into public AI; use anonymised or generic descriptions. Always **validate** AI output with your data owner or IT.
