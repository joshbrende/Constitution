[STEP] Financial and service delivery challenges — and track debt indicators in a spreadsheet

## Financial management challenges

**The numbers:**
- **R306.7 billion** in municipal debt (Sept 2023)
- **64% of outstanding debt** is irrecoverable
- **213 days** average collection period (target: under 90)
- **9.1%** of nationally raised revenue vs **46%** of constitutional functions — chronic underfunding

**Impact:** Cash flow problems; inability to maintain infrastructure; service failures; credit downgrades.

## Service delivery challenges

**The situation:** ageing water, sanitation, electricity; missing or poor services in many communities; big gaps between urban and rural; not enough technical and planning capacity.

**Impact:** Citizen dissatisfaction; health and safety risks; economic stagnation.

---

## Hands-on: Top 5 debt and revenue indicators in Excel or Google Sheets

**Tool:** **Google Sheets** (sheets.google.com) or **Microsoft Excel**

1. Open **Sheets** or **Excel**, new workbook. Name the sheet **Debt & revenue**.
2. Create a table with columns: `Indicator` | `Our figure` | `Target` | `Source` | `Updated`.
3. In the **Indicator** column, add:
   - Total debt (R)
   - Debt older than 90 days (R or %)
   - Collection rate (%)
   - Collection period (days)
   - Cost to collect (as % of revenue, if you have it)
4. In **Source**, write where you get the number (e.g. "Budget report", "CFO", "Not yet reported").
5. **Use this:** Fill in what you can. Gaps show what to start measuring. In later modules we will use AI to predict and prioritise.

[STEP] Governance and capacity challenges — and a compliance checklist from ChatGPT or Claude

## Governance and compliance challenges

**The issues:** Non-compliance with MFMA, Labour Relations Act; irregular expenditure and procurement; political interference; resistance to oversight.

**Impact:** Audit findings; loss of trust; legal risk; reduced transfers.

## Capacity and skills challenges

**The issues:** Lack of big data and analytical skills; high councillor turnover; under-capacitated MPACs; knowledge loss.

**Impact:** Poor decisions; inefficiency; unable to use technology well.

---

## Hands-on: Generate a compliance and capacity checklist

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. Give us two checklists. (1) COMPLIANCE: 8 short yes/no questions to see if we are on track with MFMA, procurement, and audit readiness (e.g. "Are all S56 managers on performance agreements?"). (2) CAPACITY: 6 short yes/no questions to see if we have the basics for data and performance (e.g. "Do we have a single list of our key performance indicators?"). For each question, add one line on why it matters. Use clear headings and bullets.

3. **Use the output:** Print or copy into a doc. In a team meeting, go through and answer yes/no. The "no" answers are priorities for your risk register or capacity plan. You can repeat with ChatGPT later to tailor more questions.

## Use the challenges as a lens

You do not need to fix everything at once. Use the four areas — **Financial**, **Service delivery**, **Governance**, **Capacity** — to decide where AI and performance management can add the most value first.

## Hands-on: Quick prioritisation

1. Look at the four challenge areas: Financial; Service delivery; Governance; Capacity.
2. For your municipality, rank them 1–4 (1 = most urgent to address in the next 12 months).
3. For the **#1** area, write one sentence: *The one thing we will start measuring or improving in the next 3 months is …*
4. **Use this:** Share the sentence with your manager or in your next planning meeting. In later modules we will link this to specific AI and performance tools.
