## Alignment with national priorities (MTSF)

Municipal performance must align with the Medium-Term Strategic Framework (MTSF). These are the priorities that matter for local government:

### Priority 1: A Capable, Ethical and Developmental State
- Better leadership, governance, accountability
- Functional, efficient, integrated government
- Professional, merit-based, ethical public administration

### Priority 2: Economic Transformation and Job Creation
- Competitiveness through ICT and innovation
- More decent jobs

### Priority 4: Consolidating the Social Wage — Reliable and Quality Basic Services
- Community development
- Improved access to basic services

### Priority 5: Spatial Integration, Human Settlements and Local Government
- Basic services; shared spatial vision; sub-national development; climate readiness

### Priority 6: Social Cohesion and Safer Communities
- Active citizenry; better corruption perception; constitutional values

Your IDP, SDBIP, and APP should show how your municipality contributes to these. AI and performance systems we build later will help you track and report on that alignment.

## Turn MTSF into a KPI map

We need to go from "national says basic services" to "we measure X and Y in our municipality". AI can help you draft a first map; you then validate and adjust.

---

## Hands-on: Map MTSF priorities to municipal KPIs

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send this prompt (you can add your municipality name or type: metro, district, local):

   > We are a [type] municipality in South Africa. Map the MTSF priorities (1: Capable state, 2: Economic transformation, 4: Social wage / basic services, 5: Spatial integration / human settlements, 6: Social cohesion) to municipal-level KPIs. For each MTSF priority, give: (1) the priority name, (2) two example KPIs we could put in our IDP or SDBIP, and (3) where we typically get the data (e.g. "Stats from billing", "Auditor-General", "Community survey"). Use a table with 4 columns. Focus on things municipalities actually measure or could measure.

3. **Use the output:** Copy the table. In your next planning cycle, check: which of these KPIs do we already have? Which are missing? The table is a starting point — your IDP coordinator and M&E unit should refine it for your context.
