## Module 6: Key Takeaways

1. **Revenue collection optimization:** Key indicators are **collection rate**, **collection period**, and **cost to collect**. They link to **SALGA Outcome 5** and **MFMA**. Levers include billing accuracy, payment convenience, ageing-based follow-up, disconnection and legal (per policy), and indigent/arrears management. **AI** can suggest leading indicators, escalation rules, and draft briefings—use **aggregated** data only.

2. **Budget variance analysis:** **Favourable** (revenue &gt; budget; expenditure &lt; budget) vs **adverse** (opposite). Key line items: property rates, services, grants (revenue); employee costs, bulk, contracts, repairs (expenditure). **MFMA Section 71/72** require explanation of material variances. A **variance worksheet** aligned with your reporting structure supports both internal management and **AI-drafted** narratives—you **validate** and **approve**.

3. **Debt management strategies:** **Ageing** (current, 30, 60, 90+ days) and **segmentation** (type, value, can-pay vs indigent) help prioritise. Collection actions escalate per policy: reminder → arrangement → legal → write-off. **Write-off policy** must be formal and approved. **AI** can help design **segmentation frameworks**, **prioritisation scores**, and **write-off checklists**—implementation is in **your** systems with **your** data.

4. **Fraud detection and prevention:** Red flags in **procurement** (split orders, single bid, price anomalies, linked directors); **revenue** (write-offs, adjustments, under-billing); **payroll** (ghost employees, unusual overtime, duplicate accounts). **Internal controls**, segregation of duties, and **whistle-blower** protection are essential. **AI** can generate **red-flag checklists** for training and control design—**humans** investigate and act; **never** put live or personal data into public AI.

5. **Financial forecasting:** **Trend-based** and **driver-based** approaches; **scenarios** (base, upside, downside) and **sensitivity** for key assumptions. **AI** can suggest **driver structures**, **scenario tables**, and **draft forecast summaries** from **aggregated** numbers—you **build** the forecast in Excel or your system and **own** the assumptions and approval.

6. **Practical skills:** You can **build a revenue and debt tracker** (Revenue sheet: billed, collected, rate, period; Debt sheet: ageing, 90+ %, irrecoverable, actions) and a **budget variance worksheet** (revenue and expenditure vs budget, material variances, brief reasons). Both align with **SDBIP**, **Section 71/72**, and your **financial dashboard** (Module 5).

7. **AI Application—data and ethics:** Use only **aggregated, non-confidential** data in ChatGPT, Claude, or other public AI. **Never** put debtor names, account numbers, ID numbers, or confidential contract details into public AI. **POPI** and **MFMA** require care. **Validate** and **approve** all AI output with the **CFO**, **Revenue Manager**, or **Budget Owner** before use in reports, council, or MFMA submissions. **AI supports**; it does **not** replace systems, policies, or human oversight. **Ethics** in financial governance (accountability, transparency, human oversight, fraud prevention) and in AI use are explicit in this module (SAQA-aligned for governance and finance-related learning).
