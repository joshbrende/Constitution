## Practical: Budget Variance Worksheet

**Tools:** **Google Sheets** or **Microsoft Excel**.

This worksheet supports **budget variance analysis** (Module 6 theory) and aligns with **MFMA Section 71/72** reporting. It helps you **explain** variances to council, management, and auditors. In the **AI Application** section you will use ChatGPT or Claude to **draft** variance explanations from the numbers you put here—using **aggregated** figures only.

### Step 1: Set up the structure

1. Open **Google Sheets** or **Excel** (you can add a new sheet to your Revenue & Debt workbook or create a new one).
2. Name the sheet **Budget Variance**.
3. **Row 1:** Title: "Budget Variance — [Municipality] — [Month/Quarter] — Data as at [date]".

### Step 2: Revenue variance table

- **Rows 2–3:** Headers. Use a structure that matches your **Section 71** or **SDBIP** revenue lines. Example:

  | Revenue line | Budget YTD (R) | Actual YTD (R) | Variance (R) | Variance % | Fav/Adv | Brief reason |
  |--------------|----------------|----------------|--------------|------------|---------|--------------|

- **Rows 4–10:** Add your main revenue lines, e.g.:
  - Property rates
  - Water
  - Electricity
  - Sanitation / Refuse
  - Equitable share and grants
  - Other
  - **Total revenue**

- **Variance (R)** = Actual – Budget. **Variance %** = (Variance ÷ Budget) × 100. **Fav/Adv:** Favourable (F) if revenue actual &gt; budget; Adverse (A) if actual &lt; budget.
- **Brief reason:** One short phrase (e.g. "Lower consumption; tariff increase delayed", "Grant received in prior month"). You will expand this into a **narrative** in the AI Application.

### Step 3: Expenditure variance table

- Same layout for **expenditure**:

  | Expenditure line | Budget YTD (R) | Actual YTD (R) | Variance (R) | Variance % | Fav/Adv | Brief reason |
  |------------------|----------------|----------------|--------------|------------|---------|--------------|

- **Rows:** Main votes or programmes—e.g. Employee costs, Bulk electricity, Bulk water, Contracted services, Repairs and maintenance, Finance costs, Other. **Total expenditure.**

- **Fav/Adv for expenditure:** Favourable (F) when Actual &lt; Budget (under-spend); Adverse (A) when Actual &gt; Budget (over-spend). As noted in the theory, under-spend is not always good (e.g. delayed projects).

### Step 4: Surplus / deficit and material variances

- **Surplus/Deficit:** Total Revenue – Total Expenditure. Compare Budget vs Actual.
- **Material variances:** In a separate section or table, list the **top 3–5** material variances (by rand or %) that need a **fuller explanation** for Section 71/72 or council. For each, add:
  - **Line item**
  - **Variance (R) and %**
  - **Root cause** (volume, price, timing, one-off)
  - **Action** (what you will do or have done)
  - **Revised estimate** (if you are doing Section 72 and need to adjust full-year)

[STEP] Narrative and handover to AI

### Step 5: Narrative and handover to AI

- In the **AI Application** section you will **paste** (1) the **material variance** lines and (2) your **brief reasons** into ChatGPT or Claude and ask it to **draft a one-paragraph explanation** for each. You will **edit** and **validate** the output—the AI helps you structure the narrative; the **CFO or budget owner** signs off.
- **Important:** Use only **aggregated** numbers. Do **not** put confidential contract details or person-specific data into public AI.

### Step 6: Owner and refresh

- **Owner:** Budget Manager or CFO. **Refresh:** Monthly or per your Section 71 cycle.
- **Link to dashboard:** The **material** variance lines can become KPIs on your **Executive** or **Finance** dashboard (Module 5) with traffic lights (e.g. Red if adverse and material).

## Example: Budget Variance (Illustrative)

| Revenue line | Budget YTD | Actual YTD | Variance (R) | Var % | F/A | Brief reason |
|--------------|------------|------------|--------------|-------|-----|--------------|
| Property rates | 50 000 | 48 200 | –1 800 | –3.6 | A | Collection below target. |
| Water | 22 000 | 21 500 | –500 | –2.3 | A | Volume down; tariff on track. |
| **Total revenue** | **85 000** | **82 100** | **–2 900** | **–3.4** | **A** | |

| Expenditure line | Budget YTD | Actual YTD | Variance (R) | Var % | F/A | Brief reason |
|------------------|------------|------------|--------------|-------|-----|--------------|
| Employee costs | 40 000 | 41 200 | 1 200 | 3.0 | A | Overtime; settlement. |
| Bulk electricity | 18 000 | 17 200 | –800 | –4.4 | F | Volume lower; price stable. |
| **Total expenditure** | **78 000** | **77 500** | **–500** | **–0.6** | **F** | |

**Surplus:** Budget 7 000; Actual 4 600; **Variance –2 400 (adverse).**

**Material variances for narrative:** (1) Revenue: property rates –3.6% (collection); (2) Expenditure: employee costs +3% (overtime and settlement).

### Use this

- **In your municipality:** Use your **actual** vote/programme structure and numbers. Align with **Section 71** and **Section 72** formats. The **narrative** you produce (with or without AI) should be usable in the **Monthly Report** or **In-Year Report**.
- **For AI Application:** You will take the **material** lines and **brief reasons** and ask AI to **draft** a concise, professional explanation. You **verify** and **approve** before it goes to council or Treasury.
