## AI Application: Fraud Red Flags and Variance Explanations

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** For **fraud**, use AI to **generate checklists and rule ideas**—not to process **live transactions** or **personal data**. For **variance**, use only **aggregated** numbers (e.g. "Property rates –3.6%", "Employee costs +3%"). **Never** put confidential contract or person-specific data into public AI.

[STEP] Task A — Fraud red-flag checklist for procurement

### Task A: Fraud Red-Flag Checklist for Procurement

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want a **red-flag checklist** for our procurement and supply chain team to use when reviewing tenders and contracts. Focus on MFMA and SCM best practice. Include 10–12 red flags in these areas: (1) split orders or repeated awards just below threshold; (2) single-source or limited bidding without clear justification; (3) bid or contract anomalies (prices, delivery, specifications); (4) supplier and director links (potential conflicts); (5) variations and changes after award. For each red flag: (1) what to look for, (2) one example of a suspicious pattern, (3) recommended action (e.g. query, escalate to internal audit). Use a table. We will use this to train staff and to design our own internal checks—we will NOT put live procurement data into AI.

3. **Use the output:** **Validate** with your **SCM**, **Internal Audit**, or **Risk** unit. Adapt to your **delegations** and **thresholds**. Use it in **training** and as a **reference** when designing **rule-based alerts** in your systems. **AI** suggests; **humans** investigate and act.

[STEP] Task B — Fraud red-flag checklist for revenue and payroll

### Task B: Fraud Red-Flag Checklist for Revenue and Payroll

1. In a **new chat**, send:

   > We are a South African municipality. We want two short **red-flag checklists**. (1) REVENUE: 5–6 red flags—e.g. write-offs or reversals to specific accounts, unusual patterns in discounts or adjustments, cash handling. For each: what to look for and one suggested control or action. (2) PAYROLL: 5–6 red flags—e.g. ghost employees, unusual overtime, duplicate accounts, changes to banking details. For each: what to look for and one suggested control or action. Use clear headings and bullets. We will use this for awareness and to design internal checks—we will NOT put any personal or account-level data into AI.

2. **Use the output:** Share with **Revenue**, **HR**, and **Internal Audit**. Use as a **training** and **control design** aid. **AI** does not access your HR or billing data—it only helps you **list** what to look for.

[STEP] Task C — Automated variance explanation (draft narrative)

### Task C: Automated Variance Explanation (Draft Narrative)

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** variance information only. Example:

   > We need to explain the following budget variances for our monthly report. Revenue: Property rates YTD is 3.6% below budget—main cause is collection; Water is 2.3% below—volume down. Expenditure: Employee costs 3% over budget—overtime and wage settlement; Bulk electricity 4.4% under—volume lower. Overall surplus is R2.4m below budget.

2. Then send:

   > Turn the variance points above into a **one-paragraph narrative** for the Municipal Manager and Council. Use formal, professional language. For each material item, include: the line, the variance direction and approximate size, and a short, neutral explanation (volume, price, timing, or one-off). End with one sentence on the overall surplus and what management is doing. Do not add information we did not provide. We will verify and approve before use.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** for **accuracy** and **tone**. The **CFO** or **Budget Manager** must **approve** before it goes into the Section 71/72 or council report. **AI** drafts; **you** own the final text. You can **repeat** this each month with that month’s **aggregated** figures—never with **confidential** detail.
