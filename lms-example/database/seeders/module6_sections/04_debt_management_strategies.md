[STEP] The scale of municipal debt

## Debt Management Strategies

**Municipal debt** (consumer debt—what households and businesses owe for rates and services) stood at **R306.7 billion** (Sept 2023). About **64%** is considered **irrecoverable**. The **average collection period** is **213 days** (target under 90). Effective debt management is therefore central to SALGA Outcome 5 and to financial sustainability.

### Why debt grows

- **Non-payment** — Economic hardship, disputes, or willingness to delay.
- **Billing and system failures** — Wrong accounts, no follow-up, poor ageing.
- **Indigent and policy** — Legitimate exclusions or write-offs; can be abused if not controlled.
- **Legal and capacity** — Slow legal process; shortage of staff to follow up at scale.

[STEP] Debt ageing and segmentation

## Debt Ageing and Segmentation

**Ageing** means grouping debt by how old it is (e.g. current, 30, 60, 90, 180+ days). The older the debt, the harder it is to collect. **Segmentation** means grouping debtors by characteristics so you can prioritise:

| Segment | Typical action | AI / data angle |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| **Current / 30 days** | Reminder; ensure billing correct. | Low priority for heavy intervention. |
| **60–90 days, can pay** | Payment arrangement; stricter follow-up. | **AI:** Predict "can pay" vs "cannot pay" to target arrangements. |
| **90+ days, high value** | Prioritise for legal, disconnection, or arrangements. | **AI:** Rank by value × probability of collection. |
| **Indigent / in arrangement** | Per policy; do not disconnect if compliant. | Data: flag so follow-up avoids them. |
| **Irrecoverable** | Write-off per policy; possible handover to debt collection. | **AI:** Predict which accounts are likely irrecoverable to focus write-off and recovery efforts. |

[STEP] Collection actions and write-off policy

## Collection Actions and Write-Off Policy

**Collection actions** (in order of escalation, per your policy):

1. **Reminder** (SMS, letter, email)
2. **Final demand**
3. **Restriction / disconnection** (where permitted and policy-compliant)
4. **Payment arrangement**
5. **Legal** (summons, judgment, emolument attachment, etc.)
6. **Handover to external collection / write-off**

**Write-off policy:** MFMA and municipal policy require a formal **write-off policy** for irrecoverable debt. It should define: (a) when debt is considered irrecoverable; (b) who may approve write-off; (c) thresholds and reporting. **AI** can help **prioritise** which accounts to assess for write-off by predicting likelihood of recovery, but the **decision** remains with the authorised official.
