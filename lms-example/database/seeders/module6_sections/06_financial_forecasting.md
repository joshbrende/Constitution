[STEP] Why municipalities need financial forecasting

## Financial Forecasting

**Financial forecasting** is the process of estimating **future** revenue and expenditure to support: (1) **in-year** reallocations and Section 72 revisions; (2) **next year’s budget**; (3) **medium-term** planning and borrowing; (4) **scenario planning** (e.g. economic downturn, tariff changes). SALGA Outcome 5 and MFMA expect credible, substantiated revenue and expenditure estimates.

### Types of forecast

| Type | Horizon | Typical use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **In-year / rolling** | Rest of current year | Section 72; reallocation; cash flow. |
| **Budget year** | Next 12 months | Annual budget; tabled to council. |
| **MTREF** | 3 years (Medium-Term Revenue and Expenditure Framework) | Multi-year budget; borrowing; IDP. |

[STEP] Trend-based and driver-based approaches

## Trend-Based and Driver-Based Approaches

**Trend-based:** Use **historical** revenue or expenditure (monthly, annual) and project forward—e.g. simple growth rate, or seasonal pattern. Good when the **driver** is hard to measure (e.g. "other income") or as a **sanity check**.

**Driver-based:** Link the forecast to **underlying drivers**:

- **Revenue:** Property rates = valuation × tariff × collection rate; water = connections × consumption × tariff × collection. Volume, tariff, and collection can be forecast separately.
- **Expenditure:** Bulk electricity = bulk price × bulk volume; salaries = headcount × average cost × settlement.

**AI** can help: (1) **suggest** drivers from your data descriptions; (2) **draft** a simple trend or growth scenario from past numbers you provide (aggregated); (3) **generate** a narrative or assumptions table for a forecast. You must **validate** all assumptions and outputs.

[STEP] Scenarios and sensitivity

## Scenarios and Sensitivity

**Scenarios** help you prepare for different futures:

- **Base:** Most likely (e.g. current tariffs, moderate collection).
- **Upside:** Better collection; grant increase; cost savings.
- **Downside:** Economic slump; lower collection; bulk price increase.

**Sensitivity:** Change **one** input (e.g. collection rate –5%) and see the impact on revenue or surplus. Shows which assumptions matter most.

**AI application:** You can ask ChatGPT or Claude to **draft** a scenario table (e.g. "If collection rate is 70%, 75%, 80%, what is the revenue impact?") or **suggest** assumptions for base/upside/downside. Use **aggregated** figures only; you plug in your real numbers and own the final forecast.
