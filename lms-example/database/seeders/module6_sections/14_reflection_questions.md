## Module 6: Reflection Questions

Before proceeding to the Knowledge Check, consider:

1. **Revenue and collection:** What is your municipality’s **collection rate** and **collection period**? How do they compare to the 75% and 90-day targets? What **one** change would have the biggest impact in the next 6 months—and do you have the **data** to measure it?

2. **Budget variance:** Does your municipality routinely produce a **variance report** (revenue and expenditure vs budget) that aligns with **Section 71/72**? Who owns it? Have you ever used a **narrative** to explain material variances—and could **AI** help you **draft** that narrative from **aggregated** numbers?

3. **Debt and prioritisation:** How is **debt** currently **segmented** (ageing, type, value)? Is there a clear **prioritisation** for who gets reminder, arrangement, legal, or write-off? Would a **segmentation framework** or **prioritisation score** (designed with AI, implemented in your system) help? Is your **write-off policy** up to date and consistently applied?

4. **Fraud and red flags:** Have you or your colleagues had **training** on **procurement**, **revenue**, or **payroll** red flags? Would a **checklist** (like the one from the AI task) help in your SCM, Revenue, or HR reviews? Who is responsible for **internal controls** and **escalation** when something looks wrong?

5. **Forecasting:** How does your municipality produce **revenue** and **expenditure** forecasts for the **budget** and **MTREF**? Are the **drivers** (e.g. collection rate, tariff, bulk price) explicit? Would **scenario** or **sensitivity** analysis strengthen your case to council or Treasury—and could **AI** help you **structure** the assumptions table or a **draft** summary?

6. **AI and data ethics:** For the **AI Application** tasks you tried: did you use **only aggregated** data? Would you be comfortable **validating** and **approving** AI-drafted briefings or variance explanations before they go to the MM or council? What would you need (e.g. CFO sign-off, template) to make that routine?
