## Revenue Collection Optimization

**Revenue collection** is the process of billing for municipal services (property rates, water, electricity, sanitation, refuse) and collecting what is due. **Optimization** means improving the **collection rate**, shortening the **collection period**, and managing the **cost to collect** so that cash flow supports service delivery and debt is kept under control. This is core to **SALGA Outcome 5** (Inclusive Socio-Economic Return): effective revenue collection, equitable share advocacy, and elimination of fiscal leakages.

### Key indicators

| Indicator | Definition | Good practice (municipal) | Why it matters |
|-----------|------------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **Collection rate** | Cash collected ÷ billed (or billed minus provisions) in a period, as % | 85–95%+; many municipalities are 60–75% | Directly affects cash flow and ability to fund operations. |
| **Collection period (days)** | Average time from billing to payment (or: debtors ÷ average daily revenue) | Under 90 days; SA average ~213 days | Long period = cash tied up; worsens irrecoverable debt. |
| **Cost to collect** | Cost of billing, meter reading, disconnections, legal, etc. as % of revenue | 3–8% typical; high = inefficient or high bad debt | Must be justified by improved collection. |
| **Billing accuracy** | % of accounts billed correctly (no under/over) | 95%+ | Errors cause disputes, non-payment, and write-offs. |

## Link to MFMA, SDBIP, and Dashboards

- **MFMA:** Section 65 (treasury regulations), Section 71 (monthly reports), and municipal budget processes require credible revenue estimates and disclosure of collection performance. Poor collection undermines the budget and can trigger credit-rating and grant conditions.
- **SDBIP:** Revenue and collection KPIs (e.g. collection rate, collection period, debtors 90+ days) are commonly in the CFO’s or Revenue Manager’s scorecard. Your **dashboard** (Module 5) should surface these with traffic lights and "Data as at" dates.
- **Data sources:** Billing system (billed, collected, ageing); bank statements; debtors ledger. Your **data–KPI mapping** (Module 4) should show which system feeds which KPI and how often.

## Levers to Optimize Collection

| Lever | What you can do | AI / data angle |
|-------|-----------------|------------------|
| **Billing accuracy** | Fix meter-read errors, correct tariffs, reduce disputed accounts. | Data quality checks; AI to flag anomalies in consumption or billing patterns. |
| **Payment convenience** | More pay points, online, debit orders, pre-paid. | Data on payment channels and timing; segment by channel to prioritise. |
| **Ageing and follow-up** | Segment debtors by age (30, 60, 90+ days) and value; target high-value, "can pay" first. | **AI:** Predict probability of payment; prioritise accounts for follow-up. |
| **Disconnection and legal** | Policy-based disconnection and legal action for persistent default. | Ensure fairness (indigent, payment agreements); use data to avoid disconnecting those in arrangements. |
| **Indigent and arrears** | Indigent register, payment arrangements, write-off policy for irrecoverable. | Data to segment indigent vs can-pay; **AI** to suggest which arrears are worth pursuing. |
