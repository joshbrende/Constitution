[STEP] Why fraud matters in municipalities

## Fraud Detection and Prevention

**Fraud** in the municipal context includes: **corruption** (bribery, kickbacks); **asset misappropriation** (theft of cash, inventory, misuse of assets); **financial statement fraud** (manipulation of reports); and **procurement fraud** (rigged bids, split orders, favouritism). MFMA, the **Municipal Finance Management Act**, and supply chain management (SCM) regulations require internal controls, segregation of duties, and consequence management. SALGA Outcome 5 refers to **eliminating fiscal leakages**—fraud is a major leakage.

### Impact

- **Financial:** Direct loss; cost of investigation and litigation.
- **Reputational:** Loss of trust; credit-rating and investor impact.
- **Service delivery:** Resources diverted from communities.
- **Compliance:** Audit findings; possible criminal and disciplinary action.

[STEP] Red flags — procurement and SCM

## Red Flags: Procurement and SCM

| Red flag | What to look for | Possible action |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Split orders** | Same supplier; similar items; just below threshold; same period. | Query and data analysis; tighten approval limits. |
| **Single / limited bids** | Repeated use without clear justification. | Require documented justification; rotate panel. |
| **Short delivery periods** | Tender allows only one bidder to meet. | Standardise realistic periods. |
| **Price anomalies** | Same item much higher than market or prior. | Benchmark; question. |
| **Same directors / addresses** | Different "companies" with links. | Due diligence; blacklist if fraud. |
| **Change orders** | Large increases post-award without proper approval. | Enforce variation and approval policy. |

**AI:** Can help **screen** transactions for patterns (e.g. repeated same-supplier, same-amount) and **flag** outliers for human review. Rules can be encoded from known red flags.

[STEP] Red flags — payroll and revenue

## Red Flags: Payroll and Revenue

**Payroll:**

- Ghost employees (no physical presence, duplicate ID).
- Overtime or allowances disproportionate to role or roster.
- Salary level inconsistent with job; frequent changes.
- Bank account changes; duplicate accounts.

**Revenue:**

- **Write-offs or discounts** to connected parties or without approval.
- **Adjustments** (reversals, credit notes) that favour specific accounts.
- **Meter tampering** or systematic under-billing for a segment.
- **Cash** not banked or banked late; differences between receipts and bank.

**Internal controls:** Segregation of duties (who raises, approves, pays); reconciliation (bank, receipts, debtors); rotation and leave for key staff; and **whistle-blower** protection. **AI** can help by **flagging** outliers (e.g. unusual write-off amounts, unusual overtime) for review—it does not replace controls or human judgment.

[STEP] Fraud and AI — rules, not replacement

## Fraud and AI: Rules and Human Oversight

- **Rule-based alerts:** Define thresholds (e.g. write-off above R X; order split at R Y). Systems or spreadsheets can flag. **AI** can suggest **additional rules** from red-flag lists.
- **Anomaly detection:** Statistical or ML models can flag **unusual** transactions (e.g. supplier never used before; spike in adjustments). These are **alerts for investigation**, not proof of fraud.
- **Human in the loop:** All flags must be **reviewed** by authorised staff. AI supports; it does not replace internal audit, forensic work, or discipline.
