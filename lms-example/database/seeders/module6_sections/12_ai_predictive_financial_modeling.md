## AI Application: Predictive Financial Modeling

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

**Important:** Use only **aggregated, non-confidential** numbers (e.g. "revenue was R Xm last year", "collection rate 72%"). **Never** put detailed transaction or person-specific data into public AI. AI can: (1) **suggest** forecast **structures** and **drivers**; (2) **draft** assumption tables and scenario frameworks; (3) **generate** short narrative or briefing text from **summary** figures you provide. **You** own the **final forecast** and **budget**; AI supports structure and wording.

[STEP] Task A — Suggest revenue forecast drivers and structure

### Task A: Suggest Revenue Forecast Drivers and Structure

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Send (edit with your municipality type):

   > We are a [metro/district/local] municipality in South Africa. We need to prepare a **revenue forecast** for the next 12 months for our budget. We have: property rates, water, electricity, sanitation/refuse, equitable share and grants, and other. For each **revenue stream**, suggest: (1) the main **driver(s)** we should use (e.g. for rates: valuation × tariff × collection rate; for water: connections × consumption × tariff × collection); (2) one or two **assumptions** we need to estimate (e.g. collection rate, bulk price); (3) where we typically get the data. Use a table with columns: Revenue stream | Main driver(s) | Key assumptions to estimate | Data source. We will build the actual forecast in Excel or our budget system with our own numbers.

3. **Use the output:** Check against your **budget** and **Section 71** structure. Use it to **design** your **revenue** forecast model or to **brief** the person who builds it. **AI** does not see your real data—it suggests a **logic** you can implement.

[STEP] Task B — Draft a scenario table (base / upside / downside)

### Task B: Draft a Scenario Table (Base / Upside / Downside)

1. In a **new chat**, send (you can add your own high-level figures if you wish, but only aggregated):

   > We are a South African municipality. We want a **scenario table** for our revenue forecast: Base, Upside, and Downside. For each scenario, we need 4–5 **assumption rows** that would change across scenarios—e.g. collection rate, growth in billed amounts, equitable share change. For each assumption, give: (1) a plausible **Base** value, (2) **Upside** (better), (3) **Downside** (worse), and (4) one sentence on what would cause that (e.g. "Upside: successful collection campaign; Downside: economic downturn"). Use a table. We will then plug in our own numbers and calculate revenue in Excel—we are NOT putting our actual data into AI.

2. **Use the output:** **Replace** the example values with **your** agreed assumptions. Use it in **budget** or **MTREF** documentation to show you have considered **alternatives**. **Council** and **Treasury** may require scenario or sensitivity analysis; this gives you a **structure**.

[STEP] Task C — One-paragraph forecast summary from aggregated numbers

### Task C: One-Paragraph Forecast Summary from Aggregated Numbers

1. In a **new chat**, provide **aggregated** numbers only. Example:

   > Our municipality’s forecast for next year: Total revenue R Xm (assuming collection rate 75%, tariff increase 6%, equitable share as per indicative); Total expenditure R Ym (employee cost increase 5.5%, bulk electricity +8%, other as per MTREF). Surplus R Zm. Main risks: collection could be 2% lower; bulk price could be 2% higher.

2. Then send:

   > Using only the numbers and risks I provided, write a **one-paragraph executive summary** of our forecast for the Municipal Manager. Include: (1) total revenue and expenditure and surplus; (2) the main assumptions (1–2); (3) the main risks (1–2); (4) one sentence on what we recommend (e.g. "Council to note; Finance to monitor collection monthly"). Use formal language. Do not add data we did not provide. We will verify and approve.

3. **Use the output:** **Edit** for **accuracy**. The **CFO** **approves** before it goes to **MM** or **council**. **AI** helps you **turn numbers into narrative**; you **own** the forecast and the message.

## When to Use Which AI Task

| Module 6 area | AI task | What you get | What you must do |
|---------------|---------|--------------|------------------|
| **Revenue** | Leading indicators; briefing; escalation rules | Structures, drafts, rule ideas | Validate with Revenue/CFO; use only aggregated data. |
| **Debt** | Segmentation; prioritisation score; write-off checklist | Frameworks and checklists | Implement in your systems; align with policy. |
| **Fraud** | Procurement, revenue, payroll red-flag checklists | Training and control-design aids | Validate with Audit/Risk; do not put live data in AI. |
| **Variance** | Draft variance narrative | One-paragraph explanation | CFO approves; use aggregated figures only. |
| **Forecasting** | Driver structure; scenario table; forecast summary | Logic and narrative | Build forecast in Excel/your system; CFO approves. |

**Remember:** AI **supports**; it does **not** replace your **systems**, **policies**, or **human oversight**. Always **validate** and **approve** before use in reports, council, or MFMA submissions.
