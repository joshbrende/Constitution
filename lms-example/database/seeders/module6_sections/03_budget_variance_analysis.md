[STEP] What is budget variance?

## Budget Variance Analysis

**Budget variance** is the difference between **budgeted** amounts and **actual** amounts for revenue or expenditure. Analysing variance helps you: (1) explain to council and auditors why results differ from the budget; (2) take corrective action during the year; (3) improve the next budget and forecasts. **MFMA** requires that municipalities manage revenue and expenditure in line with the approved budget and report on in-year performance (Section 71 monthly reports, Section 72 in-year reports).

### Variance direction (convention)

| Type | Revenue | Expenditure | Meaning |
|------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Favourable** | Actual > Budget | Actual < Budget | More revenue or less spend than planned — generally positive. |
| **Adverse** | Actual < Budget | Actual > Budget | Less revenue or more spend than planned — requires explanation and, if material, action. |

**Note:** "Favourable" for expenditure (under-spend) is not always good: it can mean delayed projects or under-delivery. Context matters.

[STEP] Key variance line items — revenue

## Key Variance Line Items: Revenue

Municipal revenue is typically broken into:

- **Property rates** — Often the largest source; variance from valuation changes, collection, and appeals.
- **Service charges** (water, electricity, sanitation, refuse) — Volume, tariff, and collection effects.
- **Equitable share and grants** — Timing of transfers; conditions met or not.
- **Other** (rent, interest, fines, etc.) — Smaller but can have large % variances.

For each, ask: *Is the variance due to **volume** (more/fewer units), **price/tariff**, **timing**, or **collection**?* That dictates the explanation and the action.

[STEP] Key variance line items — expenditure

## Key Variance Line Items: Expenditure

Common vote or program lines:

- **Employee costs** — Vacancies, overtime, wage settlements. Often the largest; small % = big rand.
- **Bulk purchases** (electricity, water) — Volume and tariff; hard to control without demand-side action.
- **Contracted services and materials** — Timing of projects; price changes; contract variations.
- **Repairs and maintenance** — Reactive vs planned; can be delayed (under-spend) or spike (crisis).
- **Finance charges** — Interest; can rise with debt or late payment to creditors.
- **Other** — Donations, grants to entities, provisions.

For each, ask: *Is the variance **operational** (we chose to spend more/less), **timing** (will catch up), **price** (external), or **one-off**?*

[STEP] MFMA Section 71 and 72 — what to report

## MFMA Section 71 and 72: What to Report

- **Section 71 (monthly):** Treasury requires monthly submissions on revenue and expenditure vs budget; cash flow; and other key aggregates. Large variances must be explained.
- **Section 72 (in-year):** Mid-year and other in-year reports to council; must include revised revenue and expenditure estimates if material changes.

**Implications for your worksheet:** Your budget variance worksheet should align with the **structure** your municipality uses for Section 71/72 (votes, programmes, or both). A clear, consistent **narrative** for material variances supports both internal management and external reporting. **AI** can help **draft** variance explanations from numbers you provide—you must verify and sign off.
