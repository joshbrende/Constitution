# Module 12: Action Planning Workshop

Time Slot: 15:15 - 16:30 (Day 3)

## Purpose of This Session

This session is the **Action Planning Workshop**: you will produce a **concrete** **action** **plan** to **take** **back** to your municipality. It draws on your **roadmap** and **prioritisation** from **Module 11**, and on the **AI** **use** **cases** and **trackers** from **Modules 2–10**. **SALGA Outcome 1** (Agile Force—action-oriented) and **Outcome 3** (Capable Local Government—implementation readiness) require **concrete** **plans** and **immediate** **next** **steps**. You will learn **theory** on **action** **plan** **development**, **prioritisation** (linking to Module 11), **resource** **identification**, **timeline** **development**, and **success** **metrics**; **practical** tasks to **build** your **action** **plan** and to **give** and **receive** **peer** **review**; and **AI** **application** tasks for **action** **plan** **generation**, **template-based** **planning**, **peer** **review** and **expert** **consultation** **support**, and **plan** **validation**.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** action plan development: **individual** vs **team** **plans**; **structure** (actions, owners, dates, resources, success metrics).
2. **Apply** prioritisation of AI initiatives: **select** from your **Module 11** **roadmap** and **P1–P4** for your **first** **3–6** **months**.
3. **Identify** resources: **who** (owner, **champion**, **support**); **what** (budget, **tools**, **data**); **where** to **find** them.
4. **Develop** a timeline: **milestones**, **due** **dates**, **dependencies**; **realistic** **first** **steps**.
5. **Define** success metrics: **what** to **measure** for **each** **action** or **initiative**; **when** to **review**.
6. **Build** your **action** **plan** using the **template** and **complete** a **peer** **review** **exchange**.
7. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to **assist** **action** **plan** **generation** from your **priorities**, **template-based** **planning**, **peer** **review** **prompts**, and **plan** **validation** **checklists**—with **generic** or **aggregated** inputs; **you** **own** the **final** **plan**.

## How to Use This Module

1. **Use your Module 11 outputs.** Your **roadmap** **initiatives** **list** and **prioritisation** (P1–P4) are the **source** of **actions**. If you **skipped** or **simplified** Module 11, **pick** **2–5** **initiatives** from **Modules 2–10** that you **will** **do** in the **next** **3–6** **months**.
2. **Work through the Practical.** The **action** **plan** **template** (Practical) is the **main** **output**—**fill** it in during this **workshop**. **Peer** **review** gives you **feedback** before you **finalise**.
3. **Use AI to assist, not replace.** **AI** can **draft** **actions** from **priorities**, **suggest** **templates**, **generate** **peer** **review** **questions**, and **validation** **checklists**. **You** **edit**, **own**, and **commit** to the **plan**.
4. **Prepare for Course Closure.** **Selected** **action** **plans** may be **presented** in **Course** **Closure** (16:30–17:00). **Finish** your **plan** so you can **present** or **share** **highlights**.
