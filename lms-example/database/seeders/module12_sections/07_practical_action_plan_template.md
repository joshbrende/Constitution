## Practical: Action Plan Template

**Tools:** **Google Sheets** or **Microsoft Excel**.

This **template** turns **theory** ( **sections** **2–6**) and your **Module 11** **priorities** into a **concrete** **action** **plan**. **Columns**: **Action**, **Owner**, **Due** **date**, **Resources**, **Success** **metric**, **Dependencies**. **AI** can **assist** **drafting** **actions** from **priorities**—you **populate**, **edit**, and **own** the **plan**.

### Step 1: Create the workbook

1. Open **Google Sheets** or **Excel**, new workbook.
2. **Sheet 1:** Name it "**Action** **Plan**". **Optional:** **Sheet 2** "**Cover**" with **title** "**Action** **Plan** — [Municipality] / [Directorate]", "**As** **at** [date]", "**Owner** [your name or team lead]", "**Scope**: **first** **3–6** **months**", "**Source**: **Module 11** **roadmap** / **P1–P4**".

### Step 2: Define column headers

**Row 1**, headers:

| # | Action | Owner | Due date | Resources | Success metric | Dependencies |
|---|--------|-------|----------|-----------|----------------|--------------|

- **#** — **Row** **number** or **ID** (1, 2, 3…).
- **Action** — **Specific** **task** or **deliverable** ( **SMART** where possible). **Link** to **Module 11** **initiative** in **brackets** if helpful (e.g. "**Complete** **revenue** **data** **inventory** [**Data** **mapping**, **M4**]").
- **Owner** — **Name** or **role** **accountable**.
- **Due date** — **Date** (e.g. **31‑Mar‑26**) or **quarter** (e.g. **Q1**).
- **Resources** — **Budget**, **tools**, **data** **access**, **approvals**, **support** ( **who**/ **what**).
- **Success metric** — **One** **sentence**: **how** you will **know** it **succeeded**.
- **Dependencies** — **What** or **who** must **be** **in** **place** **first**; **or** "—" if **none**.

### Step 3: Populate from Module 11

1. **List** your **P1** **initiatives** (and **selected** **P2** if **3–6** **months**). **One** **initiative** → **1–3** **actions**.
2. **Order** by **dependency** and **due** **date**: **earliest** **or** **blocking** **first**.
3. **Fill** **every** **column** for **each** **action**. **Gaps** (e.g. "**Data** **access**—**pending** **IT**") go in **Resources** or **Dependencies**—**escalate** as **needed**.
4. **Aim**: **3–6** **actions** (individual) or **5–12** (team). **At** **least** **one** **Quick** **win** with **due** **date** in **&lt;3** **months**.

### Step 4: Link to data–KPI mapping and SDBIP (optional)

- If **actions** relate to **dashboards** or **trackers**, **note** the **data** **source** and **KPI** (from **Module 4** **data–KPI** **mapping** and **SDBIP**). Example: "**Revenue** **tracker** — **billing** **system**; **KPI** **R1** **collection** **rate**."
- **Cover** or **footer**: "**Aligned** **to** **SDBIP** [references] and **Module 11** **roadmap**."

### Step 5: What needs attention

- **Bottom** of **sheet** or **separate** **row**: **3–5** **bullets** "**What** **needs** **attention**": (1) **Key** **dependency** or **risk** (e.g. "**IT** **data** **access**"); (2) **Approval** **needed** (e.g. "**MM** **for** **pilot**"); (3) **Resource** **gap** (e.g. "**Training** **slots**"); (4) **First** **review** **date**. **AI** can help **draft** this **from** **your** **plan**—you **edit** and **approve**.
