## AI Application 1: Extract Indicators from IDP or SDBIP Text

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

### Task: From a planning paragraph to a table of indicators

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Paste this sample (or a **non-confidential** excerpt from your IDP or SDBIP—remove names, exact figures, or sensitive projects):

   > Our municipality will refurbish 15 km of roads, connect 400 new households to water, and reduce water losses by 6%. We will train 25 staff in project management and complete the asset register update by Q2. We aim to improve our revenue collection rate from 68% to 75% and to reduce the average debt collection period.

3. Send this prompt:

   > From the paragraph above: (a) list each commitment as a separate row; (b) for each, say if it is an OUTPUT or an OUTCOME; (c) suggest one SMART indicator (with a suggested measure and, if possible, target or timeframe); (d) suggest which level of the results chain it fits (output, outcome, or—if applicable—impact). Use a table with columns: Commitment | Type (Output/Outcome) | Suggested indicator | Results level.

4. **Use the output:** This is a **first draft**. You would verify with your IDP or M&E unit: Are the types correct? Are the indicators measurable with your data? Do they align with your existing SDBIP? Then refine and adopt.

[STEP] AI Application 2: Generate a Theory of Change or Logic Model from a programme description

## AI Application 2: Generate a Theory of Change or Logic Model from a Programme Description

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

### Task: From a short programme description to a results chain

1. In a **new chat**, send this prompt (edit the bracketed parts to match a programme you know, or use the example):

   > We are a South African municipality. We have a programme to [e.g. expand water access to an underserved ward / improve revenue collection / run an LED project]. The programme will [e.g. lay 15 km of pipe, install 500 meters, run 4 community awareness sessions / implement a new billing campaign, train revenue staff, and work with wards on payment culture / support 10 SMMEs with training and market access]. In a table with 5 columns—Inputs | Activities | Outputs | Outcomes | Impact—give 2 to 4 bullets per column for this programme. Use typical municipal resources and timeframes. Add one assumption and one risk below the table.

2. **Use the output:** You now have a **draft Logic Model**. Compare it to your actual programme: What is missing? What would you change? Use it as a starting point for the Logic Model you built in the **Practical** section, or to brief a colleague.

### Optional: Ask for indicators

3. Add a follow-up message:

   > For the Outputs and Outcomes in your table, suggest one indicator each (measure + example target or timeframe). Use a small table: Level | Indicator | Example target.

4. **Use the output:** These are **suggested** indicators. Validate against your data sources and SDBIP format. Adopt or adapt.

[STEP] AI Application 3: Validate and improve a list of indicators

## AI Application 3: Validate and Improve a List of Indicators

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

### Task: Check if indicators are SMART and correctly typed

1. In a **new chat**, paste a short list of indicators (from your SDBIP, or use this sample):

   > Our KPIs include: (1) Number of water connections, (2) Collection rate, (3) Roads maintained, (4) Citizen satisfaction, (5) Audit outcome.

2. Send:

   > For each indicator above: (a) is it an OUTPUT or an OUTCOME? (b) is it SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)? If not, what is missing? (c) suggest a clearer wording or an added element (e.g. target, baseline) if needed. Use a table.

3. **Use the output:** AI can catch mislabelled types (e.g. “Citizen satisfaction” is an outcome, not an output) and missing SMART elements. You still decide what to adopt; use this as a quality check.
