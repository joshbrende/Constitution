[STEP] What is the golden thread?

## The Golden Thread: IDP → SDBIP → Budget → Scorecards

The **golden thread** is the clear, traceable link from your **long-term strategic plan** (IDP) to your **annual performance plan** (SDBIP), your **budget**, and your **individual performance agreements** (Section 56/57). If an auditor or oversight body asks *“Where does this KPI come from?”* you should be able to trace it back to an IDP priority and show that it is funded in the budget.

### The chain

| Document | Role | Typical horizon |
|----------|------|------------------|
| **IDP (Integrated Development Plan)** | Strategic: vision, priorities, objectives, key projects. Aligned to MTSF, SALGA, DDM. | 5 years (reviewed annually) |
| **SDBIP (Service Delivery and Budget Implementation Plan)** | Annual: top-layer objectives and KPIs; what we will achieve this year; links to budget. | 1 year |
| **Budget** | Resources: revenue and expenditure; which programmes and projects get money. Must fund SDBIP/IDP. | 1 year (3-year MTREF) |
| **Section 56/57 agreements (and lower-level scorecards)** | Individual/team: what each manager must deliver; derived from SDBIP and, through it, IDP. | 1 year |

### Why it matters for audits and CoGTA

- **Auditor-General** and **National Treasury** expect to see: IDP objectives reflected in SDBIP; SDBIP objectives funded in the budget; and individual agreements that clearly support SDBIP.
- **Disconnect** = audit findings, questions from MPAC and council, and difficulty reporting to CoGTA and SALGA. A visible **linkage table** or **mapping** reduces that risk.

[STEP] How to build and document the linkage

## How to Build and Document the Linkage

### 1. Start from the IDP

List the **strategic priorities** or **objectives** from your IDP (or the relevant chapter, e.g. basic services, finance, governance). Each should have or imply **outcomes** and **outputs**.

### 2. Map to SDBIP

For each IDP priority, identify the **SDBIP objective(s)** and **KPI(s)** that implement it in the current year. One IDP objective can have several SDBIP KPIs.

### 3. Link to the budget

For each SDBIP KPI or programme, identify the **budget line** or **vote** that funds it. No KPI should be “orphaned” (unfunded); no major budget line should lack a corresponding objective or KPI where relevant.

### 4. Link to Section 56/57

Which **directorate** or **manager** is responsible for each SDBIP KPI? Their performance agreement should list those KPIs (or a subset for that level). The “cascade” from SDBIP to individual is your organisation’s structure.

### 5. Document in a linkage table

A **linkage table** has columns such as:

**IDP priority/objective** | **SDBIP objective** | **SDBIP KPI** | **Budget vote/line** | **Owner (role)** | **Indicator type (output/outcome)**

You do not need every cell for every row, but the table should make the thread visible. In the **Practical** section we will build one. In the **AI Application** section we will use AI to generate a template and to draft linkage text.
