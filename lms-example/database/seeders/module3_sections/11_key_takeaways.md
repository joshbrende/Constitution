## Module 3: Key Takeaways

1. **Results-based performance management** means focusing on results (outputs, outcomes, impact), not only on activities and inputs. It is required by MFMA, the Systems Act, and SALGA Outcomes 3 and 4.

2. **Theory of Change** and **Logic Models** structure the results chain: Inputs → Activities → Outputs → Outcomes → Impact. Use them to design indicators, communicate to council and oversight, and check alignment with the IDP and budget.

3. **Output** = what we deliver (e.g. connections, km of road). **Outcome** = what changes as a result (e.g. reliable supply, better collection rate). **Impact** = longer-term change for the community. Get the type right for audits and reporting.

4. **Leading indicators** help you steer during the year; **lagging indicators** support year-end accountability. Use both. Indicators should be **SMART** and have a clear **data source** and **owner**.

5. The **golden thread** links IDP → SDBIP → Budget → Section 56/57 scorecards. Document it in a **linkage table**. Disconnect creates audit risk and makes it hard to report to CoGTA and SALGA.

6. The **District Development Model (DDM)** supports One Plan, One Budget across the district. Your IDP and SDBIP should show how you align with district priorities; report on it in the annual report.

7. **Practical skills:** You can build a **Logic Model** for one programme and an **IDP–SDBIP linkage table** for one priority. These are tools you can use immediately.

8. **AI Application:** You can use **ChatGPT** or **Claude** to extract indicators from planning text; generate a Theory of Change or Logic Model from a programme description; validate indicators (SMART, type); generate linkage checklists and alignment narratives; and create linkage table templates. Always **validate** AI output with your IDP coordinator or M&E unit.
