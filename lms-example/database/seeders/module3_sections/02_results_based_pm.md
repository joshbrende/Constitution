## What Is Results-Based Performance Management?

**Results-based performance management** (sometimes called results-based management, RBM) means designing planning, budgeting, and reporting around **results**—what we achieve—rather than only around **activities** or **inputs**. We still track how much we spend and what we do; but we also define and measure **outputs** (what we deliver), **outcomes** (what changes as a result), and where possible **impact** (longer-term change for citizens and the municipality).

### Why it matters for South African municipalities

- **MFMA (Municipal Finance Management Act)** and **Municipal Systems Act** require municipalities to plan, budget, and report on **performance**. Auditors and National Treasury expect to see a clear link between money spent, activities, and results.
- **SALGA Outcome 3 (Capable and Reputable Local Government)** and **Outcome 4 (Effective Networks and IGR)** emphasise results-based planning, integrated planning (IDP, SDBIP, DDM), and accountability. Your performance framework must support these.
- **CoGTA and the Auditor-General** look for the **golden thread**: do your IDP priorities flow into your SDBIP objectives, your budget, and your Section 56/57 agreements? Results-based design makes that thread explicit and measurable.

## Principles of Results-Based Performance Management

| Principle | What it means in practice |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Focus on results** | Define what “success” looks like in measurable terms (outputs, outcomes). Activities and inputs serve those results. |
| **Link planning to budgeting** | The budget funds the activities that produce the outputs and outcomes in your SDBIP and IDP. No “orphan” spending. |
| **Evidence-based** | Collect data to verify whether you achieved the results. Evidence supports audits and oversight. |
| **Accountability** | Clear ownership: who is responsible for which outputs and outcomes? Section 56/57 managers, directors, and the MM. |
| **Learning and adaptation** | Use performance information to improve. When results are off track, adjust activities, resources, or—with council approval—targets. |

### Legal and policy anchors

- **Municipal Systems Act (s 38–41)**: Integrated Development Plan (IDP) as the principal strategic plan; must reflect the municipality’s vision, objectives, and priorities. Performance must be monitored.
- **MFMA**: Municipal budgets must be aligned with the IDP; in-year and annual reporting on performance. Treasury regulations and circulars specify formats (e.g. SDBIP, annual report).
- **SALGA 2026, Outcome 3**: Results-based planning, systems thinking, performance management systems. **Outcome 4**: Integrated planning (IDP, DDM), coordination across spheres.

Your **performance management framework**—the set of plans, indicators, and processes you use—must be built so it can deliver on these requirements. Theory of Change and Logic Models (next section) give you a structure.
