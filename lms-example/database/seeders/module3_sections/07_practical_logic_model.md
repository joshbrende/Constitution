## Practical: Build a Logic Model for One Programme

**Tools:** **Google Sheets**, **Excel**, or **Word** (table).

### Step 1: Choose the programme

Pick **one** programme or project (e.g. water connections, revenue collection improvement, LED project, waste service, by-law enforcement). Use one you know or have access to.

### Step 2: Create a one-page Logic Model table

Create a table with 5 columns: **Inputs** | **Activities** | **Outputs** | **Outcomes** | **Impact**.

### Step 3: Fill each column (2–5 bullets each)

- **Inputs:** Budget (or “as per vote X”), staff (roles, not names), systems, equipment, partnerships.
- **Activities:** What you actually do. Be specific: “Lay 15 km pipe,” “Conduct 4 revenue campaigns,” “Train 20 staff.”
- **Outputs:** Tangible deliverables. “500 new water connections,” “1 000 pre-termination notices issued,” “20 staff trained.”
- **Outcomes:** Short- to medium-term changes. “Increased % with reliable supply,” “Improved collection rate,” “Fewer complaints.”
- **Impact:** Long-term. “Sustainable services,” “Financial sustainability,” “Improved citizen trust.”

### Step 4: Add assumptions and one risk

Below or beside the table, add:
- **Assumptions:** e.g. “Households will connect when network is extended”; “Billing system can support the new campaigns.”
- **Risks:** e.g. “Vandalism may affect new infrastructure”; “Economic downturn may affect collection.”

### Step 5: Draft 2 output and 2 outcome indicators

For the **Outputs** column, pick 2 and write them as **indicators** (measure + target + timing). Example: “Number of new household water connections, target 500, by 30 June.”  
For the **Outcomes** column, pick 2. Example: “% of households with reliable supply (7 days/week, no unplanned cut > 24 h), target 85%, by year-end.”
