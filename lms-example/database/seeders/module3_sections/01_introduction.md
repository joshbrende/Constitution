# Module 3: Performance Management Framework Design

Time Slot: 13:30 - 15:00

## Purpose of This Session

This session gives you the **conceptual and practical foundation** for designing performance management frameworks that work: results-based planning, Theory of Change, Logic Models, and the **golden thread** linking your IDP, SDBIP, budget, and individual scorecards. You will learn how to distinguish output, outcome, and impact indicators; how to build a simple Logic Model for a municipal programme; and how to use **AI** to extract indicators, draft Logic Models, and check alignment between your plans. The focus is on **theory, practical application, and AI** so you can design and defend frameworks that meet MFMA, Systems Act, and SALGA requirements.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this session, you will be able to:

1. **Explain** what results-based performance management is and why it matters for municipalities (MFMA, Systems Act, SALGA).
2. **Describe** Theory of Change and Logic Models and use them to structure a programme or directorate’s results chain.
3. **Distinguish** output, outcome, and impact indicators—and leading vs lagging indicators—with examples from municipal work.
4. **Map** the golden thread from IDP to SDBIP to budget to Section 56/57 scorecards and explain why linkage matters for audits and oversight.
5. **Explain** how the District Development Model (DDM) fits into IDP and performance reporting.
6. **Build** a simple Logic Model and an IDP–SDBIP linkage table for one priority or programme.
7. **Use** generative AI (ChatGPT, Claude) to extract indicators from planning text, suggest a Theory of Change or Logic Model, and generate linkage checklists and alignment text.

## How to Use This Module

1. **Connect to your plans.** Have your IDP, SDBIP, or APP to hand (or non-confidential excerpts). The hands-on and AI tasks use samples you can swap for your own.
2. **Work through each step.** Use **Next** and **Previous** to move. Theory sections set the concepts; **Practical** and **AI Application** sections give you tools you can use immediately.
3. **Validate AI output.** AI suggests structure and wording; you and your IDP coordinator or M&E unit validate and approve.
4. **Build for the Knowledge Check.** The Module 3 Knowledge Check will test your understanding of frameworks, indicators, and linkage. Use this content as your reference.
