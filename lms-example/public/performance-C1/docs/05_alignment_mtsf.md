## What is MTSF and why municipal alignment matters

The **Medium-Term Strategic Framework (MTSF)** is the government’s five-year plan. It translates the National Development Plan and electoral mandate into priorities, outcomes, and targets. National and provincial departments align their strategic and annual plans to it—and **municipalities must do the same**.

Your **IDP, SDBIP, budget, and APP** need to show how your municipality contributes to these national priorities. CoGTA, National Treasury, and the Auditor-General use MTSF alignment when they assess municipal performance, funding, and oversight. In the following steps we expand on each priority and include an **AI Application** for each—so you can use AI to check alignment, draft KPIs, and strengthen the link between MTSF and what you measure and report.

[STEP] Priority 1: A Capable, Ethical and Developmental State

## Priority 1: A Capable, Ethical and Developmental State

**Context:** This priority focuses on a state that can deliver: skilled leaders, sound governance, clear accountability, and professional administration. For municipalities it means capable political and administrative leadership, clean audits, and systems that support rather than block service delivery.

**What it includes:**
- **Improved leadership, governance, and accountability** — Mayors, councillors, and officials who can steer the municipality, comply with the law, and account for decisions and spending.
- **Functional, efficient, and integrated government** — Systems and processes that work across departments and with provincial and national government (e.g. DDM, IGR).
- **Professional, merit-based, ethical public administration** — Recruitment, performance management, and conduct that build trust and reduce political interference and corruption.

**Municipal angle:** Your IDP and APP should reflect capacity-building, governance reforms, and audit readiness. KPIs might include: % of S56 managers on performance agreements, audit outcome, implementation of council resolutions, and ethics and fraud prevention measures.

[STEP] Priority 2: Economic Transformation and Job Creation

## Priority 2: Economic Transformation and Job Creation

**Context:** National goals include more jobs, inclusive growth, and a more competitive economy. Local government supports this through **Local Economic Development (LED)**, a conducive business environment, and the delivery of infrastructure and services that enable economic activity.

**What it includes:**
- **Competitiveness through ICT and innovation** — Use of technology, e-government, and innovation to improve services and attract investment.
- **More decent jobs created and sustained** — LED projects, SMME support, and procurement that create or safeguard jobs.
- **Competitive and accessible markets** — Regulation, by-laws, and land use that support formal and informal business.

**Municipal angle:** Your IDP and SDBIP often include LED targets, business licence turnaround times, support to township and rural economy, and infrastructure that enables growth (e.g. industrial land, bulk services). KPIs might cover jobs indirectly supported, SMME programmes, or investment pipeline.

[STEP] Priority 4: Consolidating the Social Wage — Reliable and Quality Basic Services

## Priority 4: Consolidating the Social Wage — Reliable and Quality Basic Services

**Context:** The “social wage” is the basket of services and support that improve quality of life: water, sanitation, electricity, health, education, housing, and social protection. Municipalities are central to **basic services** and to the dignity and safety of communities.

**What it includes:**
- **Community development interventions** — Programmes and partnerships that build capacity, cohesion, and resilience in communities.
- **Improved access to basic services** — More households with access to water, sanitation, electricity, and refuse removal; reduced backlogs and more reliable supply.

**Municipal angle:** Your IDP and SDBIP are built around basic service targets: household connections, percentage of population with access, backlog reduction, and quality (e.g. water quality, loss levels). This is where many of your **output and outcome indicators** live. AI and performance systems in later modules will help you monitor these in real time.

[STEP] Priority 5: Spatial Integration, Human Settlements and Local Government

## Priority 5: Spatial Integration, Human Settlements and Local Government

**Context:** Where people live and how land is used shape service delivery, economic opportunity, and climate risk. This priority links **spatial planning**, human settlements, and the role of local government in implementing a shared national spatial vision.

**What it includes:**
- **Improved access to basic services** — Aligning spatial and infrastructure investment so underserved areas are prioritised.
- **Shared national spatial vision** — Spatial Development Frameworks (SDFs) and land-use schemes that support density, mixing, and resilience.
- **Functional sub-national regional development** — Coordination across municipalities and with provinces (e.g. DDM, city‑region planning).
- **Climate change preparedness** — Spatial and infrastructure choices that reduce vulnerability and support adaptation.

**Municipal angle:** Your **SDF**, **land-use scheme**, and **IDP** must align with SPLUMA, the Integrated Urban Development Framework (IUDF), and provincial spatial plans. KPIs can include: % of households in formalised areas, planning applications finalised, inclusion of climate risks in IDP, and DDM alignment.

[STEP] Priority 6: Social Cohesion and Safer Communities

## Priority 6: Social Cohesion and Safer Communities

**Context:** Social cohesion and safety depend on active citizens, trust in institutions, and respect for rights. Municipalities contribute through **inclusive participation**, **transparency**, **anti-corruption**, and **partnerships** with communities and other spheres.

**What it includes:**
- **Promoting active citizenry and leadership** — Public participation in IDP and budgeting; ward committees; and community structures that have a real say.
- **Improvement in corruption perception** — Transparent procurement, consequence management, and credible anti‑fraud measures.
- **Fostering constitutional values** — Non‑discrimination, redress, and inclusion in services and employment.
- **Equal opportunities, inclusion, and redress** — Equitable access to services and economic opportunity across geography, gender, and grouping.

**Municipal angle:** Your IDP and APP often include participation targets (e.g. number of IDP consultations), disclosure of tenders and payments, and diversity and redress in employment and procurement. KPIs might cover: public participation reach, disclosure compliance, or progress on employment equity and procurement for designated groups.
