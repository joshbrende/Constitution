[STEP] Vision, mission, and core values

## SALGA's Strategic Context

### Vision

An association that leads innovation through cutting-edge quality and sustainable service delivery to member municipalities for better services to communities.

### Mission

To be consultative, informed, mandated, credible, accountable and protect the interests of membership and act as a catalyst for developmental local government.

### Core values

- **Responsive:** Quick and flexible in responding to member needs
- **Innovative:** Exploring new ways and providing fresh, unconventional services
- **Dynamic:** Flexible and adapting to change
- **Excellence:** Serving members with pride and excellence

Use these when you communicate with SALGA, CoGTA, or other spheres: they show you share the same language and priorities.

[STEP] The 5 R's framework

## The 5 R's framework

SALGA's strategic approach is built on five principles:

1. **Reform** — Build new approaches beyond current difficulties
2. **Reimagine** — Relook at how things are done to enhance local government functioning
3. **Return** — Refine processes to return municipalities to better positions
4. **Resilience** — Remain true to the course and fight through until solutions are found
5. **Resolve** — Explore new ways and provide members with fresh services

## Hands-on: Turn the 5 R's into an action checklist

**Tool:** [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

1. Open **ChatGPT** or **Claude** in a new chat.
2. Paste this prompt:

   > SALGA uses five principles for local government: Reform, Reimagine, Return, Resilience, Resolve. For each one, give: (1) a one-sentence definition in plain English, (2) one example of what a municipality could DO under that principle (e.g. under "Reform": review the revenue collection process and pilot a new pre-paid system), and (3) one question we can ask ourselves in our next planning meeting to check if we are applying it. Use a table with 4 columns: Principle | Definition | Example action | Planning question.

3. **Use the output:** Copy the table into a Word or Google Doc. In your next IDP, APP, or SDBIP review, work through the "Planning question" column and note which 5 R's you are strong in and which need more attention.
