[STEP] Why AI now?

## Why AI now?

Three factors make AI adoption critical for municipalities:

1. **Data availability** — Municipalities already generate huge amounts of data (billing, assets, complaints, HR, projects).
2. **Computing power** — Cloud and free/low-cost tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Google, Excel) make AI accessible without big IT budgets.
3. **Proven applications** — Other governments use AI for revenue, compliance, and service delivery. We can learn and adapt.

## Hands-on: List data sources that could feed AI

**Tool:** **ChatGPT** or **Claude** — [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) or [Claude](https://claude.ai)

[STEP] AI as a force multiplier

## AI as a force multiplier

AI does not replace human judgment. It:

- Processes large amounts of data quickly
- Surfaces patterns humans might miss
- Gives real-time insights
- Automates routine tasks (e.g. first-level report drafting, checking lists)
- Enables simple predictive use cases (e.g. "who might not pay?")

## Hands-on: Use ChatGPT or Claude to analyse a planning paragraph

[STEP] Hands-on: Create a KPI table for one area in Excel or Google Sheets

## Hands-on: Create a KPI table for one area in Excel or Google Sheets

**Tool:** **Google Sheets** (sheets.google.com) or **Microsoft Excel**

1. Open **Google Sheets** or **Excel**, new workbook.
2. Name the sheet (tab) **Governance KPIs** (or **Financial** or **Service delivery** — pick one).
3. In **row 1** put: `KPI` | `What we measure` | `Source of data` | `How often` | `Who owns it`.
4. Add 4–5 rows. Example for **Governance**:
   - *MFMA compliance* | % of required submissions done on time | CoGTA / Treasury returns | Quarterly | CFO
   - *Audit findings* | Number of findings (or qualified/disclaimer) | AG report | Annually | CFO / Audit
   - *Council resolutions implemented* | % implemented within 90 days | Council / MM office | Quarterly | MM office
   - *By-law enforcement* | Number of by-law cases opened and closed | Enforcement unit | Monthly | Community services
5. In **Source of data**, write where the number comes from (system, report, or "manual count").
6. **Use this:** This is the start of a dashboard. In Modules 2–12 we will add AI to automate the "source" and to highlight risks. For now, the table clarifies what to measure and who is responsible.
