# SALGA 2025-2026 Annual Performance Plan: Key Requirements for Municipal Performance in 2026

## Executive Summary

Based on the SALGA 2025-2026 Annual Performance Plan, this document outlines the critical performance areas, KPIs, and requirements that South African municipalities must meet to perform well in 2026 and advance the developmental agenda.

---

## 1. SALGA's Strategic Context

### Vision
An association that leads innovation through cutting-edge quality and sustainable service delivery to member municipalities for better services to communities.

### Mission
To be consultative, informed, mandated, credible, accountable and protect the interests of membership and act as a catalyst for developmental local government.

### Core Values
- **Responsive**: Quick and flexible in responding to member needs
- **Innovative**: Exploring new ways and providing fresh, unconventional services
- **Dynamic**: Flexible and adapting to change
- **Excellence**: Serving members with pride and excellence

### Key Principles (5 R's)
- **Reform**: Build new approaches beyond current difficulties
- **Reimagine**: Relook at how things are done to enhance local government functioning
- **Return**: Refine processes to return municipalities to better positions
- **Resilience**: Remain true to the course and fight through until solutions are found
- **Resolve**: Explore new ways and provide members with fresh services

## 3. Critical Performance Areas for Municipalities

### 3.1 Political Leadership and Administrative Management

**Core Challenges:**
- Poor political leadership capacity and weak administrative management
- High turnover of councillors (two-thirds new deployees every 5 years)
- Lack of skilled mayors and councillors
- Under-capacitated Municipal Public Accounts Committees (MPACs)
- Coalition government instability
- Non-standardised recruitment-to-retirement processes

**Key Performance Requirements:**
1. **Leadership Development:**
   - Targeted high-impact programmes for weak municipalities
   - Tailor-made councillor training programmes (fit-for-purpose)
   - Differentiated training material based on needs analysis
   - Mentorship programmes
   - Professionalisation of local government sector

2. **Performance Management:**
   - Remunerative performance management system for political and administrative leadership
   - Rigorous political oversight to ensure delivery of quality services
   - Performance-based allocation of budget

3. **Standardisation:**
   - Standardised recruitment-to-retirement processes for municipal staff
   - Standardised job requirements for technical levels (CFOs, Municipal Managers)
   - Professionalisation model implementation

4. **Accountability:**
   - Introduction of performance management and accountability-based remuneration regime
   - Local government service commissioners for swift investigation of allegations
   - Speakers' forum for oversight requirements

### 3.2 Financial Management and Revenue Collection

**Core Challenges:**
- Municipal debt of R306.7 billion (as at September 2023)
- 64% of outstanding debt is irrecoverable
- Average debt collection period of 213 days
- Chronic underfunding (9.1% of nationally raised revenue vs 46% of constitutional functions)
- Unfunded mandates
- Poor financial administration

**Key Performance Requirements:**
1. **Revenue Management:**
   - Innovative revenue collection methods (prepaid systems, debt restructuring)
   - Review of equitable share formula
   - Review of Municipal Property Rates Act (MPRA) for revenue diversification
   - Innovative measures for restructuring uncollectable historical debts

2. **Financial Planning:**
   - Appropriate financial planning based on identified community needs
   - Gender-responsive budgeting (GRB) mainstreaming
   - Performance-oriented system to direct financial allocations

3. **Financial Governance:**
   - Accountable, transparent and good financial governance
   - Effective and efficient utilisation of allocated financials
   - Elimination of fiscal leakages and inefficiencies
   - Consequence management for financial transgressions

4. **Infrastructure Funding:**
   - Infrastructure borrowings and funding mechanisms
   - Strategic partnerships for infrastructure projects
   - Municipal Systems Improvement Grant (MSIG) utilisation

### 3.3 Service Delivery and Infrastructure

**Core Challenges:**
- Degenerating infrastructure
- Non-existent or poor services in many communities
- Service delivery disparities (especially rural areas)
- Inadequate technical and planning capacity

**Key Performance Requirements:**
1. **Service Delivery Standards:**
   - Accessibility of services (all citizens regardless of race, gender, sexual orientation)
   - Affordability of services (balanced tariffs, appropriate service levels, cross-subsidization)
   - Quality of products and services (suitability, timeliness, convenience, safety, continuity)

2. **Infrastructure Management:**
   - Sustainable infrastructure enabling basic service delivery
   - Continuous citizen engagement in local government matters
   - Adequate technical and planning capacity

3. **Integrated Planning:**
   - Results-based planning processes
   - Integrated Development Plans (IDPs) with outcome indicators
   - Linkage between IDPs, SDBIPs and budgets
   - District Development Model (DDM) implementation

### 3.4 Digital Transformation and Technology

**Core Challenges:**
- Lack of big data analytical skills
- Non-aligned planning, monitoring and evaluation systems
   - Obsolete systems and solutions
   - Inefficient ICT/knowledge and information generation
   - Digital divide between urban and rural municipalities

**Key Performance Requirements:**
1. **Digital Strategy:**
   - SALGA IT strategy to inform IS and ICT strategy
   - Adoption of SALGA strategic digital framework
   - Standardised processes and protocols for digital strategies
   - E-government (Digitalisation) adoption

2. **Data and Analytics:**
   - Data analytics capacity development
   - Intelligence products for decision-making
   - Data-as-a-service platform for municipalities
   - Big data management and interpretation

3. **Knowledge Management:**
   - Lessons learned portal
   - Research capability advancement
   - Knowledge sharing platforms
   - Information hub for local government

### 3.5 Governance and Compliance

**Core Challenges:**
- Non-compliance with statutory requirements (MFMA, Labour Relations Act)
- Irregular expenditures and procurement violations
- Political interference in operations
- Resistance to external oversight

**Key Performance Requirements:**
1. **Legal Compliance:**
   - Compliance with Municipal Finance Management Act (MFMA)
   - Compliance with Labour Relations Act
   - By-law enforcement
   - Legislative compliance enforcement

2. **Ethical Governance:**
   - Anti-corruption measures (whistleblower protection, transparent procurement)
   - Ethical leadership practices
   - Merit-based recruitment practices

3. **Cooperative Governance:**
   - Effective intergovernmental relations (IGR)
   - Coordination across spheres of government
   - Reduced red-tape and well-coordinated policy development

### 3.6 Economic Development and Investment

**Core Challenges:**
- High unemployment rates
- Declining local economies
- Lack of investment attraction
   - Underdeveloped rural and township economies

**Key Performance Requirements:**
1. **Local Economic Development (LED):**
   - Conducive business climate creation
   - Investment attraction and retention strategies
   - Reindustrialisation initiatives
   - Rural, township and regional economy development

2. **Green Economy:**
   - Building Inclusive Green Municipalities (BIGM) replication
   - Green Economy initiatives as alternative revenue streams
   - Climate change adaptation and mitigation

3. **Spatial Development:**
   - Spatial justice through aligned municipal spatial development plans
   - Integrated Urban Development Framework (IUDF) compliance
   - Spatial Planning and Land-Use Management Act (SPLUMA) compliance

## Conclusion

For South African municipalities to perform well in 2026 and meet SALGA's KPIs, they must focus on:

1. **Leadership Excellence**: Skilled, ethical, and accountable leadership
2. **Financial Sustainability**: Effective revenue collection and resource utilisation
3. **Service Delivery**: Quality, accessible, and affordable services
4. **Digital Transformation**: Technology-enabled governance and operations
5. **Integrated Planning**: Results-based, coordinated, and aligned planning
6. **Stakeholder Engagement**: Active citizenry and effective partnerships
7. **Compliance and Governance**: Legal compliance and ethical conduct
8. **Economic Development**: Investment attraction and local economic growth

The integration of AI and intelligent oversight systems will be critical in achieving these objectives and advancing the 2026 developmental agenda.
