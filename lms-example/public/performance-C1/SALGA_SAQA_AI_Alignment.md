# SALGA, SAQA and AI Application Alignment

This note describes how the **Performance Management and Monitoring Using AI** course aligns with:

- **SALGA 2025–2026 Annual Performance Plan** (`salga.pdf`): strategic outcomes, mandate, and municipal value
- **SAQA Guidelines** (`saqa_guidelines.pdf`): purpose, outcomes, applied competence, ethics, and assessment
- **AI application**: standards for prompts, data use, and take-back value so AI use is **solid, readable and practical** upon completion

---

## 1. SALGA — Value and benefits

### 1.1 Six strategic outcomes → modules and municipal value

| SALGA outcome | Module(s) | Municipal value and benefits |
|---------------|-----------|-----------------------------|
| **1. Agile Force of Influence** | 1, 8, 11, 12 | Capacity, leadership, advocacy, validated data; risk and compliance; implementation roadmap; action plans for MM and IGR. |
| **2. Customised Innovative Solutions** | 2, 4, 5, 9, 11 | Digital and AI capability; data and big data; dashboards and predictive tools; practical roadmap. |
| **3. Capable and Reputable Local Government** | 1, 3, 5, 8, 11, 12 | Performance management, accountability, DDM, ethics, compliance, audit readiness, implementation readiness. |
| **4. Effective Networks and IGR** | 1, 3, 10 | IDP/DDM alignment; IGR and common positions; citizen engagement and participation. |
| **5. Inclusive Socio-Economic Return** | 5, 6, 7, 9, 10 | Financial sustainability; revenue and debt; service delivery; planning; citizen satisfaction. |
| **6. Developmental Growth for Global Competitiveness** | 1, 9 | Predictive planning; resource allocation; LED; long-term trends. |

### 1.2 SALGA mandate and APP

- The course supports SALGA’s mandate: **representation**, **capacity-building**, **cooperative governance**, **innovation**, and **developmental local government**.
- Content and benefits are aligned with the **SALGA 2025–26 APP** (impact, outcome statements, pathway of change), **MTSF**, and **District Development Model (DDM)**.

## 2. SAQA — Quality and consistency

### 2.1 Purpose

- **Benefit to learner**: Applied AI and performance management skills; templates and plans to use the same day and take back to the municipality.
- **What the learner will know, do and understand**: SALGA outcomes, MFMA/IDP/SDBIP, data and AI concepts; design of indicators, dashboards, trackers, and action plans; when and how to use AI within ethics and POPI.
- **Graduate attributes**: Analyse performance gaps; design and implement monitoring; use AI to assist (not replace) judgment; validate and own outputs.

### 2.2 Exit-level and applied competence

- **Foundational**: Concepts, legislation, SALGA, and AI; ability to analyse and interpret.
- **Practical**: Building logic models, linkage tables, dashboards, trackers, and action plans in Excel/Sheets.
- **Reflexive**: Reflection questions, peer review, and validation; ability to adapt and explain in context.

### 2.3 Ethics

- **Governance, management and finance** (Modules 1, 3, 6, 8): **Ethics** is explicit in compliance, accountability, fraud prevention, and human oversight.
- **AI** (all modules): Ethical AI, POPI, and “what not to put in public AI” are in Module 2 and reinforced in every AI application section.

### 2.4 Integrated assessment

- **Formative**: Practicals, AI tasks, peer review, reflection questions.
- **Summative**: Action plan, selected presentations, course evaluation.
- **Consistency**: Objectives and outcomes are reflected in section content, practicals, and AI tasks.

## 3. AI application — Solid, readable, practical

### 3.1 Standard for every AI section

1. **Tool**: State [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com) and/or [Claude](https://claude.ai).
2. **Data and ethics (up front)**:
   - Use only **aggregated, non-confidential, or generic** inputs.
   - **Never** put confidential budget, personnel, debtor or citizen identifiers, or sensitive contracts into public AI.
   - POPI and MFMA require care; you **own** the final output and **validate** with the right owner (MM, CFO, PMU, IT) before council, SDBIP or audit.

3. **Tasks**: Clear **Task A, B, C** with:
   - **Copy-paste prompts** (or very close), so they work the same day.
   - **“Use the output”**: what to do with the result (edit, put in a template, get sign-off).

4. **Readable**: Short sentences; [STEP] structure; tables for prompts and outputs where it helps.

### 3.2 Upon completion

- Participants can **same day**: run prompts for drafting, KPI extraction, checklists, and dashboard specs; use only aggregated/generic data.
- **Take-back**: Prompt library; trackers; linkage and validation checklists; action plan and roadmap templates.
- **Accountability**: Clear that AI assists; the official **validates and owns** all content used in governance, finance, or reporting.
