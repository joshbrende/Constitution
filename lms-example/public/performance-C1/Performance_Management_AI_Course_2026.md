## 1. INTRODUCTION

In an era where South African municipalities face unprecedented challenges—from R306.7 billion in municipal debt to deteriorating infrastructure and service delivery backlogs—the need for intelligent, data-driven performance management has never been more critical. The South African Local Government Association (SALGA) 2025-2026 Annual Performance Plan identifies six strategic outcomes that require municipalities to transform their approach to governance, service delivery, and accountability.

This comprehensive 3-day course addresses the urgent need for municipalities to leverage Artificial Intelligence (AI) and intelligent oversight systems to meet SALGA's 2026 developmental agenda. The course is specifically designed to bridge the gap between traditional performance management approaches and the cutting-edge AI technologies that can revolutionize how municipalities monitor, evaluate, and improve their performance.

**Why This Course Matters:**

- **64% of municipal debt is irrecoverable** - AI can predict and prevent future debt accumulation
- **Average debt collection period of 213 days** - AI-powered systems can optimize revenue collection
- **Chronic underfunding** (9.1% of revenue vs 46% of functions) - AI enables smarter resource allocation
- **Lack of big data analytical skills** - This course builds critical AI and analytics capabilities
- **Obsolete monitoring and evaluation systems** - Learn to implement modern AI-driven M&E frameworks

The course aligns directly with SALGA's 2025–2026 Annual Performance Plan and six strategic outcomes:
- **Outcome 1**: Agile Force of Influence (capacity, leadership, advocacy, validated data—Modules 1, 8, 11, 12)
- **Outcome 2**: Customised Innovative Solutions (digital transformation, big data, technology—Modules 2, 4, 5, 9, 11)
- **Outcome 3**: Capable and Reputable Local Government (performance management, compliance, DDM, ethics—Modules 1, 3, 5, 8, 11, 12)
- **Outcome 4**: Effective Networks and IGR (IDP/DDM, partnerships, citizen engagement—Modules 1, 3, 10)
- **Outcome 5**: Inclusive Socio-Economic Return (financial sustainability, revenue, service delivery—Modules 5, 6, 7, 9, 10)
- **Outcome 6**: Developmental Growth for Global Competitiveness (planning, LED, resource allocation—Modules 1, 9)

Design reflects the SALGA mandate (representation, capacity-building, cooperative governance), MTSF priorities, and District Development Model (DDM). Quality and consistency are informed by SAQA guidelines for outcomes-based learning, applied competence, and integrated assessment (see *SALGA_SAQA_AI_Alignment.md*).

Formative tasks (practicals, AI exercises) and summative elements (action plan, presentations) support integrated assessment.

### 4.2 Ethics, governance and POPI

**Governance and finance** content (Modules 1, 3, 6, 8) and **AI use** (all modules) explicitly include **ethics**: fairness, transparency, human-in-the-loop, and **POPI Act** compliance. You will know what to put—and **not** put—into public AI tools; when to validate with MM, CFO, or Risk; and how to document accountability. This addresses SAQA expectations for ethics in management, governance and finance-related learning.

### 4.3 AI application upon completion

Upon completion, you will be able to **use AI in a solid, readable and practical way**:
- **Same-day use**: Apply copy-paste prompts (ChatGPT/Claude) for drafting, KPI extraction, checklists, and dashboards; use only **aggregated or generic** inputs; **never** confidential or identifiable data in public AI.
- **Take-back value**: Templates (action plan, trackers, linkage tables, validation checklists), a prompt library, and an AI implementation roadmap to tailor for your municipality.
- **Accountability**: You **own** all outputs; validate AI-assisted work with the appropriate owner (MM, CFO, PMU, IT) before council, SDBIP or audit use.
