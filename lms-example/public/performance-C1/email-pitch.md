## Context (from course document)

South African municipalities face **unprecedented challenges**: R306.7 billion in municipal debt, deteriorating infrastructure, and service delivery backlogs. The [SALGA](https://www.salga.org.za/) 2025–2026 Annual Performance Plan identifies **six strategic outcomes** that require municipalities to transform governance, service delivery, and accountability. This 3-day course addresses the urgent need to leverage **AI and intelligent oversight systems** to meet that 2026 developmental agenda—bridging traditional performance management and cutting-edge AI technologies.

**Why this course matters (from course doc):**

- **64% of municipal debt is irrecoverable** — AI can predict and prevent future debt accumulation  
- **Average debt collection period of 213 days** — AI-powered systems can optimize revenue collection  
- **Chronic underfunding** (9.1% of revenue vs 46% of functions) — AI enables smarter resource allocation  
- **Lack of big data analytical skills** — This course builds critical AI and analytics capabilities  
- **Obsolete monitoring and evaluation systems** — Learn to implement modern AI-driven M&E frameworks  

The course aligns directly with **SALGA’s six strategic outcomes**: Agile Force of Influence; Customised Innovative Solutions; Capable and Reputable Local Government; Effective Networks and IGR; Inclusive Socio-Economic Return; Developmental Growth for Global Competitiveness. Design reflects the SALGA mandate (representation, capacity-building, cooperative governance), MTSF priorities, and District Development Model (DDM). Quality is informed by SAQA guidelines (see course doc).

**Who must attend (from course doc):** Executive and senior management (Municipal Managers, CFOs, COOs, Section 56/57); Performance Management and M&E specialists (PMU, IDP coordinators, SDBIP managers); Financial management; IT and data; Service delivery departments; Governance and compliance (Risk, MPAC support); Political leadership support; SALGA representatives; CoGTA and provincial/local government officials. **Recommended:** teams of 3–5 (e.g. 1 senior manager, 1 PM/M&E, 1 IT/data, 1 service delivery, 1 finance).
